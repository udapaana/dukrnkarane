## § 666. There is a peculiar class of Kṛt or Primary affixes

§ 666.  There is a peculiar class of Kṛt  or Primary affixes
technically  designated  by Sanskrit grammarians—Unādi or
those beginning with the affix  @deva[उण्]  i. e.  the affix  @deva[उ]  with the
signs or indicatory  letter @deva[न्]; @deva[इष्व] called  from the two roots @deva[सिष्],
@deva[याङु], &c. in the first Sūtra  being derived with the affix.   These
Unādi affixes form primary nouns, like other  Kṛt  affixes  form
verbal roots, but are classed  separately because their applica-
tion is limited, because the nouns  derived by their means are
either formed irregularly,  or the connection between their
senses  and  the  meanings of roots  from which they  are
supposed to be derived is not  so clearly discernible as in the
case of other primary derivatives;  e. g.  @deva[अस्वुवर्  अस्थान स्यातोतिति]
