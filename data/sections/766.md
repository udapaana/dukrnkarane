## § 766. The gerund in @deva[अम्] of the root @deva[कु] is used *

§ 766.  The gerund in @deva[अम्] of the root @deva[कु] is used *

( a ) with the noun  governed by it, if it be compounded  with
it prepositionally when  censure  is  implied;  @deva[चौरुकाते  आकोष्युत्ते]
( @deva[चौरुछुद्युय उत्याचियुथुर्यै]: ) ' cries out ( he is ) a thief, a thief.' In this
case @deva[यु] is added to the noun governed.

( b ) with the words @deva[स्वाद्], @deva[छुण], and  @deva[संस्युद्] ,  @deva[यु]  being  added to
these as before; @deva[आस्वात्ङुर स्वादु छुत्युवा थुकैं स्वाद्ङु]-@deva[छुरूणं]-@deva[संस्यदै]—@deva[कारु थुकैं]
'he eats having sweetened or seasoned his food.'

( c )  with the words @deva[अत्युया],  @deva[एव],  @deva[हुर्युं],  and  @deva[कुर्युं],  provided the
root @deva[य] takes its sense; @deva[अत्युयाकारुं  हुत्तो]  'he  speaks  in  a coherent
manner;' ' @deva[एवं कृत्वा] the acts thus;'  @deva[किंकारुते],  @deva[कुथंकारुते  हुयुभुरामु] ,
but @deva[शिनैत्युयुत्या हुत्वा मुङुकैं].

( d ) with the particles  @deva[युथुं]  and  @deva[युथा]  when an angry  reply
is given; @deva[युथुमुकारुं  शोध्युं  तुथुमकारुं  शोध्युं  किं  तवौनैन]  ( Sid. Kau. );
'I will  eat  in  this  manner,  I will  eat in that manner,  what
have you do with that ?'
