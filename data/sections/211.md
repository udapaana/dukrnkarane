## § 211. Genitive Compounds: Restrictions

(a) It is not compounded with verbal derivatives[^1] ending in @deva[तृ] @[tṛ] and @deva[अक] @[aka] and denoting the agent of the action; @deva[अर्थं दातृ] @[arthaṃ dātṛ] and not @deva[अर्थदातृ] @[arthadātṛ]; @deva[घटस्य कर्ता] @[ghaṭasya kartā], @deva[ओदनस्य पाचकः] @[odanasya pācakaḥ] etc.; but @deva[इष्टकानां भक्षयिता] @[iṣṭakānāṃ bhakṣayitā] as the @deva[अक] @[aka] here does not denote the agent.

**Exception.**—But a compound is allowed in the case of the words @deva[याजक] @[yājaka] one who employs as sacrificing priests, @deva[पूजक] @[pūjaka], @deva[परिचारक] @[paricāraka], @deva[परिवेषक] @[pariveṣaka] a waiter at meals, @deva[स्नापक] @[snāpaka] a servant who bathes his master or brings bathing water for him, @deva[अध्यापक] @[adhyāpaka], @deva[उत्सादक] @[utsādaka] one who destroys, @deva[हेतु] @[hetu], @deva[भर्तृ] @[bhartṛ], when it does not mean 'a holder,' and others not very important:[^2] @deva[ब्राह्मणयाजकः] @[brāhmaṇayājakaḥ], @deva[देवपूजकः] @[devapūjakaḥ]; @deva[राजपरिचारकः] @[rājaparicārakaḥ] etc.; @deva[अग्निहोत्रा] @[agnihotrā], @deva[सोमभर्ता] @[somabhartā], etc. but @deva[वज्रस्य भर्ता] @[vajrasya bhartā] 'the thunder bolt-bearer'.

(b) A specific Genitive (i.e. from which a singling out is made) is not compounded with any other words,[^3] as @deva[वृक्षाणां द्विजः] @[vṛkṣāṇāṃ dvijaḥ] @deva[खगः] @[khagaḥ].

(c) A noun in the Genitive is not compounded with ordinal numbers, words denoting qualities, words implying satisfaction, present participles, past participles meaning 'honoured or approved of' or showing the place of the act, verbal indeclinables and verbal nouns ending in @deva[तव] @[tava];[^4] e.g. @deva[सर्वेषां प्रथमः] @[sarveṣāṃ prathamaḥ] @deva[ब्राह्मणस्य श्रेष्ठः] @[brāhmaṇasya śreṣṭhaḥ] (or @deva[श्रेष्ठतरः] @[śreṣṭhataraḥ]); @deva[फलानां तृप्तः] @[phalānāṃ tṛptaḥ] 'satisfied with fruits' (an Inst. Tat. is, of course, allowed in these cases); @deva[हितस्य कुर्वन्] @[hitasya kurvan] @deva[कुर्वाणो वा] @[kurvāṇo vā] @deva[हितकुर्] @[hitakur]; @deva[सज्जनैः मतः] @[sajjanaiḥ mataḥ] esteemed by the good; @deva[राज्ञा पूजितः] @[rājñā pūjitaḥ] @deva[इदं आसितं] @[idaṃ āsitaṃ] (place of sitting) @deva[गतं] @[gataṃ] @deva[सुप्तं वा] @[suptaṃ vā]; @deva[ब्राह्मणस्य दत्वा] @[brāhmaṇasya datvā], @deva[नरस्य कर्तव्यम्] @[narasya kartavyam] etc.

**N. B.** Compounds like @deva[राजपूजितः] @[rājapūjitaḥ], @deva[राजमतः] @[rājamataḥ], etc. ought to be considered as Instrumental Tatpurushas.

### Exceptions

(1) If the word denoting a quality ends in @deva[तर] @[tara] it does combine with a noun in the Genitive, and the @deva[तर] @[tara] is dropped; @deva[सर्वेषां श्वेततरः] @[sarveṣāṃ śvetataraḥ] @deva[सर्वेश्वेतः] @[sarveśvetaḥ] whiter than all others; similarly @deva[सर्वेषां महान्तरः] @[sarveṣāṃ mahāntaraḥ] @deva[सर्वेमहान्] @[sarvemahān] etc.

(2) The words @deva[द्वितीय] @[dvitīya], @deva[तृतीय] @[tṛtīya], @deva[चतुर्थ] @[caturtha] and @deva[तुर्य] @[turya] are compounded with nouns signifying the whole of which they are parts, and are optionally placed first,[^5] @deva[द्वितीयं भिक्षायाः] @[dvitīyaṃ bhikṣāyāḥ] @deva[द्वितीयभिक्षा] @[dvitīyabhikṣā], @deva[भिक्षाद्वितीयम्] @[bhikṣādvitīyam] half of the quantity of alms; but @deva[द्वितीयं भिक्षायाः] @[dvitīyaṃ bhikṣāyāḥ] (begging alms a second time) @deva[भिक्षुकस्य] @[bhikṣukasya].

**Note**—Compounds like @deva[द्वितीयभिक्षा] @[dvitīyabhikṣā], @deva[पूर्वकायः] @[pūrvakāyaḥ] (see further on) etc. are not strictly Gen. Tat, as the case of the first word determines the name, but mere Tat.; some call these Nom. Tat (@deva[प्रथमातत्पुरुष] @[prathamātatpuruṣa]).

(d) A noun in the Genitive, used as an object in a sentence, when the agent and object of the action denoted by the nouns formed by krit affixes are both used, is not compounded,[^6] as @deva[आश्चर्यं गवां दोहनं अगोपेन] @[āścaryaṃ gavāṃ dohanaṃ agopena] 'the milking of cows by one, not a cow-herd, is a wonder.'
