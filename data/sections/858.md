## § 858. † The Genitive is not used with present participles

§ 858.  † The  Genitive is  not  used  with  present  participles
except  that  of  @deva[द्विष्],  verbal  derivatives  ending  in  @deva[क्]  and  @deva[उकुच्]
except  that  derived  from  @deva[कुयुच्],  verbal  indeclinables,  past  parti-
ciples,  passive  and active,  nouns  formed  with  the  affix  @deva[युयुनु]  (see
p.  456)  and  with  such  as  mean  'in  the  habit  of,  or  having  the
properties  of  or  doing  any  thing  well;'  @deva[क्तुर्नों  क्तुयुर्नं]  or  @deva[कुयूनुयुयुयुयुः]  but
@deva[युरै  दुयुस्युयु  वा  हिद्युद्  हरिः]  Hari,  the  enemy  of  Mura;  @deva[हुरै  विद्युयुयुट्युः]  desir-
ous of seeing  Hari;  @deva[युरै  अट्युदुहुरियुयुट्],  @deva[दैन्युयुरु  यावुदुकुयो  हरिः]  Hari  is  the
killer of demons;  @deva[कुयुच्युः  कुरुणुयुयुङु]  :  @deva[युविदुयुस्युयु  युयुवै  तुयु  युङु  न  पुयुवुयु  द्युयु
दैन्युयु];  @deva[दैन्युयुरु  हुवुनुयुरु]:  @deva[हुयुकुन्तुः  प्रयुच्चो  हुरेणु]  worldly  life  goes  asray
with  Hari;  @deva[आतुयुनुनों  अट्युकुत्तुयुरुष्णुयुट्युः]  in  habit  of  decorating  oneself;
@deva[अवुनं  विद्युयुट्ु]:  a  habitual  beggar;  @deva[क्तुर्नो  कुट्युं]  one  who  prepares  a  mat;
@deva[युनै  पुनुयुनुयु]  'killing  the  fruit'  means  the  fruit  by  terminations
