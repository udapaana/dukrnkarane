## § 232. Those Tatpurusha compounds whose first member is a preposition are termed Prādi by Sanskrit grammari

§ 232. Those Tatpurusha compounds whose first member is a preposition are termed Prādi by Sanskrit grammarians*; some of these prepositions combine with a following noun in the Nom., with a noun in the Acc. &c.; e. g. @deva[प्रशतः आचार्यं प्राचार्यं] 'an eminent teacher', @deva[संगतः अध्यानं समध्ध]: 'taken to a path-way' (vide Bhatti. III. 45). @deva[प्रछद्दो वातः प्रवातः] 'strong or excessive wind,' &c.; @deva[अतिक्रान्तो मालां अतिमालः] 'excelling a garland' (in fragrance or beauty), @deva[अतिक्रान्तौ रश्मिं वा अतिरश्मः] 'a chariot-warrior who has no rival or equal'; so @deva[अतिमात्र] 'exceeding measure,' @deva[अतिसर्वं] &c. @deva[अवछुटः कोकिलया अवकोकिलः] 'attracted by the cuckoo;' @deva[परिकलानः अध्ययनान् परंध्ययनः] 'tired of studying;' @deva[निष्क्रान्तः कौशाम्ब्याः निष्कौशाम्बिः] 'gone out of Kauśāmbi;' so @deva[निर्लङ्ज]: &c. A compound with prepositions which govern cases (@deva[कर्मप्रवचनीय]) is forbidden; @deva[द्रुभें प्राति].

5 Gati.
