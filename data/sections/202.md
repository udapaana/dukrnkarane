## § 202. Shortening of Feminine Endings in Compounds

The final vowel of a feminine noun ending in any of the affixes @deva[आ] @[ā], @deva[ई] @[ī] or @deva[ऊ] @[ū] and forming the second member of a compound used adjectively is shortened; the @deva[आ] @[ā] of @deva[गो] @[go] is changed to @deva[उ] @[u] under the same circumstances.[^1]

e.g. @deva[प्राप्त] @[prāpta] + @deva[जीविका] @[jīvikā] = @deva[प्राप्तजीविकः] @[prāptajīvikaḥ] (Tat.); @deva[अतिमालः] @[atimālaḥ] (Tat.); @deva[पञ्चगुः] @[pañcaguḥ] something bought for five cows; @deva[वृद्धगौ] @[vṛddhagau] @deva[नाञ्चौ] @[nāñcau] @deva[प्रचिकित्सः] @[pracikitsaḥ] @deva[बहुनाडीः] @[bahunāḍīḥ] many-veined @deva[देहः] @[dehaḥ]; @deva[चित्रा गावौ यस्य] @[citrā gāvau yasya] @deva[चित्रगुः] @[citraguḥ] having variegated cows, etc.; but @deva[कल्याणपञ्चमीकः] @[kalyāṇapañcamīkaḥ] as the @deva[ई] @[ī] is not final.

(a) If the final @deva[ई] @[ī] or @deva[ऊ] @[ū] be not a feminine termination, it remains unchanged; @deva[बहु] @[bahu] @deva[धीः] @[dhīḥ] @deva[सा] @[sā] @deva[बहुधीः] @[bahudhīḥ], @deva[बहुतन्त्रीर्वीणी] @[bahutantrīrvīṇī].
