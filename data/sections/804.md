## § 804. Particles governing the Accusative

The particles[^1] @deva[उभयतः] @[ubhayataḥ], @deva[सर्वतः] @[sarvataḥ], @deva[उभाद्धि] @[ubhāddhi], @deva[अन्तरेण] @[antareṇa], @deva[अभितः] @[abhitaḥ] and @deva[निकषा] @[nikaṣā]; @deva[अन्तरा] @[antarā], @deva[परितः] @[paritaḥ], @deva[समया] @[samayā], @deva[निकषाम्] @[nikaṣām], @deva[द्रुम] @[druma] and @deva[प्रति] @[prati] meaning 'to', and @deva[अन्तर] @[antara] 'between,' and @deva[अनृतेण] @[anṛteṇa] 'without,' 'regarding to,' govern the Accusative.

e.g. @deva[उभयतः कृष्णम् गोपाः] @[ubhayataḥ kṛṣṇam gopāḥ]: the cowherds are on both sides of Kṛṣṇa.

@deva[निकटम् प्रासादम् चारिणो दर्शनम्] @[nikaṭam prāsādam cāriṇo darśanam]: guards keep vigil on all sides of the palace.

@deva[उभयस्य लोकम् हरिः] @[ubhayasya lokam hariḥ]: Hari is over all the worlds.

@deva[अमुदस्य लोकम् गतः] @[amudasya lokam gataḥ]: went below the world, except itself.

@deva[धिक् कुरुराजम्] @[dhik kururājam]: fie upon the lord of the Kurus, with all his brothers.

(@deva[धिक्] @[dhik]) is used sometimes with the Nom. and sometimes with the Voc. @deva[धिक् धनम् दुःखभम्] @[dhik dhanam duḥkhabham]: fie upon wealth which is attended with troubles.

@deva[किम् भूते तत् दिव्यानि यत्रे भर्तु पत्युरतोऽपि शरीरम् न वर्तते भीतिनाः प्रधानस्य] @[kim bhūte tat divyāni yatre bharta patyurato'pi śarīram na vartate bhītināḥ pradhānasya] (Bhaṭṭi I. 12): he dispelled the demons from around the altar and offered sacrifices to the minor deities arranged round the principal one.

@deva[अभितरम् प्रष्णम् अद्यम् परितः त्वस्ये] @[abhitaram praṣṇam adyam paritaḥ tvasye] Kir. XI. 8

@deva[ग्रामम् समया-निकषाम् चारिणि] @[grāmam samayā-nikaṣām cāriṇi]: goes near to the village. Vide Ragh. VI, 71.

@deva[अतः अभाय-द्रे] @[ataḥ abhāya-dre]: no one except Hari is not a devotee of Kṛṣṇa.

@deva[मन्युरोऽस्य कारोऽपि नगुरम् प्रति] @[manyuro'sya kāro'pi naguram prati]: I have but a faint desire to go to the town.

@deva[अन्तर त्वस्य भो हरिम् हरिन्युरतेण न सुग्म्] @[antar tvasya bho harim harinyurateṇa na sugma]: happiness is not possible without Hari.

@deva[देवस्य वसुमतोऽनृतेण] @[devasya vasumato'nṛteṇa]: with reference to queen Vasumatī.

Some of the indeclinables given above are used with the Genitive; e.g. @deva[उपेत्युरि त्विदिवनुयम् अदि वच् सेत्यस्युम्] @[upetyuri tvinadivanuyam adi vac setyasyam] (he stood) very high above all by his learning, like the sun, &c.
