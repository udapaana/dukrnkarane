## § 489. The Reduplicative Perfect is formed of all monosyllabic roots beginning with consonants, a

§ 489.  The Reduplicative  Perfect  is  formed  of  all  monosyllabic roots  beginning  with  consonants,  as  well as of those beginning with the vowels @deva[अ] or @deva[आ], and @deva[इ], @deva[उ]; and @deva[अ]  short.
    Exceptions—@deva[ऊय], @deva[अस्], @deva[कास्], @deva[आस्]. These take the Periphrastic Perfect necessary.
