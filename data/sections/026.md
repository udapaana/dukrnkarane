## § 26. Exceptions - Pragṛhya Cases (No Sandhi)

No sandhi is possible in the case of:

### (a) Dual Forms

What are called the Pragṛhya exceptions; viz.— When a dual form (whether of a noun, pronoun or verb) ends in @deva[ई, ऊ] @[ī, ū], or @deva[ए] @[e]; e.g. @deva[हरी एतौ] @[harī etau]; @deva[विष्णू इमौ] @[viṣṇū imau]; @deva[गुरवे अयं] @[gurave ayaṃ]; @deva[पश्यैते इमौ] @[paśyaite imau].

### (b) After अदस् Pronoun

When @deva[ई] @[ī] or @deva[ऊ] @[ū] follows the @deva[इ] @[i] of the pronoun @deva[अदस्] @[adas][^1]; e.g., @deva[अमी ईशा:] @[amī īśāḥ] these lords; @deva[अमू आसाते] @[amū āsāte] the two sit down.


### (c) Single Vowel Particles

Particles consisting of a single vowel, except @deva[आ] @[ā] meaning 'a little, or a limit' exclusive or inclusive or used as a preposition, when followed by a vowel, e.g. @deva[इ इन्द्र] @[i indra] Oh Indra. @deva[उ उद्येष, आ पूर्वं न मन्यसे] @[u udyeṣa, ā pūrvaṃ na manyase] ah, you think so; but @deva[आ + उष्णम् = ओष्णम्] @[ā + uṣṇam = oṣṇam] (a little warm) &c.

### (d) Indeclinables Ending in ओ

When an indeclinable ends in @deva[ओ] @[o]; e.g. @deva[अहो ईशा:] @[aho īśāḥ]; the final @deva[ओ] @[o] of a noun in the vocative case optionally combines when followed by @deva[इति] @[iti]; e.g. @deva[विष्णो इति = विष्णविति] @[viṣṇo iti = viṣṇaviti], and @deva[विष्णो इति:] @[viṣṇo itiḥ] also @deva[विष्ण इति] @[viṣṇa iti] (see § 24 a.).


### (e) Pluta Vowels

Pluta vowels; e.g. @deva[पश्य छागा ३ अत्र गोष्ठगताः] @[paśya chāgā 3 atra goṣṭhagatāḥ] O Kṛṣṇa; here is the cow grazing.
