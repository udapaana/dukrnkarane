§ 7. In the case of metres regulated by syllabic instants (@deva[मात्रा] @[mātrā]) or @deva[जाति] @[jāti], each line is divided into groups of four @deva[मात्रा] @[mātrā] called @deva[मात्रा-गण] @[mātrā-gaṇa]. They are 8 in number viz. @deva[म] @[ma], @deva[र] @[ra], @deva[न] @[na], @deva[स] @[sa], @deva[भ] @[bha], @deva[य] @[ya], @deva[ज] @[ja], @deva[त] @[ta].

The symbol ∪ stands for a light syllable equivalent to one @deva[मात्रा] @[mātrā]; and ऽ stands for a long or heavy syllable equivalent to two @deva[मात्रा] @[mātrā]. Symbolically they are represented as under:

- @deva[म] @[ma]: ऽ ऽ
- @deva[र] @[ra]: ऽ ∪ ∪
- @deva[न] @[na]: ∪ ∪ ऽ
- @deva[स] @[sa]: ∪ ∪ ∪ ∪
- @deva[भ] @[bha]: ऽ ∪
- @deva[य] @[ya]: ∪ ऽ
- @deva[ज] @[ja]: ∪ ∪ ∪
- @deva[त] @[ta]: ∪
