## [^5] 22. Semivowel Substitution (Yaṇ)

When @deva[इ, उ, ऋ] @[i, u, ṛ] and @deva[लृ] @[lṛ], short or long, are followed by a dissimilar vowel, @deva[य्, व्, र्] @[y, v, r] and @deva[ल्] @[l] are respectively substituted for them[^1]; e.g. @deva[इति + आह = इत्याह] @[iti + āha = ityāha]; @deva[बुधी + उपास्य: = बुध्युपास्यः] @[budhī + upāsyaḥ = budhyupāsyaḥ] adored by the wise; @deva[मधु + अरि: = मध्वरि:] @[madhu + ariḥ = madhvariḥ] the enemy of @deva[मधु] @[madhu] i.e. Vishṇu, @deva[पातृ + अंश: = पात्रंश:] @[pātṛ + aṃśaḥ = pātraṃśaḥ] a portion of the creator; @deva[लृ + आकृतिः = लाकृतिः] @[lṛ + ākṛtiḥ = lākṛtiḥ] a figure like that of @deva[लृ] @[lṛ] &c.



### (a) Optional Doubling Before Consonant

A consonant except @deva[ङ्] @[ṅ], preceded by a vowel, is optionally doubled, if no vowel follows[^1]; @deva[सुप्र + उपास्य: = सुप्पुपास्य:] @[supra + upāsyaḥ = suppupāsyaḥ] and @deva[सुप्रम् + उपास्य:] @[supram + upāsyaḥ].

### (b) Soft Unaspirate Substitution

When a consonant, except a nasal or a semivowel, is followed by a soft unaspirate or aspirate (3rd or 4th letter of a class) it is changed to the soft unaspirate (3rd letter) of its class[^3]; @deva[सुप्रम् + उपास्य: = सुद्दुपास्य:] @[supram + upāsyaḥ = suddupāsyaḥ].

### (c) Optional Doubling of Semivowels

Semivowels, when preceded by a letter of the first five classes except @deva[म्] @[m] are optionally doubled[^4]; thus @deva[सुःम् + उपास्य: = सुःम् + उपास्य:] @[suḥm + upāsyaḥ = suḥm + upāsyaḥ] and @deva[सुःम्म् + उपास्य:] @[suḥmm + upāsyaḥ]; @deva[सुद्म् + उपास्य: = सुद्म् + उपास्य:] @[sudm + upāsyaḥ = sudm + upāsyaḥ] and @deva[सुद्म्म् + उपास्य:] @[sudmm + upāsyaḥ]. Thus there are four forms ultimately, viz. @deva[सुःमुपास्य:, सुद्दुपास्य:] @[suḥmupāsya:, suddupāsyaḥ] and @deva[सुद्दुरुपास्य:] @[suddrupāsyaḥ].

Similarly @deva[मधु + अरि: = मध्वरि:, मद्ध्वरि:, मद्धुवरि:] @[madhu + ariḥ = madhvari:, maddhvari:, madhuvariḥ], and @deva[मद्दुव्वरि:] @[madduvvariḥ]; @deva[पाद् + अङ्क: = पादङ्क:] @[pād + aṅkaḥ = pādaṅkaḥ] and @deva[पाद्दङ्क:] @[pāddaṅkaḥ]. In @deva[ल् + आकृति:] @[l + ākṛtiḥ] of course there is no possibility of any other form.

### (d) Doubling After र्/र्

A consonant except @deva[ङ्] @[ṅ], coming after @deva[र्] @[r] or @deva[र्] @[r] preceded by a vowel is optionally doubled[^5]; e.g. @deva[हरि + अनुभव: = हर्य् + अनुभव: = हर्य्यनुभव:] @[hari + anubhavaḥ = hary + anubhavaḥ = haryyānubhavaḥ] by the general rule; and optionally @deva[हर्य्म् + अनुभव: = हर्य्यनुभव:] @[harym + anubhavaḥ = haryyanubhavaḥ] (Hari's experience) by this rule; so @deva[न हि + अस्ति = न ह्यस्ति] @[na hi + asti = na hyasti] and @deva[न हय्यस्ति] @[na hayyasti].
