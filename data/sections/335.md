## § 335. Dvigu Ending in Kāṇḍa

A Dvigu ending in @deva[काण्ड] @[kāṇḍa] (a particular measure) and qualifying a word denoting 'a field' has its feminine formed by the addition of @deva[आ] @[ā], but when a Taddhita affix has been first added to it and then dropped.

e.g. @deva[द्वे काण्डे प्रमाणं यस्याः असौ द्विकाण्ड] @[dve kāṇḍe pramāṇaṃ yasyāḥ asau dvikāṇḍa] + @deva[मात्रा] @[mātrā] = @deva[द्विकाण्डा क्षेत्रभक्तिः] @[dvikāṇḍā kṣetrabhaktiḥ]: a piece of land 30 hands in extent

But @deva[द्विकाण्डी रज्जुः] @[dvikāṇḍī rajjuḥ]: a rope, 30 hands in length

Also when the ending word is one that does not denote a measure, except the words @deva[बिस्त] @[bista] 'a tola', @deva[आचित] @[ācita] 'the load of a cart', and @deva[कम्बल्या] @[kambalyā] (a weight equal to 33 tolas).

e.g. @deva[पञ्चभिः अश्वैः क्रीता पञ्चाश्वा] @[pañcabhiḥ aśvaiḥ krītā pañcāśvā]

e.g. @deva[द्वौ बिस्तौ प्रमाणमस्या इति द्विबिस्ता स्थाली] @[dvau bistau pramāṇamasyā iti dvibistā sthālī]

e.g. @deva[द्व्याचिता] @[dvyācitā], @deva[द्विकम्बल्या] @[dvikambalyā]

**Obs.** When the word @deva[पुरुष] @[puruṣa] expressive of measurement ends a Dvigu and the Taddhita affix is added and dropped as before, its feminine is formed both in @deva[आ] @[ā] and @deva[ई] @[ī].

e.g. @deva[द्वौ पुरुषौ प्रमाणं अस्याः द्विपुरुषा] @[dvau puruṣau pramāṇaṃ asyāḥ dvipuruṣā] or @deva[द्विपुरुषी परिखा] @[dvipuruṣī parikhā]: a ditch two puruṣas (13 feet) in depth
