## § 376. The following are the principal conjunctions in

§ 376.    The following are the principal conjunctions in
Sanskrit:—

( a ) Copulative @deva[अथ], @deva[अथो], @deva[उत], @deva[च], @deva[किन्च], &c.

( b ) Disjunctive @deva[वा], @deva[वा]...@deva[वा], &c.

( c ) Adversative @deva[अथवा], @deva[तु], @deva[किन्तु], @deva[किन्वा], &c.

( d ) Conditional @deva[चेत्], @deva[यदि], @deva[यदापि], @deva[नेत्], @deva[नोचेत्], @deva[वेद्] ( used in
        sacrificial ceremonies ), &c.

( e ) Causal @deva[हि], @deva[तत्], @deva[तेन], &c.

( f ) Interrogative  @deva[आहो अहोस्विद्],  @deva[उत],  @deva[उताहो],  @deva[कि],  @deva[किच्चु],
        @deva[किधुत], @deva[किन्चित्], @deva[नञु], @deva[नघा], @deva[नु], &c.

( g ) Affirmatives and negatives @deva[अहु], @deva[अथ किम्] , @deva[आम्], @deva[अत्ता] &c.

( h ) Conjunctions of time @deva[यावत्]—@deva[तावत्], @deva[यदा], @deva[तदा] &c.

( i ) @deva[अथ] and @deva[इति] are used to mark the beginning and the close
        of a work respectively.
