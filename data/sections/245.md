## § 245. ] TATPURUSHA. 149

§ 245 ]                    TATPURUSHA.                    149

GENDERS OF TATPURUSHA COMPOUNDS.

§ 245. *As a general rule a Tatpurusha compound follows the gender of the final noun.

Exceptions † —

(a) Compounds with @deva[प्राण] and @deva[आपद] for their first member and the Prādi compounds follow the gender of the words they qualify; @deva[प्राञ्जीविकः नरः], @deva[प्राञ्जीविका स्त्री], @deva[निष्कौशाम्बिः पुरुषः] &c.

(b) A Tatpurusha ending in @deva[राज्ञ], @deva[अह्न] and @deva[शह] is masculine, except when @deva[राज्ञ] is preceded by a numeral and @deva[शह] by @deva[षुण्य] and @deva[सुदिन]; e. g. @deva[पूर्वैराज्ञः], @deva[मध्याह्नः], @deva[सराहः], @deva[नवराज्ञम्], @deva[गणरात्रम्], @deva[षुण्याहम्], @deva[सुदिनाहम्]; also that ending in @deva[पथ] (substituted for @deva[पथिन्], vide § 280) preceded by a numeral or an indeclinable; as @deva[त्रिपाणां पन्थाः त्रिपथम्], @deva[विरुपः पन्थाः विपथम्], a bad or a wrong way; but @deva[सुपन्थाः], @deva[अतिपन्थाः]
