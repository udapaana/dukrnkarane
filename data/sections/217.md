## § 217. Additional Aluk of the Locative

(a) Aluk is necessary in the case of the words—(1) @deva[गौ] @[gau] and @deva[युधि] @[yudhi] followed by @deva[विधृ] @[vidhṛ], as @deva[गावि विधृ] @[gāvi vidhṛ], stationary in the sky, @deva[युवि विधृ] @[yuvi vidhṛ] firm in battle; (2) @deva[हृद्] @[hṛd] and @deva[दिव्] @[div] followed by @deva[स्पृश्] @[spṛś]; @deva[हृदिस्पृक्] @[hṛdispṛk], @deva[दिविस्पृक्] @[divispṛk] (@deva[हृदयं दिवं च स्पृशतीति] @[hṛdayaṃ divaṃ ca spṛśatīti]); (3) @deva[अन्तर्] @[antar] and @deva[मध्य] @[madhya] followed by @deva[गुह्] @[guh]; @deva[मध्येगुहः] @[madhyeguhaḥ]; @deva[अन्तर्गुहः] @[antarguhaḥ] and (4) words expressive of parts of the body except @deva[मूर्धन्] @[mūrdhan] and @deva[मस्तक] @[mastaka] and not followed by @deva[केश] @[keśa]; @deva[कर्णे काकः] @[karṇe kākaḥ], @deva[उरसि लोम] @[urasi loma] (having the hair on the breast: Bah.), but @deva[मूर्धे-मस्तके-शिखा] @[mūrdhe-mastake-śikhā]; @deva[छुले कामो अस्य] @[chule kāmo asya] @deva[छुलकामः] @[chulakāmaḥ].

(b) When a noun in the Loc. is compounded with a verbal derivative and the whole is a name, aluk takes place in most cases;[^1] e.g. @deva[स्तम्बे रमः] @[stambe ramaḥ] (@deva[स्तम्ब] @[stamba] a kind of grass @deva[रमते अत्र] @[ramate atra]) an elephant; @deva[कर्णे जपः] @[karṇe japaḥ] @deva[कर्णेजपः] @[karṇejapaḥ] one who whispers into the ear, a spy; @deva[दिवि चरः] @[divi caraḥ] @deva[दिवाचरः] @[divācaraḥ] a heavenly being; @deva[पङ्के रुहम्] @[paṅke ruham] @deva[पङ्केरुहम्] @[paṅkeruham] a lotus; @deva[कुशे शयम्] @[kuśe śayam] @deva[कुशेशयम्] @[kuśeśayam], @deva[जले शयः] @[jale śayaḥ] @deva[जलेशयः] @[jaleśayaḥ] a fish; but @deva[दिवाचरा] @[divācarā]; @deva[सरोजम्] @[sarojam] or @deva[सरोरुहम्] @[saroruham] etc.

(c) There is optional aluk of the Loc. of a word expressive of time when compounded with @deva[काल] @[kāla] and words ending in the affixes @deva[तर] @[tara], @deva[तम] @[tama] and @deva[तन] @[tana]; @deva[पूर्वाह्णे काले] @[pūrvāhṇe kāle] @deva[पूर्वाह्णकाले] @[pūrvāhṇakāle]; @deva[पूर्वाह्णे उत्तरे] @[pūrvāhṇe uttare] etc. @deva[पूर्वाह्णे उत्तने] @[pūrvāhṇe uttane].

(d) When the compound is not a name, aluk is necessary when @deva[ज] @[ja] comes after @deva[प्रादु] @[prādu], @deva[शरद्] @[śarad], @deva[काल] @[kāla] and @deva[दिवि] @[divi]; and optional when it comes after @deva[वर्ष] @[varṣa], @deva[क्षर] @[kṣara], @deva[घर] @[ghara] and @deva[वर] @[vara];[^2] as @deva[प्रादुषि-शरदि-काले-दिवि-जः] @[prāduṣi-śaradi-kāle-divi-jaḥ]; @deva[वर्षे जः] @[varṣe jaḥ] or @deva[वर्षजः] @[varṣajaḥ] produced in the rainy season, etc.

**Exception:**—No aluk takes place in the case of verbal derivatives ending in @deva[इन्] @[in] and the words @deva[सिद्ध] @[siddha], @deva[बद्ध] @[baddha], and @deva[स्थ] @[stha] in the ordinary language;[^3] as @deva[स्थाण्डिले शायी] @[sthāṇḍile śāyī] 'an ascetic,' @deva[शालूरस्थितः] @[śālūrasthitaḥ], @deva[चक्रे बद्धः] @[cakre baddhaḥ] @deva[चक्रबद्धः] @[cakrabaddhaḥ]; @deva[समे स्थः] @[same sthaḥ] @deva[समस्थः] @[samasthaḥ]; but @deva[छणे अस्य अवरेशः] @[chaṇe asya avareśaḥ] etc.

(e) When a noun in the Loc. case ending in @deva[अ] @[a] or a consonant and not denoting time is compounded with @deva[आश्रय] @[āśraya], @deva[वास] @[vāsa] and @deva[वास्तिन्] @[vāstin] and with @deva[वन] @[vana] aluk is optional;[^4] @deva[वृक्षे आश्रयः] @[vṛkṣe āśrayaḥ], @deva[ग्रामे वासः] @[grāme vāsaḥ] @deva[ग्रामेवासः] @[grāmevāsaḥ], @deva[ग्रामे वास्ती] @[grāme vāstī] @deva[ग्रामेवास्ती] @[grāmevāstī], @deva[वृक्षे अस्य वनम्] @[vṛkṣe asya vanam] @deva[वृक्षेवनम्] @[vṛkṣevanam], but @deva[भूमिश्रयः] @[bhūmiśrayaḥ], @deva[युद्धिवन्यः] @[yuddhivanyaḥ] etc.
