## § 20. Guṇa Substitution

When @deva[अ] @[a] or @deva[आ] @[ā] is followed by @deva[इ, उ, ऋ, टृ] @[i, u, ṛ, ṭṛ], short or long, the guṇa letter corresponding to the latter takes the place of both[^4] e.g. @deva[उप + इन्द्र = उपेन्द्र:] @[upa + indra = upendraḥ] Vishnu; @deva[परम + ईश्वर: = परमेश्वर:] @[parama + īśvaraḥ = parameśvaraḥ] the great lord; @deva[रमा + इच्छा = रमेच्छा] @[ramā + icchā = ramecchā] the wish of Ramā; @deva[यथा + इच्छित = यथेच्छितम्] @[yathā + icchita = yathecchitam] according to wish or desire; @deva[हित + उपदेश: = हितोपदेश:] @[hita + upadeśaḥ = hitopadeśaḥ] a friendly instruction; @deva[टत्प + ऊरु = टत्पोरु:] @[ṭatpa + ūru = ṭatporuḥ] Kṛshṇa's thigh; @deva[गङ्गा + उदक = गङ्गोदक] @[gaṅgā + udaka = gaṅgodaka], @deva[मङ्ग + ऊरु = मङ्गोरु] @[maṅga + ūru = maṅgoru]; @deva[कृष्ण + ऋद्धि: = कृष्णर्द्धि:] @[kṛṣṇa + ṛddhiḥ = kṛṣṇarddhiḥ] Kṛshṇa's prosperity; @deva[महा + ऋषि: = महर्षि:] @[mahā + ṛṣiḥ = maharṣiḥ] a great sage; @deva[तव + टृकार: = तवट्कार:] @[tava + ṭṛkāraḥ = tavaṭkāraḥ] The ṭkāra pronounced by you.

### (a) Optional Dropping of First Consonant

If a consonant be followed by homogeneous consonants, except the nasals, the semivowels, and @deva[र] @[r], the first of them is optionally dropped;[^1] @deva[कुम्भ + कुदि: = कुम्भादि:] @[kumbha + kudiḥ = kumbhādiḥ] by the general rule; then @deva[कुम्भर् + उ + य + इ = कुम्भादि:] @[kumbhar + u + ya + i = kumbhādiḥ] by this rule, the @deva[उ] @[u] being dropped; and also a third form @deva[कुम्भऋदि:] @[kumbhṛdiḥ] (see § 22, subsection d, below).

### (b) Optional Doubling

A consonant of the first five classes except @deva[य] @[y] coming after a semivowel is optionally doubled; so @deva[तत्वकार:] @[tatvakāraḥ] also becomes @deva[तत्व्वकार:, तत्त्त्वकार:] @[tatvvakāra:, tattvakāraḥ] and @deva[तत्व्व्वकार:] @[tatvvvakāraḥ], 4 forms altogether.
