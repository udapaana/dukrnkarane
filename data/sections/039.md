## § 39. Optional ट् Insertion and Consonant Doubling

If @deva[ष्] @[ṣ] or @deva[स्] @[s] at the end of a word be followed by @deva[त्] @[t], @deva[ट्] @[ṭ] (changed to @deva[ढ] @[ḍ]) is optionally inserted between them;[^1] e.g. @deva[षष् + सन्तः = षट्सन्तः] @[ṣaṣ + santaḥ = ṣaṭsantaḥ] or @deva[षट् + ट् + सन्तः = षट्टसन्त] @[ṣaṭ + ṭ + santaḥ = ṣaṭṭsanta] six good men; so @deva[सत् + सः = सत्सः] @[sat + saḥ = satsaḥ] or @deva[सन्त्सः] @[santsaḥ]: that good man.

### (a) Doubling of Final Consonants

@deva[ष्] @[ṣ], @deva[स्] @[s] and @deva[र्] @[r] at the end of a word and preceded by a short vowel double themselves when followed by a vowel;[^2] e.g. @deva[प्रत्यक् + आत्मा = प्रत्यग्गात्मा] @[pratyak + ātmā = pratyaggātmā] the individual soul; so @deva[सुपरिपणीः] @[suparipṇīḥ]: the best reckoner; @deva[सद्अच्युतः] @[sadacyutaḥ]: good Achyuta.
