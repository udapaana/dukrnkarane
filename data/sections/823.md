## § 823. * The roots @deva[कुयुयु], @deva[द्युट्], @deva[ईर्युच्], and @deva[असूयु], and others hav-

§ 823.  * The  roots  @deva[कुयुयु],  @deva[द्युट्],  @deva[ईर्युच्],  and  @deva[असूयु],  and  others  hav-
ing  the  same  sense govern  the  Dative  of  the person against whom
the  feeling  of  anger,  hatred,  &c,  is  directed;  @deva[द्रुयुरे  कुयुयुस्युति  द्युड्युटिङु]-
@deva[हुयुयुतिश्युयुदुरुतु  वा]  (Sid  Kan  )  'he is angry  with  Hari,  or  bears
hatred  towards  him,  or  is  jealous  of  him  or  finds  fault  with
him;'  @deva[रातोयुयु  मोङुक्तुयुच्युस्युयुयुयुरुद्युत]  (Bhatt.  VIII  75.  )  'he  did
neither  get  angry  with  Ṡibā  nor  find  fault  with  her;'  but  @deva[भार्यु]-
@deva[मीर्चुति]  '  he keeps  a  jealous  watch over his  wife  '  (that  others
may  not  see  her  @deva[प्रेमुयुर्ती]:@deva[असूयुर्त्योऽधेति] Sid.  Kau  )

(a) † But @deva[कुयुयु] and  @deva[द्युट्]  preceded  by  a  preposition  govern  the
Accusative case; @deva[किं मां सकुयुयुयुसि]  '  why  do  you  get  angry  with
me?'  @deva[निरुयुयुयुस्युच्छुटुरीसुर्नुवितुरुयुङु  वनते]  (Mud. I.)  'he  always  endea-
vours  to  do  harm to my  body.'

Obs:—@deva[द्युट्]  with  @deva[अवि]  is  also  found  used  with  the  Dat.;  @deva[मया
स्युयुम्युदुवुट्युस्योयुयुवुरुयुर्नों] (Rag.  XII.  52.)
