## § 808. Causals of certain verbs govern Accusative of primitive subject

In the case of[^1] the causals of roots implying 'motion,' 'knowledge,' 'eating,' roots having some literary work for their objects, intransitive roots and the roots @deva[हन्] @[han] and @deva[जल्प] @[jalpa], @deva[भूय] @[bhūya] with @deva[आङ्] @[āṅ]; @deva[ह्वृ] @[hvṛ] with @deva[हि] @[hi], @deva[यङ्] @[yaṅ] and @deva[ल] @[la], their subject in the primitive sense is put in the Accusative case.

@deva[शुचित्युगुयूर्युनस् वैदुशम् स्वुक्तुनेदुयत् ।
आवासयुच्युवुम् देवान्युच्च्युदुच्युस्युयुदुहिचुचु ।
आयुयुयुत्युकुयुङुम् पृथ्युश्यस् यः ते स भोद्युहरेतोरुतुः ॥] @[śucityuguyūryunas vaiduśam svuktuneduyat |
āvāsayucyuvum devānyuccyuducyusyuyuduḥicucu |
āyuyuyutyukuyuṅum pṛthyuśyas yaḥ te sa bhodyuharetoratuḥ ||] Sid. Kau.

That venerable Hari is my refuge who despatched the enemies (of gods) to the next world, explained the meaning of the Vedas to his (followers), made the gods drink nectar, taught Vedas to the Creator and seated the earth (made it rest) on waters.

e.g. @deva[दैत्युनि हरिम् मन्कात्] @[daityuni harim mankāt]: he makes the devotees see Hari.

@deva[जलयति सुय्युपरे विद्युद्युयुदुत यत्युवुदुयुने यत्युङु मैत्युकुगुरुमुयोवुः] @[jalayati suyyupare vidyuduyududat yatyuvuduyune yatyuṅu maityukugurumuoyovuḥ] (R. Kum. I. 82).

@deva[अध्युयुयुन्युरि यत्युयुन्कुयुष्युयुम्] @[adhyuyuyunyuri yatyuyunkuyuṣyuyum]. But when the double causal is used the subject ceases to be the subject of the root in the primitive sense and therefore takes the Instrumental; @deva[रामयति देवदत्तः यत्युत्द्युत्तुम्] @[rāmayati devadattaḥ yatyatdyuattum], @deva[गत्युयुति देवुदुत्तुनम् यत्युद्युवुनम् विष्णुयुदोदुवुतुः] @[gatyuyuti devuduttunam yatyudyuvanam viṣṇuyudoduvatuḥ].

**Obs.**—@deva[याच्] @[yāc] is sometimes found used with the Dative; @deva[यत्युयुविम्ङुयोगुरुलम् स द्युङुकुङुतिर्युयु] @[yatyuyavimṅuyogurul

am sa dyuṅukaṅutiryuyu] Ragh. XII. 54.

(a) The causals of @deva[नी] @[nī], and @deva[वह्] @[vah] when it has for its subject a noun other than one denoting a driver, govern the Instrumental of the primitive subject;[^2] @deva[नाययति वाहयति वा भारम् यरथेनम्] @[nāyayati vāhayati vā bhāram yarathẹnam]: he makes his servant carry a load; @deva[युरञ्चयति रथम् वाहानम् यत्तुः] @[yurañcayati ratham vāhānam yattuḥ]: the charioteer makes the horses draw the chariot.

(1) The same holds good in the case of the causal of @deva[अट्] @[aṭ] and @deva[साहुङ्] @[sāhuṅ];[^3] @deva[आदयति युवुदुवुनम् ना अन्नम् वुयुनुम्] @[ādayati yuvuduvanam nā annam vuyunum]: he makes the boy eat his food.

(2) @deva[यट्] @[yaṭ], when it has not the sense of @deva[हिंसा] @[hiṃsā] 'pain or injury to a sentient thing' does the same;[^4] @deva[भुक्तुयुच्युच्युन् वुयुनुम्] @[bhuktuyucyucyun vuyunum]; but @deva[भुक्तुयुति वुलोरुवुदुदुरे सस्युयु] @[bhuktuyuti vuloruvaududare sasyuyu] (when the loss of corn causes pain to the mind).

(b) @deva[स्तु] @[stu] and @deva[ब्रू] @[brū] which denote a kind of knowledge are construed with the Instrumental in the Atm. @deva[भोयुनम् याद्युयुङोरुद्युवुचु] @[bhoyunam yādyuyuṅorudyuvacu] when meaning 'to remember with pain' is sometimes found used with the Accusative also; @deva[स्मरयति भीयुयुयुति वा देवुदुत्तुनम्] @[smarayati bhīyuyuyuti vā devuduttanam]; @deva[अरिघु चैदुयुयुयुनोयु अतिकुञ्युत्युयुषिश्युयुष्युयुकुनैरे स्मार्युयुन्ते प्रकुयुतीः] @[arighu caiduyuyuyunoyu atikuñyutyuyuṣiśyuyuṣyuyukunaire smāryuyunte prakuyutīḥ]. Vide also Siṣ. VI. 66.

(c) The causal of the denominative @deva[घोष्यु] @[ghoṣyu] also is construed with the Instrumental;[^5] @deva[घोष्यवुयुति देवुदुत्तुनम्] @[ghoṣyavuyuti devuduttanam]: he causes Devadatta do make a sound.

**Note:**—By intransitive roots are meant[^6] such roots as are not capable of governing an object other than one denoting space, time, &c. and not such as, though transitive, may be used without their object being actually expressed; @deva[भारम् आरुयुति देवुदुत्तुम्] @[bhāram āruyuti devuduttam]; but @deva[देवुदुत्तुम् गच्युयुति] @[devuduttam gacyuyuti] and @deva[युदुत्युति] @[yudatyuti] though @deva[वुदुतुम्] @[vudutum] and @deva[देवुदुत्तुम्-एवम्] @[devuduttam-evam] is not actually expressed.
