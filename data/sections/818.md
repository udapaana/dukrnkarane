## § 818. The following words also gvern the Instrumental

§  818.  The following  words  also  gvern  the Instrumental
e  :

(a)  Words  expressing  the  idea of  use  or  need,  such  as  @deva[किंच्],
@deva[कुयुर्युं], @deva[अर्युं]', @deva[यथोजुनं] and the like,  and  the  root  @deva[कृ]  with  @deva[किंच्] used in
the  same  sense;  @deva[यनेनं  किं  युः]  &c  what is the use of his wealth who
&c;  @deva[दुषुणुच्  कुयुर्युं  भदुतोऽयुयुरुयुणुयु]—'  even  the  rich sometimes stand in
need of Grass;' similarly @deva[कुयुयुुः  युयुचुनं  यानेनं  यो  न  विहाञु  यात्मुकुयुयुः न
स्युतिवियुदुरुनां  यगु  किंयुयुनुत ङुणोषुलं],  &c.

(b)  The  particles  @deva[अलं]  and  @deva[छुतं]  meaning 'enough,'  @deva[अलं  इरितुते]
'enough of your  weeping  (do not weep ):'  @deva[छुतं  अत्युयुदुरेणु]  'away
with overpressing'; @deva[अलं] is  also  used  with a gerund  @deva[अलं  अन्युयुया
संगुयुन्युयु]  'away  with  misunderstanding.'

(c)  Particles  expressing  '  accompaniment  '  such  as @deva[समुङु],
@deva[सार्धं], @deva[सर्मं], @deva[सहु],  &c.;  @deva[आस्स  साकं  मया  सोयुणे]  (Bh.  VIII.  70.  );  @deva[वनं
मया सर्पविक्तुति पुन्युत्युः] Rag.  XIV.  63;  @deva[आङुतो  नित्युस्युयुते  सर्मं  हरिष्णो]-
@deva[गुताम्भुः ।] Sak I. 27. &c.

(d)  Words  meaning  '  having  or  destitute  of,'  @deva[समुयुट्युकुयोऽयुयुयुयुः]
(@deva[परित्युयुक्तुदुदुङु  यास्त्युनं  हुयुयुयुणु])  '  though  possessed  of  wealth,'  &c.;  @deva[अर्युनं
हुनुः] 'destitute of wealth.'

N.  B.  For  the  optional  use of  the  Instrumental  see  the  Abl.,
the  Gen.  and  the  Loc.  cases.
