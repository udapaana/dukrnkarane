## § 216. Locative Tatpurusha

A noun in the Locative is compounded—

(a) with @deva[शौण्ड] @[śauṇḍa], @deva[भक्त] @[bhakta], @deva[किटव] @[kiṭava] a rogue, @deva[प्रशीण] @[praśīṇa], @deva[संसृष्ट] @[saṃsṛṣṭa] adorned, invested with, @deva[अन्तर] @[antara], @deva[आविस्] @[āvis], @deva[बद्ध] @[baddha], @deva[प्रणीत] @[praṇīta], @deva[कुशल] @[kuśala], @deva[चतुर] @[catura], @deva[निपुण] @[nipuṇa], @deva[सिद्ध] @[siddha], @deva[पुष्ट] @[puṣṭa], @deva[पक्व] @[pakva], and @deva[बन्ध्य] @[bandhya];[^1] as @deva[अक्षेषु शौण्डः] @[akṣeṣu śauṇḍaḥ] @deva[अक्षशौण्डः] @[akṣaśauṇḍaḥ] skilled in dice; @deva[ईश्वरे भक्तिः] @[īśvare bhaktiḥ] @deva[ईश्वरभक्तिः] @[īśvarabhaktiḥ] dependent on God (when compounded the affix @deva[इन्] @[in] i.e. @deva[भक्तिन्] @[bhaktin] is compulsory; so the compounded form is always with @deva[भक्तिन्] @[bhaktin]); @deva[आतपपुष्टः] @[ātapapuṣṭaḥ] dried in the sun; @deva[स्थालीपक्वः] @[sthālīpakvaḥ] cooked in a pot; @deva[चक्रबन्ध्यः] @[cakrabandhyaḥ] a kind of poetic composition; etc.

(b) with words meaning a 'crow' when contempt is implied;[^2] @deva[तीर्थे काकः इव] @[tīrthe kākaḥ iva] @deva[तीर्थकाकः] @[tīrthakākaḥ] said in contempt of one who is very greedy; so @deva[तीर्थवायसः] @[tīrthavāyasaḥ]; similarly @deva[मगरकाकः] @[magarakākaḥ]:—@deva[वायसः] @[vāyasaḥ] etc. etc.

There are a few Loc. Tat. compounds given in the list @deva[परमेश्वरादि] @[parameśvarādi] having a similar sense; e.g. @deva[कूपे मण्डूकः इव] @[kūpe maṇḍūkaḥ iva] @deva[कूपमण्डूकः] @[kūpamaṇḍūkaḥ] a well-abiding frog i.e. one who knows nothing of the world outside the pale of his own village; so @deva[कूपे उदुपान-मण्डूकः] @[kūpe udupāna-maṇḍūkaḥ]; @deva[उदुम्बरमशि-मशकः] @[udumbaramaśi-maśakaḥ] lit. an insect in the fruit of the Udumbara tree; metaphorically it means the same as @deva[कूपमण्डूकः] @[kūpamaṇḍūkaḥ]; @deva[कूपकच्छपः] @[kūpakacchapaḥ] etc. In some cases there is an aluk of the Loc.; as @deva[शैलूषः] @[śailūṣaḥ] 'a boasting coward', @deva[गेहेखिन्नाषः] @[gehekhinnāṣaḥ] 'a braggadocio'; @deva[गेहोनर्दी] @[gehonardī] 'a house-hero', 'a carpet knight', @deva[भोजने कुशलः] @[bhojane kuśalaḥ] @deva[भोजनेकुशलः] @[bhojānekuśalaḥ] 'clever in eating only' i.e. 'a good-for-nothing fellow', @deva[भोजने चतुरः] @[bhojane caturaḥ] @deva[भोजनेचतुरः] @[bhojānecaturaḥ]; @deva[गोष्ठे कूरः] @[goṣṭhe kūraḥ] @deva[गोष्ठेकूरः] @[goṣṭhekūraḥ]; @deva[गोष्ठे विद्वान्] @[goṣṭhe vidvān] @deva[गोष्ठेविद्वान्] @[goṣṭhevidvān]; @deva[गेहे गर्ज्यः] @[gehe garjyaḥ] @deva[गेहेगर्ज्यः] @[gehegarjyaḥ], etc.

**Note:**—Words of this class do not enter into further combination with other words.

(c) with another, when they both imply a name (@deva[संज्ञा] @[saṃjñā]); there is an aluk of the Loc. term when the noun ends in a consonant or @deva[अ] @[a];[^3] as @deva[अरण्ये तिलकाः] @[araṇye tilakāḥ] lit 'wild sesamum yielding no oil, hence anything not answering to one's expectations;' similarly @deva[वने कसेरुकाः] @[vane kaserukāḥ]; @deva[कान्तारे सारः] @[kāntāre sāraḥ] 'a bamboo' (also @deva[कान्तारसारः] @[kāntārasāraḥ], vide Si. IV. 61.). These compounds are nitya (obligatory) as remarked by Bhattoji.

(d) with potential p. participles in @deva[य] @[ya] implying an obligation; @deva[मासि देयम्] @[māsi deyam] (@deva[ऋणम्] @[ṛṇam]); @deva[पुत्रे देयम्] @[putre deyam] (@deva[धनम्] @[dhanam]); the aluk by § 217 (b) below.

(e) with p. p. participles when it expresses a part of the day or night; @deva[तत्] @[tat] may take the place of the Loc.;[^4] as @deva[पूर्वाह्णे छन्नम्] @[pūrvāhṇe channam] @deva[पूर्वाह्णछन्नम्] @[pūrvāhṇachannam]; @deva[अपराह्णे छन्नम्] @[aparāhṇe channam] @deva[तत्राछन्नम्] @[tatrāchannam] etc; but @deva[अह्नि इदम्] @[ahni idam]; and with the p. p. participles when reproach is implied, with an aluk of the Loc; as '@deva[अवतसेनकुलस्य इव इदम् तव] @[avatasenakuḷasya iva idam tava]' 'this thy action is similar to that of a mungoose on the hot ground.' @deva[अवतसेनकुलस्थितम्] @[avatasenakuḷasthitam] is used to express the inconstancy of a person.
