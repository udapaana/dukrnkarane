## § 215. Aluk of the Genitive

Instances of the Aluk of the Gen. The noun in the Genitive may retain its case ending—[^1]

(a) when reproach is to be implied, as @deva[चोरस्य कुलम्] @[corasya kulam], but @deva[ब्राह्मणकुलम्] @[brāhmaṇakulam]. Also when @deva[देवानाम्] @[devānām] is followed by @deva[प्रिय] @[priya] and the whole means a fool; otherwise @deva[देवप्रियः] @[devapriyaḥ].

(b) when the words @deva[वाचस्] @[vācas], @deva[दिवस्] @[divas] and @deva[प्रत्यक्षतस्] @[pratyakṣatas] are followed by @deva[युक्ति] @[yukti], @deva[दृष्ट] @[dṛṣṭa] and @deva[हर] @[hara] respectively; as @deva[वाचस्युक्तिः] @[vācasyuktiḥ] 'skilful speech,' @deva[दिवोदृष्टः] @[divodṛṣṭaḥ] 'a particular appearance of stars in the shape of a staff in the sky,' @deva[प्रत्यक्षतोहरः] @[pratyakṣatoharaḥ] 'one who robs another in his very presence while he is looking on,' hence 'a goldsmith, a rogue.'

(c) in the following cases:—@deva[दिवोदासः] @[divodāsaḥ] 'a king of Kāshi,' @deva[दिवस्पतिः] @[divaspatiḥ] 'Indra,' @deva[वाचस्पतिः] @[vācaspatiḥ] lord of speech, Brihaspati; @deva[शुनःशेफः] @[śunaḥśephaḥ], @deva[शुनःपुच्छः] @[śunaḥpucchaḥ] and @deva[शुनोलाङ्गूलः] @[śunolāṅgūlaḥ] 'sons of Ajigarta.'

(d) and optionally when followed by @deva[पुत्र] @[putra] and censure is implied;[^2] @deva[दास्याः पुत्रः] @[dāsyāḥ putraḥ] or @deva[दास्योपुत्रः] @[dāsyoputraḥ] a bastard, a knave; but @deva[ब्राह्मणपुत्रः] @[brāhmaṇaputraḥ].

(e) When a word ending in @deva[इक] @[ika] is compounded with another and there is the relation by blood or some kind of learning, the aluk is necessary; and optional when it is followed by @deva[स्वसृ] @[svasṛ] and @deva[शिष्य] @[śiṣya].[^3] The initial @deva[स्] @[s] of @deva[स्वसृ] @[svasṛ] is optionally changed to @deva[ष] @[ṣa] when preceded by @deva[मातुर्] @[mātur] or @deva[पितृ] @[pitṛ] and when there is aluk, and necessarily when there is no aluk in a compound; e.g. @deva[होतुः स्वसा] @[hotuḥ svasā], @deva[होतृशिष्यः] @[hotṛśiṣyaḥ] a pupil of the hotri; @deva[मातुः स्वसा] @[mātuḥ svasā], or @deva[मातुष्वसा] @[mātuṣvasā] and @deva[मातृष्वसा] @[mātṛṣvasā]; so @deva[पितुः स्वसा] @[pituḥ svasā], etc. (but @deva[मातुः स्वसा] @[mātuḥ svasā], @deva[पितुः स्वसा] @[pituḥ svasā] when uncompounded); @deva[स्वसुःपतिः] @[svasuḥpatiḥ] or @deva[स्वसृपतिः] @[svasṛpatiḥ]; but @deva[होतृधनम्] @[hotṛdhanam].
