## § 833. The principal sense of the Ablative case is @deva[अयुयुदुयुनं] or

§ 833.  The principal sense of the  Ablative case is  @deva[अयुयुदुयुनं]  or
'motion  away  from  it;'  hence  the  noun  from  which  the  motion,
real  or  conceived,  takes  place  is  put  in  the  Ablative  case;
@deva[ग्रामुत्युयुगुच्छुति]  'comes  from  a  village;'  @deva[युयुयुत्युट्]  'falls  '  Ablative  case;
from  a  running  horse;  @deva[सदुयुजुयुत्युरुयु  भुयुते].

(a)  ‡  Words  having  the  sense of  @deva[जुयुयुयुयु],  @deva[विरास]  or  'cessation,'
'pause,'  and  @deva[यवुयुयुट्]  'swerving  from,'  also govern  the  Ablative
case;  @deva[यापुयुट् युयुट्युयुयुते]  'he  hates sin;'  @deva[न  नमुः  अघुयुमुयुछोऽदुयुयुरिस्युरुकुर्मों
विरुरुयुतु]  (Reg.  VIII.  22  )  also  new  monarch,  steady  in
action,  did  not  desist  from  efforts  until  they  bore  their  fruit';
@deva[युयुनोत्युयुयुयुयुति]  'swerves  from  duty';  @deva[स्युरुविकुत्युयुयुयुयुनुः]  (Meg.  1.)
'careless,  failing  in  the  discharge  of  the  duties  (of  his  office)';
so  @deva[प्रयुयुस्युहुयुनि];  @deva[यदुयुशोयु  निवुरुतुत  संवुनुरुस्युरु  यद्युशुयुयुट्]  (Manu  Smr.
V.  49.),  &c.

@deva[यट्]  with  @deva[यु]  in  the  sense  of  'to  be  careless  about'  is  used  with
the  Loc.  also;  @deva[जुदुयुयुद्युच्  प्रयुयुद्युचिन  यमुदुयुट्  विषुयुक्तुयुः  ।]  (Manu  Smr.
II.  213)  on  this  account  wise  men  are  not  careless  about
their  wives.
