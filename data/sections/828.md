## § 828. * The object governed by an infinitive mood not

§  828.  * The  object  governed  by  an  infinitive  mood  not
actually used but implied, is put in the Dative; @deva[फुलुत्युयो  यात्युति]  i. e.
(@deva[फुटुयु])  @deva[हुयुरु]  'goes for (in  order  to  bring)  fruits;'  @deva[हुहुकुयुयु
प्रयुछुगुयुः] (i. e  @deva[युट्युदुयुमुयुट्युदुट्युट्युलुयुदुदुङु])  'We  bow  down  to  Nṛisiṁha
(so  propitiate  him  )'

(a)  The  Dative of an abstract noun formed from a root may
be  used  to  express  the  sense  of  the  Infinitive  of  the  same  root;
@deva[शुयुयुयु  यात्युति]  'goes  to  perform  a  sacrifice;'  @deva[त्युयुयुयु  संचुयुत्युयुर्णों]
(Rag.  I.  7.  )  'who  had  amassed  wealth  in  order  to  give  it
away,'  &c.
