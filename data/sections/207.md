## § 207. Dative Tatpurusha

A noun in the Dative is compounded[^1]—

(a) with another expressive of the material of which the thing expressed by the noun in the Dative case is made; as @deva[यूपाय द्रुः] @[yūpāya druḥ] @deva[यूपद्रुः] @[yūpadruḥ] 'wood for a sacrificial post.'

(b) and with the words @deva[अर्थ] @[artha], @deva[बलि] @[bali], @deva[हित] @[hita], @deva[इष्ट] @[iṣṭa] and @deva[रक्षित] @[rakṣita]. The compound with @deva[अर्थ] @[artha] is a nityasamāsa and takes the gender and number of the noun it qualifies; @deva[ब्राह्मणाय अर्थं] @[brāhmaṇāya arthaṃ] @deva[ब्राह्मणार्थं] @[brāhmaṇārthaṃ] @deva[सूपः] @[sūpaḥ] broth for a Brāhmaṇa; @deva[ब्राह्मणार्थं इदं] @[brāhmaṇārthaṃ idaṃ] @deva[ब्राह्मणार्थं] @[brāhmaṇārthaṃ] @deva[यवागु] @[yavāgu] (water-gruel); @deva[ब्राह्मणार्थं इदं] @[brāhmaṇārthaṃ idaṃ] @deva[ब्राह्मणार्थं] @[brāhmaṇārthaṃ] @deva[पयः] @[payaḥ]; @deva[भूतेभ्यो बलिः] @[bhūtebhyo baliḥ] @deva[भूतबलिः] @[bhūtabaliḥ] an offering to the bhūtas (certain created beings); @deva[गवे हितं] @[gave hitaṃ] @deva[गोहितम्] @[gohitam] good for a cow; @deva[गवे इष्टं] @[gave iṣṭaṃ] @deva[गोइष्टम्] @[goiṣṭam]; @deva[गवे रक्षितं] @[gave rakṣitaṃ] @deva[गोरक्षितम्] @[gorakṣitam].
