## § 646. Non-Desiderative Uses of क्यच्

This form of the Nominal verb has not always a desiderative meaning.

**(a)** The affix @deva[य] @[ya] is added in the sense of 'treating or considering like,' @deva[पुत्रीयति छात्रम्] @[putrīyati chātram] 'he treats the pupil as a son,' @deva[विष्णुयति द्विजम्] @[viṣṇuyati dvijam] 'he treats the Brāhmaṇa like Viṣṇu,' @deva[प्रासादीयति कुट्यां भिक्षुः] @[prāsādīyati kuṭyāṃ bhikṣuḥ] 'the beggar considers his hut a palace,' @deva[कुटीयति प्रासादे राजा] @[kuṭīyati prāsāde rājā] 'the king in his palace considers himself to be in a hut.'

**(b)** It is added to @deva[नमस्] @[namas], @deva[वशिस्] @[vaśis] and @deva[विस्मय्] @[vismay] in the senses of 'adoring,' 'serving' and 'striking with wonder' respectively, @deva[नमस्यति देवान्] @[namasyati devān] 'he adores the gods', @deva[वशिस्यति गुरुम्] @[vaśisyati gurum] 'he serves the preceptor', @deva[विस्मययते लोकान्] @[vismayayate lokān] 'he strikes the people with wonder.' It is also added to @deva[तपस्] @[tapas] in the sense of 'practising,' @deva[तपस्यति] @[tapasyati].
