## § 131. The words ending in @deva[अन्] or @deva[वन्], such as @deva[शुन्] &c.

§ 131. The words ending in @deva[अन्] or @deva[वन्], such as @deva[शुन्] &c.
( learned ) and the words @deva[धनिन्] ( rich ) make their ins., dat.,
and abl. dual and pl., gen. dual, nom. voc. acc. dual and pl.,
regularly by the application of the case-terminations to the end
of the base ( i. e., they take the affixes @deva[यस्येतुर्थीयुतन्तास्निर्देशः] by Pan.
III. 2. 79 ) ; thus @deva[शुनौ] ( nom. dual ), @deva[शुनाम्] ( gen. pl. ) @deva[शुनि] ( loc.
sing ), @deva[शुनिभिः] ( ins. pl. ) @deva[विद्वनीनाम्], @deva[धनिनभिः ॥] In the ins. sing. and
in the nom. sing. @deva[न्] is dropped, as @deva[विद्वान्], @deva[विद्वना]; so also @deva[धनी],
@deva[धनिना ॥] The gen. voc. and loc. sing. and also the gen. sing.
are @deva[शुनिः], @deva[शुनिनः ॥] So @deva[धनिनः ॥] Before the affixes @deva[भिः], @deva[भ्यः],
@deva[भ्याम्], @deva[सु] beginning with the consonants, the penultimate @deva[न्] be-
comes a @deva[ण्] by the rule @deva[नो णः ॥] Thus @deva[धनिणभिः], @deva[धनिणभ्यः], @deva[धनिणसु],
@deva[विद्वणभिः], @deva[विद्वणभ्यः ॥]
