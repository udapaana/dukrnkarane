## § 30. Nasal Substitution

If a consonant, except @deva[र्] @[r] or @deva[ह्] @[h], coming at the end of a word, be followed by a nasal, the nasal of its class is optionally substituted for it[^2]; @deva[एतत् + मुरारि: = एतन्मुरारि:] @[etat + murāriḥ = etanmurāriḥ] and @deva[एतत्मुरारि:] @[etatmurāriḥ] (see § 22 sec. b.) this Murāri; @deva[षट् + मासा: = षण्मासा:] @[ṣaṭ + māsāḥ = ṣaṇmāsāḥ] and @deva[षट्मासा:] @[ṣaṭmāsāḥ] six months.




---

[^1]: N.B.** If the nasal belong to a termination the change is necessary; @deva[तन्मात्रम्] @[tanmātram] that alone; @deva[चिन्मात्रं] @[cinmātraṃ] mere knowledge. @deva[वाक् + मय = वाङ्मय] @[vāk + maya = vāṅmaya]. The word @deva[कड्कूवक] @[kaḍkūvaka] (see Rag. IV. 22.) is irregularly formed.
[^2]: @deva[यरोऽन्नासिकेऽन्नासिको वा] Pāṇ. VIII. 4. 45.
