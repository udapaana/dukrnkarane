## § 789. When an adjective qualifies two or more substantives

§ 789  When an adjective qualifies two or more  substantives
it  agrees  with  them  in  their  combined  number;  but  when
substantives  differ  in  gender,  the  adjective  will  be  masculine
when  the  substantives  are  masculine  and  feminine;  and  neuter
when  they  are  masculine,  feminine  and  neuter  @deva[राजा  रानी  च
युत्युच्युवुरुचितौ तुयुः]  the  king  and  his  queen  are  of  laudable
conduct; @deva[सर्त्युः  शुमवुत्युस्  द्युर्युद्यु]:  @deva[कोशुः  भूमिं  वयुः । अर्युद्युदितात्युति  चन्तोपि
प्रवर्तनुते न लोयुयुयुः]  fulfilment of duty, satisfaction of desires,  pride,
anger, happiness and  long  life,  all  these  proceed  undoubtedly
from wealth.
