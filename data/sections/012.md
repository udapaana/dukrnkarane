## § 12. Vowel Signs and Conjunct Consonants

The forms which the vowel signs assume when added to consonants and the changes which some letters undergo when compounded are given below, under (a) and (b).

### (a) Vowel Signs

@deva[अ] @[a] added to a consonant is denoted by the removal of the nether stroke; as @deva[क् + अ = क] @[k + a = ka]. The remaining vowels when compounded with a consonant become @deva[ा, ि, ी, ु, ू, ृ, ॄ, ॢ, े, ै, ो, ौ] respectively; as:

@line: @deva[क् + आ = का] @[k + ā = kā], @deva[क् + इ = कि] @[k + i = ki] :@

Similarly:

@line: @deva[की, कु, कू, कृ, कॄ, कॢ, के, कै, को, कौ] @[kī, ku, kū, kṛ, kṝ, kḷ, ke, kai, ko, kau] :@


### (b) Compounding Consonants

In compounding consonants they should be taken in the order in which they are pronounced; the last consonant takes a vowel, the preceding ones generally losing their nether and perpendicular strokes when combined e.g. @[tsna] ought to be written as @deva[त्स्न] @[tsna], @[ṇṇa] as @deva[ण्ण] @[ṇṇa], &c. Some letters, however, change their form slightly and others entirely, when compounded with other consonants; e.g. @deva[ल्प, त्र, श्च, ग्र] @[lpa, tra, śca, gra], &c.

@deva[र] @[r] immediately preceding another consonant (or the vowel @deva[ऋ] @[ṛ]) is denoted by the sign @deva[र्] written above the following consonant as @deva[र्क] @[rka]. It is then necessarily called a @deva[रेफ] @[refa].

### (c) @deva[क्ष] @[kṣa] and @deva[ज्ञ] @[jña]

In the conjuncts @deva[क्ष] @[kṣa] (@deva[क] @[k] + @deva[ष] @[ṣ]) and @deva[ज्ञ] @[jña] (@deva[ज] @[j] + @deva[ञ] @[ña]), the component elements are scarcely discernible.

### (d) Variant Forms

A few consonants are written in two ways; e.g.:

@line: @deva[त्र, त्र] @[tra]; @deva[क्र, क्र] @[kra]; @deva[स्थ, स्थ] @[stha]; @deva[क्त, क्त] @[kta]; @deva[स्त्र, स्त्र] @[stra] :@
