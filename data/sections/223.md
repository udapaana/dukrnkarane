## § 223. Karmadhāraya with Cardinal Points and Numerals

Names of quarters and numerals form a Karm. compound with any subanta (a declinable word) but only when the whole compound is an appellation;[^1] e.g. @deva[सप्तर्षयः] @[saptarṣayaḥ] 'Name of a constellation of stars,' 'Ursa Major;' @deva[पञ्चजनाः] @[pañcajanāḥ][^2] etc., @deva[पूर्वशालाकुम्] @[pūrvaśālākum] 'Name of a town in the east;' @deva[उत्तरा कुरवः] @[uttarā kuravaḥ] 'Northern Kurus; @deva[पञ्च ब्राह्मणाः] @[pañca brāhmaṇāḥ] etc. The compound is forbidden in other cases.

(a) But words denoting a cardinal point or numerals may be compounded with any other noun when a Taddhita termination is to be affixed to the compound so formed (or it conveys in addition to its sense as a Karm a derivative meaning such as is usually denoted by a Tad. aff.) or when the compound itself becomes the first member of another compound or when the compound forms a proper name;[^3] @deva[पूर्वां शालाम्] @[pūrvāṃ śālām] @deva[पूर्वशालाम्] @[pūrvaśālām] @deva[पूर्वशालाम्] @[pūrvaśālām] @deva[शालायां प्रद्] @[śālāyāṃ prad] @deva[पौर्वशालः] @[paurvaśālaḥ] 'being in the eastern hall' from @deva[पूर्वशाला] @[pūrvaśālā] + @deva[अ] @[a] (i.e. Tad. aff. @deva[अ] @[a] added by @deva[द्विपदपूर्वपदात् संख्यायां अण्] @[dvipadapūrvapadāt saṃkhyāyāṃ aṇ] Pāṇ. IV.2.107); so @deva[षट्] @[ṣaṭ] + @deva[मातृ] @[mātṛ] = @deva[षण्मातृ] @[ṣaṇmātṛ] 'six mothers' + Tad. @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[षाण्मातुरः] @[ṣāṇmāturaḥ] the son of six mothers; @deva[पूर्वां शालां प्रियां यस्य स] @[pūrvāṃ śālāṃ priyāṃ yasya sa] @deva[पूर्वशालाप्रियः] @[pūrvaśālāpriyaḥ] where @deva[पूर्वशाला] @[pūrvaśālā] is a @deva[पूर्वपद] @[pūrvapada] and not used independently, @deva[उत्तरद्वुस्तः] @[uttaradvastaḥ], @deva[दक्षिणद्रुवः] @[dakṣiṇadvuvaḥ] etc. which are names.
