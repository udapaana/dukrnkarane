## § 339. General Rules for Taddhita Affixes

The Taddhita affixes are added in various senses. They occasion various changes in the words to which they are added. The following general observations should be paid attention to:

(a) As a general rule the first vowel of a word takes its Vṛddhi substitute before the terminations @deva[अ] @[a], @deva[य] @[ya], @deva[इक] @[ika], @deva[ईन] @[īna], @deva[एय] @[eya], @deva[स्थ] @[stha], etc.

e.g. @deva[अश्वपति] @[aśvapati] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[आश्वपति] @[āśvapati] + @deva[अ] @[a]

(b) Before terminations beginning with a vowel or @deva[य] @[ya]: (1) the final @deva[अ] @[a], @deva[आ] @[ā], @deva[इ] @[i], and @deva[ई] @[ī] are rejected; (2) @deva[उ] @[u] and @deva[ऋ] @[ṛ] take their Guṇa substitute; (3) @deva[ओ] @[o] and @deva[औ] @[au] obey the ordinary rules of sandhi.

e.g. @deva[अश्वपति] @[aśvapati] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[आश्वपतः] @[āśvapataḥ]: belonging to Aśvapati

e.g. @deva[मनु] @[manu] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[मानवः] @[mānavaḥ]: a descendant of Manu

e.g. @deva[गो] @[go] + @deva[य] @[ya] = @deva[गव्यं] @[gavyaṃ]: belonging to a cow

Similarly @deva[दानव] @[dānava] from @deva[दनु] @[danu], etc.

(c) In the case of derivatives from compound words sometimes the initial vowel of the second word takes its Vṛddhi substitute, and sometimes the Vṛddhi is double.

e.g. @deva[गतसंवत्सरे] @[gatasaṃvatsare]: belonging to the last year

Similarly @deva[सुपाञ्चालकः] @[supāñcālakaḥ], etc.

@deva[सौहार्द] @[sauhārda] from @deva[सुहृद्] @[suhṛd]

@deva[सौभाग्य] @[saubhāgya] from @deva[सुभग] @[subhaga], etc.

When these terminations are added to a Dvigu compound both the words of which are the names of deities, Vṛddhi is substituted for the initial vowel of both.

e.g. @deva[आग्निमारुतं] @[āgnimārutaṃ]: a sacrifice offered to Agni and the Marut deities, etc.

(d) If the initial vowel of a word be preceded by the @deva[उ] @[u] and @deva[ऋ] @[ṛ] of a preposition the @deva[उ] @[u] or @deva[ऋ] @[ṛ] is first changed to @deva[ओ] @[o] or @deva[अर] @[ara] before Vṛddhi substitute can take place.

e.g. @deva[व्याकरण] @[vyākaraṇa] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[विआकरण] @[viākaraṇa] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[वैयाकरणः] @[vaiyākaraṇaḥ]

e.g. @deva[सुगन्ध] @[sugandha] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[सुअगन्ध] @[suagandha] + @deva[अ] @[a] = @deva[सौगन्धः] @[saugandhaḥ]

Similarly @deva[सौवस्तिक] @[sauvastak] from @deva[स्वस्ति] @[svasti]; @deva[सौवर] @[sauvara] from @deva[स्वर] @[svara], etc.

(e) Before consonantal Taddhita affixes, a final @deva[अ] @[a] is generally rejected; the final @deva[अन्] @[an] with the preceding vowel is sometimes dropped before vowel terminations and before such as begin with @deva[य] @[ya].

e.g. @deva[युवन्] @[yuvan] → @deva[युवत्वं] @[yuvatvaṃ]

e.g. @deva[राजन्] @[rājan] → @deva[राजकं] @[rājakaṃ], etc.

@deva[आत्मन्] @[ātman] → @deva[आत्म्य] @[ātmya] → @deva[आत्मीय] @[ātmīya]

There are various exceptions to this latter part of the rule.

e.g. @deva[राजन्य] @[rājanya] from @deva[राजन्] @[rājan], etc.

**N. B.** Other changes the student will easily note from the instances given.
