## § 266. † No @deva[क] is affixed

§ 266. † No @deva[क] is affixed

(a) when the whole compound is used as a name or when it ends in @deva[ईयस्], as @deva[विश्वे देवा यस्य विश्वदेवः] 'whose deities are the Viswadevas'; @deva[वहन् श्रेयस्ः यस्य वहुश्रेयाम्]. A Bah. ending in @deva[ईयस्] f. (i e. @deva[ईयसी] †) does not shorten its final vowel; e. g. @deva[बहुश्रेयसौ यस्य वहुश्रेयसी]: 'one having many excellent wives.' But @deva[अतिश्रेयसिः] as a Tatpurusha.

(b) to the word @deva[भ्रातृ] preceded by a term of praise; @deva[प्रशस्तौ भ्राता यस्य स प्रशस्तभ्राता]; but @deva[यस्वेभ्रातृकः] one whose brother is a fool.

(c) to the words @deva[नादि] and @deva[सन्नौ] both referring to the body of an animal; @deva[बहुनादि]: @deva[कायः] 'the body having many arteries'; @deva[बहुत]-@deva[न्नीमीवा] 'the many-veined neck'; but @deva[बहुनादीकः सुन्धः] 'a pillar with many ornamental lines'; @deva[बहुतन्नीका] (many-stringed) @deva[वीणा].

(d) to the final of the compound @deva[निष्प्रवाणिं] (@deva[निर्गता प्रवाणी] 'a weaver's shuttle' @deva[यस्य]) @deva[पटः] 'a new, unbleached garment.'

(e) to compounds formed according to rules §§ 251, 252, and 253, as @deva[सपुत्रः], @deva[उपदशम्], @deva[द्रक्षिणपूर्वी], &c.
