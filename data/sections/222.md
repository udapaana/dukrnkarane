## § 222. Adjective-Noun Karmadhāraya

An adjective may be compounded with the noun it qualifies in most cases[^1] as @deva[नीलं च तत् उत्पलं च] @[nīlaṃ ca tat utpalaṃ ca] @deva[नीलोत्पलम्] @[nīlotpalam], a blue lotus etc.; @deva[शुण्डाश्वौ] @[śuṇḍāśvau] @deva[सर्पेश्वरौ] @[sarpeśvarau] @deva[शुण्डाश्वरौ] @[śuṇḍāśvarau] where the comp. is necessary; but @deva[जामदग्न्यौ रामः] @[jāmadagnyau rāmaḥ]. Such compounds are called @deva[विशेषणपूर्वपदकर्मधारयम्] @[viśeṣaṇapūrvapadakarmadhārayam].

As a general rule in such compounds the qualifying word is placed first; but there are several exceptions which are given below.

(a) The qualified word is placed first and is always of the mas. gender if it denotes a class or species, when compounded with certain adjectives,[^2] e.g. @deva[युवहस्ति] @[yuvaहasti] 'a young female elephant', @deva[आखस्तोकः] @[ākhastοkaḥ] 'a small fire', @deva[उदुम्बरकतिपयम्] @[udumbarakatipayam] 'butter-milk mixed with a little water', @deva[गोप्राचि] @[goprāci] 'a cow that has brought forth a calf for the first time', @deva[गोधेनुः] @[godhenuḥ] 'a milch-cow (@deva[नवप्रसूतिका] @[navaprasūtikā])', @deva[गोवंशा] @[govaṃśā] 'a barren cow', @deva[गोविवत्] @[govivat] (@deva[गर्भघातिनी गौः] @[garbhaghātinī gauḥ]), @deva[गोवत्सतरी] @[govaτsatarī] 'a cow whose calf is one year old,' @deva[काठाभौजिकः] @[kāṭhābhaujikaḥ] 'an agnihotrin Brāhmana of the Katha branch of the Yajurveda,' @deva[काठाध्यापकः] @[kāṭhādhyāpakaḥ] 'a teacher of the Katha branch,'[^3] @deva[गौमताडिका-मदिविका-प्रकुण्डम्] @[gaumatāḍikā-madivikā-prakuṇḍam] (also mas. according to some)—@deva[उद्रः] @[udraḥ] (@deva[गवोद्र] @[gavodra])-@deva[तद्रज] @[tadraja] 'an excellent cow'. The last mentioned five words, all of which denote excellence, retain their own gender invariably; as @deva[ब्राह्मणमताडिका] @[brāhmaṇamatāḍikā] 'an eminent Brāhmana' etc.; but @deva[कुमारी मताडिका] @[kumārī matāḍikā], as no class is meant here.

(b) The words @deva[कहार] @[kahāra], @deva[सर्व] @[sarva], @deva[खञ्ज] @[khañja] lame, @deva[काण] @[kāṇa], @deva[कुण्ड] @[kuṇḍa] blunt, dull, @deva[स्फलित] @[sphalita] bald-headed, @deva[जीर्ण] @[jīrṇa], @deva[बद्ध] @[baddha], @deva[भिक्षुक] @[bhikṣuka] @deva[भीरु] @[bhīru], @deva[भिङ्गल] @[bhiṅgala], @deva[तुञ्ज] @[tuñja], @deva[जरठ] @[jaraṭha] hard, stiff, @deva[बधिर] @[badhira], @deva[कुब्ज] @[kubja] and @deva[वर्वर] @[varvara], may optionally precede the words in apposition to them;[^4] as @deva[जैमिनिकहारः] @[jaiminikahāraḥ] or @deva[कहारजैमिनिः] @[kahārajaiminiḥ] Jaimini who is turned tawny on account of his having practised penance in the sun.' etc.

(c) Words expressive of the persons or things condemned or reproached are placed first;[^5] as @deva[वैयाकरणासत्] @[vaiyākaraṇāsat] 'a bad grammarian,' 'one who has forgotten his grammar and therefore can make no use of his knowledge;' (@deva[पुत्रः सत् पुदर्ग वित्तम् आर्जयन् नु यं सुख्यति अध्यात्मवेत्युपत्ति एकद्युब्ध्यते] @[putraḥ sat pudarga vittam ārjayan nu yaṃ sukhyati adhyātmavetyupatti ekadbydubhyate] Tatv.); so @deva[मीमांसकाबुद्धः] @[mīmāṃsakābuddhaḥ] 'a mīmāmsaka who is a sceptic' or 'an unbeliever', except in the case of the words @deva[पाप] @[pāpa], @deva[अपक] @[apaka] and @deva[किम्] @[kim]; as @deva[पापनापितः] @[pāpanāpitaḥ] 'a bad barber,' @deva[अपककुम्भकारः] @[apakakumbhakāraḥ] 'a mean, silly potter,' @deva[कुत्सितः रामः] @[kutsitaḥ rāmaḥ] @deva[किंरामः] @[kiṃrāmaḥ], @deva[किंसखा] @[kiṃsakhā] (@deva[कुत्सितः सखा] @[kutsitaḥ sakhā]), etc.

(d) Words in apposition to @deva[इन्द्र] @[indra], @deva[नाग] @[nāga] and @deva[कुञ्जर] @[kuñjara] are also placed first;[^6] e.g. @deva[नृपेन्द्रः] @[nṛpendraḥ] 'an eminent king,' @deva[तापसकुञ्जरः] @[tāpasakuñjaraḥ], @deva[मुनिनागः] @[munināgaḥ], etc.

(e) @deva[कतर] @[katara] and @deva[कतम] @[katama] are compounded only when they ask a question with reference to a class (@deva[जाति] @[jāti] family), and are placed first;[^7] e.g. @deva[कतर-कतम-काठः] @[katara-katama-kāṭhaḥ] 'which Brāhmana of the Katha branch'? @deva[कतर-कतम-कलापः] @[katara-katama-kalāpaḥ] 'which Brāhmana of the Kalāpa branch'? But @deva[कतरः पुत्रः] @[kataraḥ putraḥ] 'which son'?

(f) The word @deva[कुमार] @[kumāra] (and also @deva[कुमारी] @[kumārī] changed to @deva[कुमार] @[kumāra]) is placed first when compounded with one of the words @deva[श्रमणा] @[śramaṇā], @deva[मन्त्रिता] @[mantritā], @deva[कुलटा] @[kulaṭā], @deva[गर्भिणी] @[garbhiṇī], @deva[तापसी] @[tāpasī], @deva[दासी] @[dāsī], @deva[अध्यापक] @[adhyāpaka], @deva[पीवर] @[pīvara], @deva[सुभग] @[subhaga], @deva[मुखर] @[mukhara], @deva[कुशल] @[kuśala], @deva[चपल] @[capala] and @deva[निपुण] @[nipuṇa];[^8] as @deva[कुमारश्रमणा] @[kumāraśramaṇā] 'a female mendicant,' @deva[कुमारमन्त्रिता] @[kumāramantritā] 'a young female ascetic'; @deva[कुमारसुभग] @[kumārasubhaga]- 'a delicate boy or girl, @deva[कुमारगर्भिणी] @[kumāragarbhiṇī], @deva[कुमाराध्यापकः] @[kumārādhyāpakaḥ], etc.

(g) The words @deva[एक] @[eka], @deva[सर्व] @[sarva], @deva[जरठ] @[jaraṭha], @deva[पुराण] @[purāṇa], @deva[नव] @[nava] and @deva[केवल] @[kevala]; and the words @deva[पूर्व] @[pūrva], @deva[अपर] @[apara], @deva[चरम] @[carama], @deva[जघन्य] @[jaghanya] 'hindmost,' @deva[समान] @[samāna], @deva[मध्य] @[madhya], @deva[मध्यम] @[madhyama] and @deva[वीर] @[vīra] are always placed first in a Karmadhāraya compound.[^9] @deva[अपर] @[apara] when followed by @deva[अर्ध] @[ardha] is changed to @deva[पश्च] @[paśca]; as @deva[एकनाथः] @[ekanāthaḥ] 'sole master or lord', @deva[सर्वेश्वरः] @[sarveśvaraḥ], @deva[जरठयोगविदः] @[jaraṭhayogavidaḥ], 'an old logician,' @deva[पुराणमीमांसकः] @[purāṇamīmāṃsakaḥ]; 'old mīmāmsā philosophers,' @deva[नवटीकाकुम्] @[navaṭīkākum], @deva[पूर्ववैयाकरणाः] @[pūrvavaiyākaraṇāḥ], 'the old grammarians,' @deva[अपराध्यापकः] @[aparādhyāpakaḥ] @deva[अपरस्यार्धम्] @[aparasyārdham] @deva[अपेक्ष] @[apekṣa] @deva[पश्चार्धम्] @[paścārdham] 'the hinder part or side of the body;' @deva[चरमराजः] @[caramarājaḥ] the last king,' @deva[समानाधिकरणम्] @[samānādhikaraṇam] 'being in the same category or predicament'; @deva[वीरैकः] @[vīraikaḥ] 'a solo warrior; i.e. an eminent warrior', etc. The form @deva[एकवीर] @[ekavīra] (found used by great writers) also may be defended.

(h) The words @deva[सत्] @[sat], @deva[महत्] @[mahat], @deva[परम] @[parama], @deva[उत्तम] @[uttama] and @deva[उत्कट] @[utkaṭa] are compounded with another noun when they are used as terms of praise or excellence;[^10] @deva[सद्वैद्यः] @[sadvaidyaḥ] 'an excellent physician' @deva[महद्वैयाकरणम्] @[mahadvai yākaraṇam]. etc. but @deva[उत्कटौ गौः] @[utkaṭau gauḥ] where @deva[उत्कट] @[utkaṭa] means 'uplifted, or 'drawn out of.'
