## § 252. Indeclinables and Numerals in Bahuvrīhi Compounds

A compound of an indeclinable or a numeral or of @deva[अह] @[aha], @deva[अहर्] @[ahar] or @deva[अधिक] @[adhika] with a numeral is a Bahuvrīhi. In the formation of such compounds the final vowel except that of @deva[बहु] @[bahu] and @deva[गण] @[gaṇa], or the final consonant with the preceding vowel of the latter numeral and the @deva[स्थिति] @[sthiti] of @deva[विंशति] @[viṃśati] are dropped and @deva[अ] @[a] added.

### Examples

@deva[दशानां समीपे ये सन्ति ते उपदशाः] @[daśānāṃ samīpe ye santi te upadaśāḥ] 'about ten' i.e. nine or eleven; @deva[द्वौ वा त्रयो वा त्रिद्वाः] @[dvau vā trayo vā tridvāḥ] 'two or three'; @deva[द्वे वा त्रीणि वा त्रिद्वाणि] @[dve vā trīṇi vā tridvāṇi]; @deva[द्विः आवृत्ता दश द्विदशाः] @[dviḥ āvṛttā daśa dvidaśāḥ] 'ten repeated twice i.e. twenty; so @deva[त्रिदशाः] @[tridaśāḥ] etc., @deva[विंशतेः आसन्नाः आसन्नविंशाः] @[viṃśateḥ āsannāḥ āsannaviṃśāḥ] 'nearly twenty', @deva[त्रिंशतः अदूराः अदूरत्रिंशाः] @[triṃśataḥ adūrāḥ adūratriṃśāḥ] 'not far from thirty', @deva[अधिकचत्वारिंशाः] @[adhikacatvāriṃśāḥ] 'more than forty'; but @deva[उपचत्वः, उपचत्वाः] @[upacatvaḥ, upacatvāḥ].

### Special Rule for Catur

@deva[चतुर्] @[catur] preceded by @deva[उप] @[upa] or @deva[त्रि] @[tri] does not drop any letter but simply has an @deva[अ] @[a] added to it; @deva[त्रयो वा चत्वारो वा त्रिचतुराः] @[trayo vā catvāro vā tricaturāḥ], @deva[चतुर्णां समीपे ये सन्ति ते उपचतुराः] @[caturṇāṃ samīpe ye santi te upacaturāḥ].
