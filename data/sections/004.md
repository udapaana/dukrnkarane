## § 4. Classification of Consonants

(Continued from § 3.c): that which proceeds from the upper part of the vocal organs, @deva[अनुदात्त] @[anudātta] that which proceeds from their lower part, while @deva[स्वरित] @[svarita] arises out of a mixture of these two.[^1] But these are ignored in classical Sanskrit. They are marked only in Vedic works: the @deva[उदात्त] @[udātta] is left unmarked; the @deva[अनुदात्त] @[anudātta] is marked with a horizontal line underneath; and @deva[स्वरित] @[svarita] has a perpendicular stroke above it. e.g. @deva[इव] @[iva] @deva[पोऽश्वाः] @[po'śvāḥ]; &c. Ṛg. V. 61. 2 @deva[रथान् ये रः] @[rathān ye raḥ] । &c. Ṛg. X. 78. 4. @deva[द्वातृकं यो खः] @[dvātṛkaṃ yo khaḥ] &c. Ṛg. X. 145. 4.

Thus there are eighteen different modifications of each of the vowels @deva[अ, इ, उ, ऋ] @[a, i, u, ṛ], and twelve of @deva[ऌ, ए, ऐ, ओ] @[ḷ, e, ai, o] and @deva[औ] @[au]; for there is no long @deva[ऌ] @[ḷ] and the last four have not their corresponding short vowels.

These are represented by thirty-three syllabic signs arranged as below:
