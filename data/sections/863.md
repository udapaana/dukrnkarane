## § 863. With superlatives, and words having the sense of

§ 863.  With  superlatives,  and  words  having  the  sense  of
the superlative, the Genitive is used; @deva[सुणुयो ब्राह्मुयुणुः अद्युयुः]; @deva[अयुयुयुणुयुयुयु]-
@deva[हुनुयुयुट्युोयुयुयुट्] (Rag. V. 4) the chief of sages, the authors to the
Mantras.

Note:—Words  having  the  sense  of  comparatives  are  used
with the Ablative  and  sometimes  with  the  Instrumental;  @deva[अयुयु]-
@deva[स्युरुयुयुट्  वुलं  हरिनुः]  or  @deva[अविवुकुः]  this  person  is  superior  or  inferior  to
him  in  strength;  similarly  @deva[देवुदुत्तुयो  यदुवुदुयूनुयुट्युङु]:  @deva[दूद्युहुयु  वा];  @deva[कौ  द्युच्
स्युनुत्युरो  भुयुयु]  who  will  have a happier  end  than  I?  The  word
@deva[सुणुयुनै]  is  used  with  the  Locative  as  in  the  Locative  or  the  Instru-
mental;  @deva[द्युनुहुंङु  नारायुणुविकुयुनुपि  सोऽभुवुद्]  he  was  more  (i.  e.  dearer)
to  them  than  their  sons;  @deva[तेषुयुयुस्युयुजुङुनं  यायुयुः  यङुत्युनु  द्युयुयुयुङु  क्तुयुयुयुः]
they  passed  five  months  and  twelve  nights  more  than  (those
years);  @deva[कुदुचुदुयुङुअकुच्  यत्युयुः]  A Kadru is larger than a Kūdava.

THE LOCATIVE CASE.
