## [^4] 21. Vṛddhi Substitution for ए/ऐ and ओ/औ

When @deva[अ] @[a] or @deva[आ] @[ā] is followed by @deva[ए] @[e] or @deva[ऐ] @[ai] and @deva[ओ] @[o] or @deva[औ] @[au], @deva[ऐ] @[ai] and @deva[औ] @[au] are respectively substituted for both;[^2] @deva[छुप्य + एकन्त = छुप्यैकन्त्य] @[chupya + ekanta = chupyaikantya]; @deva[देव + ऐश्वर्य = दैवैश्वर्य] @[deva + aiśvarya = daivaiśvarya] the majesty of god; @deva[सा + एव = सैव] @[sā + eva = saiva] she alone; @deva[भव + औषध्य = भवौषध्य] @[bhava + auṣadhya = bhavauṣadhya] medicine against birth and re-birth. @deva[विद्या + औत्सुक्यं = विद्यौत्सुक्यं] @[vidyā + autsukyaṃ = vidyautsukyaṃ] longing for knowledge.

### Exceptions

### (a) Preposition + Root beginning with ए/औ

If a preposition ending in @deva[अ] @[a] be followed by a root beginning with @deva[ए] @[e] or @deva[औ] @[au], @deva[ए] @[e] or @deva[औ] @[au] is substituted;[^1] @deva[प्र + एजते = प्रेजते] @[pra + ejate = prejate] quakes very much; @deva[उप + ओषति = उपोषति] @[upa + oṣati = upoṣati] burns something near; but if the root be a denominative, vṛddhi is optional; @deva[उप + एककीयति = उपेककीयति] @[upa + ekakīyati = upekakīyati] or @deva[उपैककीयति] @[upaikakīyati]; @deva[प्र + ओषीयति = प्रोषीयति] @[pra + oṣīyati = proṣīyati] or @deva[प्रौषीयति] @[prauṣīyati].


### (b) Indefinite Sense

When @deva[अ] @[a] is followed by @deva[एत्र] @[etra] in an indefinite sense, @deva[ए] @[e] is substituted for both: @deva[क + एत्र = केत्र भोक्ष्यते] @[ka + etra = ketra bhokṣyate] where will you dine to-day? (The place being not definitely known); but @deva[तत्रैव] @[tatraiva] (why, I shall dine at your house).

### (c) Before ओम् and आ

@deva[अ] @[a] followed by @deva[ओं] @[oṃ] and @deva[आ] @[ā] (prep.) is dropped e.g. @deva[शिवाय + ओं नमः = शिवायों नमः] @[śivāya + oṃ namaḥ = śivāyoṃ namaḥ]; @deva[शिव + एहि] @[śiva + ehi] (@deva[आ + इहि] @[ā + ihi]) @deva[= शिवेहि] @[= śivehi].

### (d) Optional Vṛddhi before ओष्/ओष्ठ

The vṛddhi is optional when the ending @deva[अ] @[a] of a word is followed by @deva[ओष्] @[oṣ] a cat, or @deva[ओष्ठ] @[oṣṭha] the lower lip, in a compound: @deva[स्थूल + ओष्ः = स्थूलौष्ः] @[sthūla + oṣaḥ = sthūlauṣaḥ] or @deva[स्थूलोष्ः] @[sthūloṣaḥ]; @deva[विम्बौष्, विम्बोष्ः] @[vimbauṣ, vimboṣaḥ].

### (e) Elision in Special Compounds

The final vowel or the final consonant with the preceding vowel is dropped in the case of the following words when followed by certain words in a compound:[^4] @deva[शाक] @[śāka] the country of the Śakas @deva[+ अम्बु] @[+ ambu] a well @deva[= शाकम्बु] @[= śākambu]; @deva[कम्बू] @[kambū] name of a country @deva[+ अम्बु = कम्बम्बु] @[+ ambu = kambambu]; @deva[कुल + अटा = कुलटा] @[kula + aṭā = kulaṭā] one who moves from one mansion to another, a woman of bad character; @deva[सीमन् + अन्तः = सीमन्तः] @[sīman + antaḥ = sīmantaḥ].
