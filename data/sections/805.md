## § 805. Prepositions used with the Accusative

The following[^1] prepositions are used with the Accusative.

(a) @deva[अति] @[ati] 'superior to, higher than' @deva[अनु] @[anu] 'just after, after by the side of, inferior to,' @deva[अपि] @[api] 'close to' and @deva[उप] @[upa] 'near, inferior to;'

e.g. @deva[अति देवान् कृष्णः] @[ati devān kṛṣṇaḥ]: Kṛṣṇa excels gods in might.

@deva[अतिरामम् गोविन्दः] @[atirāmam govindaḥ]: Govinda is superior to Rāma.

@deva[जपम् प्रावर्त] @[japam prāvarta]: it rained just after the muttering of prayers.

@deva[त्वम् मानुषम् ते] @[tvam mānuṣam te]: every thing of thee is after mine.

@deva[अनु पितरम् गच्छति सुतः] @[anu pitaram gacchati sutaḥ]: the son imitates his father.

@deva[न भवान् अनु रामम् चेत] @[na bhavān anu rāmam cet]: if you are not inferior to Rāma; so @deva[अनु हरि सुराम्] @[anu hari surām].

@deva[भक्तो हरि अपि] @[bhakto hari api]: the devotee is close to Hari.

@deva[उप न ते भवम्] @[upa na te bhavam]: your act is not like that of a hero (lit. is inferior to his); @deva[उपहरिम् सुराम्] @[upaharim surām] &c.

(b) The prepositions @deva[आभे] @[ābhe], @deva[अनु] @[anu], @deva[परि] @[pari] and @deva[प्रति] @[prati] are used with the Accusative when they refer to particular things; @deva[गिरिम्-आभे-अनु-परि-प्रति-विद्योत्युते विद्युत] @[girim-ābhe-anu-pari-prati-vidyotyute vidyut]: the lightning flashes towards the mountain; also when they mean 'to every one,' &c. @deva[वृक्षम् प्रत्येकम्-आभे-अनु-परि-प्रति-सिच्यति] @[vṛkṣam pratyekam-ābhe-anu-pari-prati-sicyati]: waters each and every tree; so @deva[आभे-अनु-परि-प्रति स्त्री स्त्री जातमन्युमः] @[ābhe-anu-pari-prati strī strī jātamanyumaḥ].

(c) @deva[अनु] @[anu], @deva[परि] @[pari] and @deva[प्रति] @[prati] also govern the Accusative when they imply 'one's own share;' @deva[लक्ष्मीः हरिस्य-परि-प्रति] @[lakṣmīḥ harisya-pari-prati]: Lakṣmī is the proper share (property) of Hari.
