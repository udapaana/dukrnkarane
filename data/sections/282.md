## § 282. (c) and hence the feminine ends in @deva[आ]; see N. B. For @deva[सक्थि] see also (e) below.

§ 282 (c) and hence the feminine ends in @deva[आ]; see N. B. For @deva[सक्थि] see also (e) below.

N. B The fem. of Bah. compounds ending in @deva[अक्ष] is formed by adding @deva[आ] when it qualifies an inanimate object.

(b)* @deva[अङ्गुलि], when the compound is an attribute of @deva[द्रुरुः]; @deva[पञ्च अङ्गुल्यो यस्य तत्पञ्चाङुलं द्रुरुः] (@deva[अङुलिसह्रशावचं शास्त्राद्विशेषणकाठम्]); but @deva[पञ्चाङुलौ हस्तः] the hand having five fingers.

(c)† The words @deva[मूर्धन्] preceded by @deva[द्वि] or @deva[त्रि], @deva[लोमन्] preceded by @deva[अन्तर्] and @deva[वाहि]; and @deva[नेत्र] used in apposition to the name of a constellation of stars; @deva[द्वौ मूर्धानौ यस्य स द्विमूर्धः] two-headed; @deva[त्रिमूर्धः]; @deva[अन्तर्लोमः]; @deva[वाहिलोमः]; @deva[यत्रा नेता यासां ताः घुमनेता रात्रयः] 'nights of which the Mriga constellation is the leader' i. e. 'whose position in the heavens marks their advance;' so @deva[पुष्यनेत्रः] &c.

(d) † Femi
