## § 921. Present for Future with Interrogatives

With interrogatives the Present is often used in the sense of the Future and has also to be regarded as implying @deva[किम्] @[kim]:

**Examples:**

- @deva[किं करोमि] @[kiṃ karomi] i.e. @deva[करिष्यामि] @[kariṣyāmi] What shall I do?
- @deva[कुत्र गच्छामि] @[kutra gacchāmi] i.e. @deva[गमिष्यामि] @[gamiṣyāmi] Whither shall I go?
- @deva[एतेषां कतमं] @[eteṣāṃ katamaṃ] or @deva[एतेषां किमर्थं भोजनयसि] @[eteṣāṃ kimarthaṃ bhojanayasi] (i.e. @deva[भोजयिष्यसि] @[bhojayiṣyasi] or @deva[भोजयितासि] @[bhojayitāsi]) which of these persons will you feed?

So @deva[को वै वदति यातेति] @[ko vai vadati yāteti], &c.; but @deva[कः यातं यदित्याति] @[kaḥ yātaṃ yadityāti].

**(a)** It is also used in the sense of the Future in conditional sentences as implying a condition of the occurrence of the desired object:

**Example:**

- @deva[रोकनं दुयूयूति] @[rokanaṃ duyūyūti] (@deva[राति दास्यति] @[rāti dāsyati] वा) स स्युनै याति (@deva[मातु यास्यति] @[mātu yāsyati] वा) he, who offers or (will offer) food, goes (or will go) to heaven (i.e. if one offers &c.)
