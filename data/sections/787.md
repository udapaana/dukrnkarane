## § 787. When an indeclinable used with an adjectival force

§ 787.  When an indeclinable used with an  adjectival  force
takes the place of the verb,  the  object  governed  by  it is put in
the Nominative case.  * @deva[विपश्युयोपि  संवर्धुयं  घटं  छुट्टुयुमुतुक्तानुत्रु] (Kum.
V. 71) it is not fit to cut down  even  a  poisonous  tree having
first reared it up; here the indeclinable  @deva[अलमुद्युतं]  is  equivalent to
@deva[न इच्युयुते] and the whole sentence to @deva[न्युयुं  संवर्धुयं च्युट्युद्यु  असन्मर्युतुयु] (@deva[न
इच्युयुत्युर्])  @deva[योति विपश्युयुः  स्मात ।]

Concord of the Adjective with the Substantive.
