## § 221. Upamāna Karmadhāraya Compounds

(a) A word expressive of the standard of comparison (@deva[उपमान] @[upamāna]) may be compounded in a Karmadhāraya with another denoting the common quality or ground of comparison;[^1] as @deva[घन इव श्यामः] @[ghana iva śyāmaḥ] @deva[घनश्यामः] @[ghanaśyāmaḥ] dark like a cloud. Such compounds are called @deva[उपमानपूर्वपदकर्मधारयम्] @[upamānapūrvapadakarmadhārayam].

(b) Similarly a noun denoting the person or thing compared (@deva[उपमेय] @[upameya]) may be compounded with words indicative of excellence, such, as @deva[व्याघ्र] @[vyāghra], @deva[सिंह] @[siṃha], @deva[चन्द्र] @[candra], @deva[कमल] @[kamala], etc.[^2] no mention of the common attribute (of similarity) being made;[^3] as @deva[पुरुषौ व्याघ्रः इव] @[puruṣau vyāghraḥ iva] @deva[पुरुषव्याघ्रः] @[puruṣavyāghraḥ] a man like a tiger (i.e. as bold as a tiger); @deva[मुखं चन्द्रः इव] @[mukhaṃ candraḥ iva] @deva[मुखचन्द्रः] @[mukhacandraḥ] a moon-like face i.e. as delightful as the moon; @deva[मुखं कमलम् इव] @[mukhaṃ kamalam iva] @deva[मुखकमलम्] @[mukhakalamalam] etc.; these are called @deva[उपमानोत्तरपदकर्मधारय] @[upamānottarapadakarmadhāraya].

**Note 1.**—The difference between the two is this:—In the former the common quality of comparison is actually expressed; while in the latter it is left to be understood; in fact if it be expressed there can be no compound; as @deva[पुरुषः व्याघ्रः इव शूरः] @[puruṣaḥ vyāghraḥ iva śūraḥ].

**Note 2.**—The above Karmadhārayas may be dissolved as @deva[मुखम् एव चन्द्रः] @[mukham eva candraḥ] @deva[मुखचन्द्रः] @[mukhacandraḥ], @deva[मुखम् एव कमलम्] @[mukham eva kamalam] @deva[मुखकमलम्] @[mukhakalamalam], etc. Dissolved in either way there will be no difference in the formation of the compounds; but there will be difference both in the meaning and in the metaphor. In one case prominence will be given to @deva[चन्द्र] @[candra] or the moon and the figure will be 'upamā (a simile),' while in the other to @deva[मुख] @[mukha] or the face in which case the figure will be 'Rūpaka.' Compounds like @deva[पादः एव पद्मम्] @[pādaḥ eva padmam] @deva[पादपद्म] @[pādapadma], @deva[विद्या एव धनम्] @[vidyā eva dhanam] @deva[विद्याधनम्] @[vidyādhanam], etc. are termed @deva[अव्ययीभावपूर्वपदकर्मधारयम्] @[avyayībhāvapūrvapadakarmadhārayam].
