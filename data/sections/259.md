## § 259. Mutual Action Compounds - Indeclinable Bahuvrihi

### Main Text

§ 259.* Two nouns alike in from in the Loc. and meaning 'something that can be seized,' or in the Instrumental, meaning 'weapons or things used as weapons,' may be compounded in a Bah. when the sense is 'the fight thus began' and the idea of an exchange of action is to be implied. In such compounds the final vowel of the first member is lengthened and that of the second is changed to @deva[इ] The compounds so formed are of the nature of Avyayibhāvas and are indeclinable. The final @deva[इ] takes Guna substitute before the final @deva[इ]. † e.g. @deva[केशेषु केशेषु युद्धं तृत्तं यस्मिन् केशाकेशि] 'a battle in which the warriors fight seizing each other's hair'; @deva[दण्डेभि: दण्डेभि: महदेभेदं युद्धं प्रवृत्तं दण्डादण्डि]; similarly @deva[मुष्टीमुष्टि], @deva[हस्ताहस्ति], @deva[बाद्यबाद्यावि], @deva[छुसलाछुसलि], &c.; but if the instruments used by both the parties be not the same, no compound is possible; @deva[इदाछुसलि] will, therefore, be wrong.

### Observation

(a) The following words also change their final to @deva[इ]; @deva[द्वौ दण्डौ यस्मिन् मध्यणे तद् द्विदण्डि]; similarly @deva[द्विघुसालि], @deva[उभाउभया खालि], @deva[उभाहस्ति], @deva[उभयहस्ति], @deva[उभाउभयापाणि], @deva[पद्बाहु] &c.
