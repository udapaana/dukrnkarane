## § 50. स् Elision in Pronouns

### (a) Elision of स् in तद् and एतद् Nominative Singular

The @deva[स्] @[s] of the nom. sing. of @deva[तद्] @[tad] and @deva[एतद्] @[etad] masculine is dropped before a consonant when they do not end in @deva[क्] @[k] or are not used in a negative Tatpurusha;[^1] e.g. @deva[स पुष्णुः] @[sa puṣṇuḥ]; @deva[एष विष्णुः] @[eṣa viṣṇuḥ]; but @deva[एषः को रुद्रः] @[eṣaḥ ko rudraḥ] this Rudra; @deva[असौ + शिवः = असौशिवः] @[asau + śivaḥ = asauśivaḥ] that is not Śiva (a negative Tatpurusha); @deva[एष + रुद्रः = एषोरुद्रः] @[eṣa + rudraḥ = eṣorudraḥ].

### (b) Poetic License for Vowel Combination

Sometimes in poetry the @deva[स्] @[s] of @deva[सः] @[saḥ] and @deva[एषः] @[eṣaḥ] is considered as not existing when followed by a vowel other than @deva[अ] @[a], so that the two vowels may combine in order to meet the exigencies of the metre;[^2] e.g. @deva[तेषाम् + ऋषिः + इङ्कुर्धौ = पश्येत + य ईक्षीत] @[teṣām + ṛṣiḥ + iṅkurdhau = paśyet + ya īkṣīta] etc. Ṛg. II. 24. 1
