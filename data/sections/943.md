## § 943. The Imperative Mood - General Uses

The Imperative Mood does not express merely command, but also entreaty, benediction, courteous enquiry, gentle advice, ability, etc.

**(a) Second Person:** This Mood is used to express command, entreaty, gentle advice and benedictions or blessings:

- @deva[गच्छ (त्वं) कुसुमपुरं] @[gaccha (tvaṃ) kusumapuraṃ] go to Kusumapur
- @deva[परितायवतं परित्रायस्व] @[paritāyavataṃ paritrāyasva] help! help!
- Reverently go to God; forgive
- @deva[जीवतु] @[jīvatu] live
- @deva[चिरायुर्भव] @[cirāyurbhava] prosper
- @deva[कुरु निवृषाणवपि सख्यानोजनं] @[kuru nivṛṣāṇavapi sakhyānojanaṃ] (Śak. IV.) serve your elders and treat your co-wives as if they were your friends
- @deva[पाणे काचकुरस्व न गत्वा गाच् रायाणं] @[pāṇe kācakurasva na gatvā gāc rāyāṇaṃ] be thou my messenger, go to Rāghava, and say to him
- She was addressed the truth by Fate when he said 'do thou obtain a husband not devoted to any other lady.'

**(b) Third Person:** Often used to express a blessing and sometimes gentle command:

- @deva[विपाणं लिखिङ् गोः...अदानः पायापर्णो हरिचरणारुणौरुरुयू] @[vipāṇaṃ likhiṅ goḥ...adānaḥ pāyāparṇo haricaraṇāruṇaururayū] may this handful of flowers scattered on the feet of Hari give us success
- @deva[वर्णेन्यः कालपर्णो गावत] @[varṇenyaḥ kālaparṇo gāvata] may rain pour down
- @deva[विध्यायु भारतिने स्तनोत् लकातोदस्या] @[vidhyāyu bhāratine stanot lakātodasyā] (Mud. III. 1st)

**(c) First Person:** Expresses a question, necessity, ability, etc.:

- @deva[किं करावू तुयू न युर्य व्याकरणाम्] @[kiṃ karāvū tuyū na yarya vyākaraṇām] what shall I tell you?
- @deva[पादामि] @[pādāmi] I would die
- @deva[स्वान् परायाम् मनति निर्युनां तव] @[svān parāyām manati niryunāṃ tava] we will (are able to) do this thing, oh queen, which is agreeable to you
- @deva[नहि अदैयानुं यौरने स्वापस्य तै नीति] @[nahi adaiyānuṃ yaurane svāpasya tai nīti] (Bhaṭṭṛi XX. 6.) let your thought be 'I must not commit the horrible murder of an ambassador.'
