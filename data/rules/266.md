No @deva[क] is affixed[^1]

**(a)** when the whole compound is used as a name or when it ends in @deva[ईश्वर], as @deva[विश्वे देवा यस्य विश्वेदेवः] 'whose deities are the Viswadevas'; @deva[बहवः भेयार्याः यस्य बहुभेयार्य]. A Bah. ending in @deva[ईश्वर] f. (i. e. @deva[ईश्वरी][^2]) does not shorten its final vowel; e. g. @deva[बहवः श्रेयस्यो यस्य बहुश्रेयसीः] 'one having many excellent wives.' But @deva[अतिश्रेयसीः] as a Tatpurusha.

**(b)** to the word @deva[भ्रातृ] preceded by a term of praise; @deva[प्रशस्तो भ्राता यस्य स प्रशस्तभ्राता]; but @deva[मूर्खभ्रातृकः] one whose brother is a fool.

**(c)** to the words @deva[नाडि] and @deva[तन्त्री] both referring to the body of an animal; @deva[बहुनाडिः कायः] 'the body having many arteries'; @deva[बहुत-न्त्रीग्रीवा] 'the many-veined neck'; but @deva[बहुनाडीकः स्तम्भः] 'a pillar with many ornamental lines'; @deva[बहुतन्त्रीका] (many-stringed) @deva[वीणा].

**(d)** to the final of the compound @deva[निष्प्रवापिः] (@deva[निर्गता प्रवाणी] 'a weaver's shuttle' @deva[यस्य]) @deva[पटः] 'a new, unbleached garment.'

**(e)** to compounds formed according to rules @ref[251], @ref[252], and @ref[253], as @deva[सपुथः], @deva[उपवहवः], @deva[दक्षिणपूर्वी], &c.
