A Prâtipadika ending in @deva[अ] and the words included in the Ajâdi group[^1] form their feminine in @deva[आ];[^2] as @deva[भुञ्जान-भुञ्जाना] ; @deva[अज-अजा], @deva[एडका], @deva[अभा], @deva[चटका], @deva[मूषिका]; @deva[बाला], @deva[बत्सा], @deva[होडा], @deva[मन्दा], @deva[विडाला], all meaning 'a young girl'; (of these the first five form an exception to @ref[313], and the second to @ref[308], a°); @deva[आ] is also added to @deva[फल] when compounded with @deva[सत्], @deva[भद्र], @deva[अर्जुन], @deva[पद्म] and @deva[पिण्ड]; to @deva[पुष्प] when compounded with @deva[सत्], words ending in @deva[अच्], to @deva[काण्ड], @deva[प्रान्त], @deva[शत] and @deva[एक]; to @deva[शूद्र] not preceded by @deva[महत्], when class is implied, and to @deva[मल] preceded by @deva[अ]; @deva[संस्फला]; @deva[भद्रफला], @deva[पुणयफला], &c all 'kinds of creeper'; @deva[सत्-पाक्-काण्ड-प्रान्त-शत-एक-पुष्पा], 'kinds of creeper' @deva[शूद्रा] 'a woman of the @deva[शूद्र] class;' @deva[अमला].

**(a)** But if the Prâtipadika end in @deva[क] (the member of the termination ) the preceding @deva[अ] is changed to @deva[इ];[^3] @deva[सर्विका], @deva[कारिका], &c.; the words @deva[मासक], @deva[नरक], and those ending in the Tad affix @deva[क] with @deva[क] suffixed to it, changed their @deva[अ] to @deva[इ] similarly; @deva[मासिका], @deva[नरकं कथयति] calls out to @deva[इति नरिका], @deva[दाक्षिणात्यिका], @deva[देहत्यिका] a woman of this place.

Exceptions:—The @deva[अ] is not changed to @deva[इ] when the @deva[क] is added to the pronouns @deva[यत्] and @deva[तत्]. or when it is a part of the Tad. affix @deva[त्यक], or when there is a compound, or when there is a word included in the Kshipaka group (@deva[क्षिपकगण]);[^4] as @deva[यक्का], @deva[तक्का], @deva[अभिष्यका], 'a tableland', @deva[उपत्यका] 'the land at the foot of a mountain.' @deva[बहुपरिव्राजका नगरी], @deva[क्षिपका], @deva[धुक्का], @deva[कन्यका], &c.

**(b)** @deva[अ] is optionally changed to @deva[इ] in the following cases.—[^5]

**(1)** @deva[तारका] 'a star', @deva[तारिका] 'able to protect'; @deva[वर्णका] 'a cloak, a garment;' @deva[अणिका] (in other cases), @deva[वर्तका-वर्तिका शकुनौ प्राचाम्] @deva[उदीचां च वर्तिका]; @deva[अष्टका] 'kind of Shrāddha,' @deva[अष्टिका] (in other cases).

**(2)** @deva[सूतिका-सूतका], a woman recently delivered; @deva[सुविका] or @deva[सुवका], @deva[वृन्दारका] or @deva[वृन्दारिका], a goddess.

**(3)** when the @deva[क] follows upon the fem. termination @deva[आ] shortened to @deva[अ] and is preceded by @deva[य] or @deva[क]; as @deva[आर्या+क=आर्यक+ आ=आरिका] or @deva[आर्यका]; @deva[षट्का+क=षट्कक+आ=षट्किका] or @deva[षट्कका] &c. but @deva[संकाश्ये भवा सांकाश्यिका], @deva[अभीका], @deva[शुभयातीति शुभया]; @deva[अज्ञाता शुभयाः शुभयिका].

**(c)** This change of @deva[अ] to @deva[इ] is necessary when the @deva[क] follows the @deva[य] or @deva[क] of a root @deva[इनयिका], @deva[इपाक्रिका], &c.
