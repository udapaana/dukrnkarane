## Dative/Accusative with मन् Showing Contempt (Non-Animals)

The indirect object of the root @deva[मन्] cl. 4 'to think' if not an animal, may be put in the Accusative or the Dative case when contempt is to be shown; @deva[न त्वां तृणं मन्ये तृणाय वा] 'I do not consider thee a straw;' but @deva[न त्वां तृणं मन्ये] (@deva[मन्] cl. 8) when mere comparison is meant, the Accusative is used; @deva[त्वां तृणं मन्ये] (Mb.).

### Kātyāyana's Vārtika on अप्राणिषु

On @deva[अप्राणिषु] 'non-animals', Kātyāyana remarks: @deva[अप्राणिषु इत्यवतीय नौकाकाष्ठुकडङ्गकाकशुन्येष्विति वाच्यम्] i.e. "Instead of 'if not an animal' it should be said, 'if not one of a ship, or food, or a crow, or a parrot, or a jackal'"; @deva[न त्वां मन्ये नावं अन्नं वा], @deva[न त्वां शुकं श्वानं वा मन्ये]; in the former case though neither @deva[नौ] nor @deva[अन्न] is an animal, the Accusative ought to be used; in the latter though @deva[श्वान] is an animal the Dative may be optionally used.[^1]

[^1]: Pāṇ. II. 3. 17: @deva[मन्यकर्मण्यनादरे विभाषाऽप्राणिषु]
