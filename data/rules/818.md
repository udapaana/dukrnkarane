## Instrumental: Use, Need, Accompaniment, Possession

The following words also govern the Instrumental e.g.:

(a) Words expressing the idea of use or need, such as @deva[किम्], @deva[कार्यं], @deva[अर्थः], @deva[प्रयोजनं] and the like, and the root @deva[कृ] with @deva[किम्] used in the same sense; @deva[येनेन किं यः] &c. what is the use of his wealth who &c.; @deva[दुष्यच् कार्यं भदतोऽयरणय]— 'even the rich sometimes stand in need of Grass;' similarly @deva[कापः पचनं यानेन यो न विहाय यात्मकापः न स्वतिवियरनां यगु किंयनत ङणोष्यलं], &c.

(b) The particles @deva[अलं] and @deva[लं] meaning 'enough,' @deva[अलं रितिते] 'enough of your weeping (do not weep):' @deva[लं अत्येदेरेण] 'away with overpressing'; @deva[अलं] is also used with a gerund @deva[अलं अन्यथा संगतनुते] 'away with misunderstanding.'

(c) Particles expressing 'accompaniment' such as @deva[समं], @deva[सार्धं], @deva[सर्मं], @deva[सह], &c.; @deva[आस्स साकं मया बोणे] (Bh. VIII. 70.); @deva[वनं मया सर्विक्तति पुन्त्यः] (Rag. XIV. 63); @deva[आगतो नित्यस्यते सर्मं हरिष्णोगतांभुः]. (Ś'ak. I. 27) &c.

(d) Words meaning 'having or destitute of,' @deva[समेतकोऽययः] (@deva[परित्यक्तदङ यास्तनं हययण]) 'though possessed of wealth,' &c.; @deva[अर्थेन हीनः] 'destitute of wealth.'

N.B. For the optional use of the Instrumental see the Abl., the Gen. and the Loc. cases.
