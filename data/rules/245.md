As a general rule a Tatpurusha compound follows the gender of the final noun.[^1]

**Exceptions:**[^2]

**(a)** Compounds with @deva[प्राप्त] and @deva[आपन्न] for their first member and the Prādi compounds follow the gender of the words they qualify; @deva[प्राप्तजीविकः नरः], @deva[प्राप्तजीविका स्त्री], @deva[निष्कोषात्मकः पुरुषः] &c.

**(b)** A Tatpurusha ending in @deva[राज], @deva[अह्न] and @deva[अह] is masculine, except when @deva[राज] is preceded by a numeral and @deva[अह] by @deva[पुण्य] and @deva[द्विन]; e. g. @deva[पूर्वराजः], @deva[मध्याह्नः], @deva[सप्ताहः], @deva[नवराजम्], @deva[गणराजम्], @deva[पुण्याहम्], @deva[द्विनाहम्]; also that ending in @deva[पथ] ( substituted for @deva[पथिन्], vide @ref[280] ) preceded by a numeral or an indeclinable; as @deva[त्रयाणां पन्थाः त्रिपथम्], @deva[विरूपः पन्थाः विपथम्], a bad or a wrong way; but @deva[सुपन्था], @deva[अतिपन्थाः] as they do not end in @deva[पथ]; ( see @ref[285] )

**(c)**[^3] A collective Dwigu is neu.; but that ending in @deva[अ] is generally feminine, and that ending in @deva[आ] is optionally so in which case it takes the termination @deva[ई]; @deva[पञ्चगर्व] a collection of five cows; @deva[त्रयाणां लोकानां समाहारः त्रिलोकी]; but @deva[पञ्चपर्णम्], @deva[त्रिभुवनम्], @deva[चतुर्युगम्] &c.; @deva[पञ्चानां सहानां समाहारः पञ्चसहस्री], @deva[पञ्चसहस्र्म्]. The @deva[न] of final @deva[अन्] is dropped and the Dwigu is optionally feminine @deva[पञ्चतन्त्री-त्रम्], ( fr. @deva[पञ्च + तन्त्रम्] a carpenter ).

**(d)**[^4] A Tatpurusha ending in @deva[उपज्ञा] and @deva[उपक्रम] is of the neuter gender, when the idea of @deva[प्राथम्य] 'being the first' of what is known or commenced ( introduced ) is meant to be expressed; @deva[पाणिनीयोपज्ञा पाणिनीयपूर्वं प्रयुक्तः] the work ( grammar ) first taught or composed by Pāṇini. @deva[नन्दोपक्रमं द्रोणः] the drona ( a measure of corn ) first brought into use in the reign of King Nanda.

**(e)**[^5] A Tatpurusha ending in @deva[छाया] is neuter when the objects casting the shade are many @deva[वृक्षूणां वृक्षच्छायम्].

**(f)**[^6] A Tatpurusha having for its first member a synonym of @deva[राजन्] ( and not @deva[राजन्] itself ) or the words @deva[रक्षस्], @deva[पितामह] &c. and @deva[सभा] for its latter member is neuter; as @deva[इन्द्रसभम्], @deva[ऐश्वरसभम्], a king's council; but @deva[राजसभाः] @deva[रक्षःसभम्], @deva[पितामहसभम्]; also that ending in @deva[सभा] when it means 'a multitude'; as @deva[स्त्रीसभम्] 'an assembly of women'; but @deva[धर्मसभा] in the sense of @deva[धर्मशाला].

**(g)**[^7] A Tatpurusha ending in @deva[सेना], @deva[सुरा], @deva[छाया], @deva[शाला] and @deva[निशा] is optionally neuter; @deva[ब्राह्मणसेना-नम्], @deva[मधुसुरा-रम्] malt liquor beer, @deva[कुड्यच्छाया-यम्], the shadow of a wall; @deva[गोशाला-लम्], @deva[श्वनि-शा प्रम्] ( the 14th day of the dark half of a month, so called because certain dogs observe a fast on this night, according to the Sābarabhāṣya ).

**N. B.** These rules hold good in the case of Tat. alone; so @deva[इन्द्रसेनो राजा] ( Bah. ); @deva[असेना] ( Neg. Tat. ), @deva[परमसेना] ( Karm. ).
