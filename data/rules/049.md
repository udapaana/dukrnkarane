@deva[अः] at the end of a word is changed to @deva[र्] before @deva[र्];[^1] e.g. @deva[पुनः] + @deva[रमते] = @deva[पुनर्रमते] he again rejoices.

**Exception.** This change of Visarga into @deva[र्] is not admissible in the following cases, as they are prohibited in the Vedas, viz.:—

**(a)** When there are two or more words besides the one to which the succeeding @deva[र्] is attached; e.g. @deva[पूर्णः पूर्णं राज्यशतम्] not @deva[पूर्णः पूर्णर्राज्यशतम्]. The rule applies when there are only one or two words, as @deva[पूर्णं राज्यशतम्] or @deva[पूर्णर्राज्यशतम्].

**(b)** When the word is already a compound; @deva[राजपुत्रः] + @deva[रमते] = @deva[राजपुत्रः रमते], not @deva[राजपुत्रर्रमते] the prince rejoices.

**(c)** When the compound is a Bahuvrīhi or Dvandva or consists of an indeclinable and a verb; as @deva[पीतोदकः] + @deva[रमते] = @deva[पीतोदकः रमते], not @deva[पीतोदकर्रमते] one who has drunk water rejoices. So @deva[रामकृष्णौ रमेते] not @deva[रामकृष्णौ र्रमेते] Rāma and Kṛṣṇa rejoice; @deva[अनु राजते] not @deva[अनुर्राजते] he shines after.

**(d)** In Tatpuruṣa compound, it is optional; @deva[राजपुत्रः] + @deva[रमते] = @deva[राजपुत्रः रमते] or @deva[राजपुत्रर्रमते] the prince rejoices.
