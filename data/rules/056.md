Sup (@deva[सुप्]) is the technical term for a case termination in Sanskrit. Declension consists in adding the case termination to the crude form or base.[^1]

---

[^1]: The general terminations as given by Pāṇini are—@deva[सुजोसमौ-च्छष्टाभ्यामिभिस्ङेभ्याम्भ्यस्ङसिभ्याम्भ्यस्ङसोसाम्ङ्योस्सुप्] — Pāṇ. IV. 1. 2. i. e. Nom. @deva[सु औ जस्]; Acc. @deva[अम् औट् शस्]; Ins. @deva[टा भ्याम् भिस्]; Dat. @deva[ङे भ्याम् भ्यस्]; Ab @deva[इसि भ्याम् भ्यस्]; Gen. @deva[ङस् ओस् आम्]; Loc. @deva[ङि ओस् सुप्]. Some of these terminations, as in common with Pāṇini's system of nomenclature, have certain Its (servile letters) added or prefixed to them; e.g. @deva[ट] in @deva[सु], @deva[टु] in @deva[जस्] &c. It will be easily seen that the term @deva[सुप्] is obtained by taking the first letter and the last It in the sūtra.
