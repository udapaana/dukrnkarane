The present participle is to be used when contemporaneity of action is to be indicated. It is often idiomatically used to express the sense of 'while' or 'whilst' in English; @deva[अरण्ये चरन्] while wandering in the forest; @deva[विवाहसूत्रकं विभवन् एव] while he yet wore the marriage string. Vide 670 (b).
