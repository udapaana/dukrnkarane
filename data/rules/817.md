## Instrumental: Excelling, Resemblance, Swearing, Rejoicing, Motion, Price

The Instrumental is used with words expressing the idea of—

(a) excelling; @deva[गुणैः महाभाग तत् गुणैर्वधते] Oh fortunate one, you excel your ancestors in that (devotion); @deva[भरावितेन भारं सहस्रशः] (Mu. 3. 17) he surpasses by his lustre, the lustre of the sun; @deva[दुहितरं संदृश्य रूपेणासयुयुञ्य वनह्नगिः]. (Ś'ak. I.)

(b) resemblance, likeness, equality; @deva[स्वरेण पितरि आदिति] resembles his father in voice; @deva[देदवन्येन स्वरेण च रायच्छदिटरति] (Utta. 4.); @deva[अस्य यं सादृश्यं मातुः मुखेन वादति] his face resembles that of his mother; @deva[विष्णुना पराक्रमो न] equal to Viṣṇu in valour.

(c) swearing; @deva[भरतेनात्मनेन च शपे मूर्धना] I swear by Bharata and myself; @deva[सर्पितव्यं मम जीवितेन] I conjure thee by my life.

(d) rejoicing, being pleased; @deva[भक्त्या गुरो यथामङ्कारेया च प्रीतःऽस्मि] I am pleased with thee by thy devotion to your preceptor and compassion upon me; @deva[काष्ठलुयुः स्वल्येनापि तुष्यति] a low person is satisfied with little.

(e) motion (the conveyance or the part of the body on which a thing is carried being put in the Instrumental case); @deva[रथेन संचरते] 'he moves about in a chariot.'

(f) price (real or metaphorical) at which a thing is bought; @deva[शतेन क्रीतः] bought for a hundred (rupees, etc.), @deva[स्वात्मना अपि आर्तस्य स्नेहेन मित्रस्य हितं रुदितव्यं] 'the life of a friend ought to be saved even at the cost of one's life.'[^1]

[^1]: Pāṇ. II. 3. 23 and related Vārtikas (Sid. Kau.)
