## Dative for Purpose, Result, Evil Portent, Benefit

(a) A noun expressive of the purpose for which anything is done, or the result to which anything pertains, or action for which anything exists, is put in the Dative case; @deva[मुक्तायै हरिं भजति] 'worships Hari for final beatitude;' @deva[भक्तिमार्गाय करणं ज्ञानाय जायते] 'devotion leads to knowledge;' @deva[सुधा करणं जायतं सन्युबनं] (Mb.) 'water gruel tends to (produce) fire,' @deva[सुवर्णाय विरूपण हिरण्यं] (Mah. Bhā.) 'gold for Kuṇḍala (a kind of ornament);' @deva[स्तम्भाय दारुः] 'wood for a sacrificial post,' &c.

N.B. The roots @deva[भू] or @deva[अस्] are often omitted after a Dative used in this sense @deva[काव्यं यशसे] (@deva[भवति]), 'poetry is for fame.'

(b) An evil foreboded by a portentous phenomenon is also put in the Dative case; @deva[वायवे कपिला विद्युत्] 'the tawny lightning forebodes a storm.'

(c) The word @deva[हितं] is used with the Dative; @deva[ब्राह्मणाय हितं] 'good for a Brāhmaṇa.'[^1]
