When @deva[इ], @deva[उ], @deva[ऋ] and @deva[ऌ], short or long, are followed by a dissimilar vowel, @deva[य्], @deva[व्], @deva[र्] and @deva[ल्] are respectively substituted for them[^1]; e.g. @deva[इति] + @deva[आह] = @deva[इत्याह]; @deva[बुधी] + @deva[उपास्यः] = @deva[बुध्युपास्यः] adored by the wise; @deva[मधु] + @deva[अरिः] = @deva[मध्वरिः] the enemy of @deva[मधु] i.e. Viṣṇu, @deva[पितृ] + @deva[अंशः] = @deva[पित्रंशः] a portion of the creator; @deva[ऌ] + @deva[आकृतिः] = @deva[लाकृतिः] a figure like that of @deva[ऌ] &c.

**Obs.** Several of the above words may have more than one form when combined; e.g. @deva[बुधी] + @deva[उपास्यः] = @deva[बुध्यु] + @deva[उपास्यः] by the general rule:—

**N.B.** The following rules and the two (a), (b) given under @ref[20], though they properly form the subject of the next section, are given here to avoid confusion. Ordinary students may pass over these except sec. (b) of the present rule.

**(a)** A consonant except @deva[ङ्], preceded by a vowel, is optionally doubled, if no vowel follows[^2]; @deva[बुध्यु] + @deva[उपास्यः] = @deva[बुद्ध्युपास्यः] by the general rule; then @deva[बुद्ध्यु] + @deva[उ] + @deva[पास्यः] = @deva[बुद्ध्युपास्यः] by this rule, the @deva[उ] being dropped; and also a third form @deva[बुद्धूपास्यः] (vide § 22, sec. d., below).

**(b)** When a consonant, except a nasal or a semivowel, is followed by a soft unaspirate or aspirate (3rd or 4th letter of a class) it is changed to the soft unaspirate (3rd letter) of its class[^3]; @deva[बुद्ध्यु] + @deva[उपास्यः] = @deva[बुद्ध्युपास्यः].

**(c)** Semivowels, when preceded by a letter of the first five classes except @deva[म्] are optionally doubled[^4]; thus @deva[बुद्ध्यु] + @deva[उपास्यः] = @deva[बुद्ध्यु] + @deva[उपास्यः] and @deva[बुद्ध्य्यु] + @deva[उपास्यः]; @deva[बुद्ध्युपास्यः] = @deva[बुद्ध्युपास्यः] and @deva[बुद्ध्य्युपास्यः]. Thus there are four forms ultimately, viz. @deva[बुध्युपास्यः], @deva[बुद्ध्युपास्यः], @deva[बुद्धूपास्यः] and @deva[बुद्ध्य्युपास्यः].

Similarly @deva[मधु] + @deva[अरिः] = @deva[मध्वरिः], @deva[मद्ध्वरिः], @deva[मद्धुवरिः], and @deva[मद्धुव्वरिः]. @deva[पित्] + @deva[अङ्कः] = @deva[पितङ्कः] and @deva[पित्तङ्कः]. In @deva[ल्] + @deva[आकृतिः] of course there is no possibility of any other form.

**(d)** A consonant except @deva[ङ्], coming after @deva[र्] or @deva[ल्] preceded by a vowel is optionally doubled[^5]; e.g. @deva[हरि] + @deva[अनुभवः] = @deva[हर्य्] + @deva[अनुभवः] = @deva[हर्यनुभवः] by the general rule; and optionally @deva[हर्य्य्] + @deva[अनुभवः] = @deva[हर्य्यनुभवः] (Hari's experience) by this rule; so @deva[न हि] + @deva[अस्ति] = @deva[न ह्यस्ति] and @deva[न ह्य्यस्ति].
