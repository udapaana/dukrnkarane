## Third Aorist of Bases with Initial Vowels

Bases with initial vowels:—

(a) If a root begins with a vowel and ends in a single consonant, the consonant is reduplicated and @deva[इ] added to it in the reduplicative syllable: @deva[अद्]-@deva[आद्]-@deva[आदिद्द्]; @deva[आप्]-@deva[आप्]-@deva[आपिप्]; &c.

(b) If it ends in a conjunct consonant with @deva[ध्], @deva[र्] or @deva[द्] for its first member, the second member of the conjunct is reduplicated. @deva[उन्द्]-@deva[उचन्द्]-@deva[उचिन्द्द्] and finally @deva[औचिन्द्त्] 3 Sing, similarly @deva[आविन्द्त्] from @deva[अञ्ज्] (considered as @deva[अजद्], otherwise @deva[आविद्त्]), @deva[आविञ्छत्] from @deva[अञ्च्]; @deva[आविज्जत्] from @deva[अञ्ज्], &c.

(c) The roots @deva[उच्], @deva[आञ्च्], @deva[अञ्च्], @deva[अञ्], @deva[आद्], @deva[अर्ध्] Ātm. and some others, substitute @deva[अ] for @deva[इ] in the reduplicative syllable; 3 sing. @deva[अौजत्], @deva[आवच्त्], @deva[आजघत्], @deva[आञ्चवत्], @deva[आअर्त्], @deva[आर्द्त्], &c.
