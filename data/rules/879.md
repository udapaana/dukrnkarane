## Locative/Genitive Absolute for 'when, while, since, although'

The locative or Genitive Absolute may be used to express the sense of the English particles 'when, while, since, although,' &c. (and may thus supply the place of a pluperfect tense); @deva[एवं तयोः परस्परं वदतोः] while they two were thus talking; @deva[दिवाभागे अवसरि भविष्यद्ध्यक्षयो] (Meg. 40); Thou wilt accomplish the rest of thy journey when the sun rises again.
