## Genitive with Superlatives; Ablative with Comparatives

With superlatives, and words having the sense of the superlative, the Genitive is used; @deva[श्रेष्ठो ब्राह्मणः अद्यः]; @deva[अर्षीणाम्हनस्पत्याम्] (Rag. V. 4) the chief of sages, the authors to the Mantras.

Note:—Words having the sense of comparatives are used with the Ablative and sometimes with the Instrumental; @deva[अस्मात्वलं हरितः] or @deva[अधिकः] this person is superior or inferior to him in strength; similarly @deva[देवदत्तो यशोनात्युङ्गः दीर्घहा वा]; @deva[को धर्म् सततरो भव्या] who will have a happier end than I? The word @deva[सततरै] is used with the Locative as in the Locative or the Instrumental; @deva[धनहीनं नारायणविकानपि सोऽभवत्] he was more (i. e. dearer) to them than their sons; @deva[तेषुमुस्याजनं वार्षः युद्धति द्विमुत्रे क्रमेः] they passed five months and twelve nights more than (those years); @deva[कादचिदुङुअक् युत्रः] A Kadru is larger than a Kūdava.
