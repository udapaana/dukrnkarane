An adjective may be compounded with the noun it qualifies in most cases[^1] as @deva[नीलं च उत्पलं च नीलोत्पलं], a blue lotus &c.; @deva[कृष्णश्चासौ सर्पश्च कृष्णसर्पः] where the comp. is necessary; but @deva[कामदत्तो रामः] Such compounds are called @deva[विशेषणपूर्वपदकर्मधारयs].

As a general rule in such compounds the qualifying word is placed first; but there are several exceptions which are given below.

**(a)**[^2] The qualified word is placed first and is always of the mas. gender if it denotes a class or species, when compounded with certain adjectives; e. g. @deva[युथजवती]: 'a young female elephant', @deva[अग्नितोक]: 'a small fire', @deva[उदविकतिपय्] 'butter-milk mixed with a little water', @deva[गोप्रथै]: 'a cow that has brought forth a calf for the first time', @deva[गोवेद्य]: 'a milch-cow (@deva[नवप्रसूतिका])'; @deva[गोवध्या] 'a barren cow', @deva[गोवेहत] ( @deva[गर्भपातिनी गौ:] ), @deva[गोवत्सकपणी] 'a cow whose calf is one year old,' @deva[कटप्रौषिध्य]: 'an agnihotrin Brāhmaṇa of the Kaṭha branch of the Yajurveda,' @deva[कटाध्यापक]: 'a teacher of the Kaṭha branch,' @deva[गो-मतल्लिका-मध्यल्लिका-प्रकाऽइ][^3] (also mas. according to some) @deva[-उद्द]: (@deva[गवोद्द] )@deva[-मह्यन] 'an excellent cow'. The last mentioned five words, all of which denote excellence, retain their own gender invariably; as @deva[ब्राह्मणमतल्लिका] 'an eminent Brāhmaṇa' &c.; but @deva[कुमारी मतल्लिका], as no class is meant here.

**(b)**[^4] The words @deva[कहार], @deva[खञ्ज], @deva[सोढ] lame, @deva[काण], @deva[कुण्ठ] blunt, dull, @deva[खल्वति] bald-headed, @deva[गौर], @deva[वृद्ध], @deva[भिक्षुक] @deva[पिङ्ग], @deva[पिङ्गल], @deva[तड], @deva[जरठ] hard, stiff, @deva[वधिर], @deva[कुब्ज] and @deva[बधिर], may optionally precede the words in apposition to them; as @deva[जैमिनिकहार]: or @deva[कहारजैमिनि]: 'Jaimini who is turned tawny on account of his having practised penance in the sun,' &c.

**(c)**[^5] Words expressive of the persons or things condemned or reproached are placed first; as @deva[वैयाकरणवक्षुर्वि]: 'a bad grammarian,' 'one who has forgotten his grammar and therefore can make no use of his knowledge,' ( @deva[वृष: सत् प्रज्ञं विस्मारिविद् सै क्षुवयति अध्यासव्यैयंति एकह्व्ये] Tatv. ); so @deva[मीमांसकदुर्ष्क]: 'a mīmāṃsaka who is a sceptic' or 'an unbeliever'; except in the case of the words @deva[पाप], @deva[अपक] and @deva[किम्]; as @deva[पापनापित]: 'a bad barber,' @deva[अपककुलाल]: 'a mean, silly potter,' @deva[कुसित: राजा किराज]:, @deva[किबसा] ( @deva[कुसित: सखा] ), &c.

**(d)**[^6] Words in apposition to @deva[इन्द्राक्ष], @deva[नाय] and @deva[कुजर] are also placed first; e. g. @deva[इषपद्नारक]: 'an eminent king,' @deva[नायसकुजर], @deva[इषवनाथ]:, &c.

**(e)**[^7] @deva[कदर] and @deva[कतम] are compounded only when they ask a question with reference to a class ( @deva[जाति] family), and are placed first; e.g. @deva[कदर-कतम-कठ]: 'which Brāhmaṇa of the Kaṭha branch?' @deva[कदर-कतम-कलाप]: 'which Brāhmaṇa of the Kalāpa branch?' But @deva[कतर: सुत]: 'which son'!

**(f)**[^8] The word @deva[कुमार] ( and also @deva[कुमारी] changed to @deva[कुमार] ) is placed first when compounded with one of the words @deva[भ्रमणा], @deva[प्रव्रजिता], @deva[कुलटा], @deva[गर्भिणी], @deva[तापसी], @deva[दासी], @deva[अध्यापक], @deva[पण्डित], @deva[पड्], @deva[इध्], @deva[दुबल], @deva[चपल] and @deva[निपुण]; as @deva[कुमारभ्रमणा] 'a female mendicant,' @deva[कुमारप्रव्रजिता] 'a young female ascetic'; @deva[कुमारइध्:द्री] 'a delicate boy or girl, @deva[कुमारगर्भिणी], @deva[कुमारअध्यापक]:, &c.

**(g)**[^9] The words @deva[एक], @deva[सर्व], @deva[जरद्], @deva[पराण], @deva[नव] and @deva[कैवल]: and the words @deva[एकं], @deva[अपर], @deva[चरम], @deva[जघन्य] 'hindmost,' @deva[समान], @deva[महत्], @deva[मध्यम] and @deva[वीर] are always placed first in a Karmadhāraya compound. @deva[अपर] when followed by @deva[कर्म] is changed to @deva[अश्म]: as @deva[एकनाथः] 'sole master or lord,' @deva[वृद्धकालः]:, @deva[जरद्वैयापिकः]:, 'an old logician,' @deva[पुराणकौ-सांख्यः]:, 'old mimamsā philosophers,' @deva[नवपाचकाः]:, @deva[पूर्वबैयाकरणाः]:, 'the old grammarians,' @deva[अपराङ्गयापकः]: @deva[अपरक्षातों] @deva[अयंक्ष पक्षाक्षै]: 'the hinder part or side of the body;' @deva[चरमराजः]: the last king, @deva[समानतधिकरणम्] 'being in the same category or predicament'; @deva[वीरैकः]: 'a sole warrior i. e. an eminent warrior', &c. The form @deva[एकवीर] ( found used by great writers ) also may be defended.[^10]

**(h)**[^11] The words @deva[सत्], @deva[महत्], @deva[परम्], @deva[उत्तम] and @deva[उच्छृष्ट] are compounded with another noun when they are used as terms of praise or excellence; @deva[सद्वैद्यः]: 'an excellent physician' @deva[महाविद्याकरणः]:. &c., but @deva[उच्छ्रृती गौः]: where @deva[उच्छृष्ट] means 'uplifted, or 'drawn out of '
