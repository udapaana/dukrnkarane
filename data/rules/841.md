## Ablative with अपि, परिङ्, आ, प्रति

The ablative is used with—

(a) The prepositions @deva[अपि] and @deva[परिङ्] meaning 'away from, without' and meaning 'as far as' or 'including, comprehending'; @deva[अपि हरेः सत्तरः] Saṃsāra exists outside Hari; @deva[अपि त्रिगर्ताद् व्यांड देवः] it rained everywhere, except in the country of Trigarṭa. Similarly @deva[परिङ् हरेः सीमायाः परिङ् प्रदेशां व्यः] (Vop.) &c. @deva[आकाशं संकर्षण आकषत् ब्रह्म] Brahman pervades every thing; @deva[आ यत्तो शत्रोर्णो] until the learned are satisfied.

(b) @deva[प्रति] meaning 'the representative of, in exchange for or giving in return for;' @deva[प्रद्युम्नः कृष्णात् प्रतिनुच्यते] (Sid. Kau.) Pradyumna is the representative of Kṛṣṇa, @deva[निरभात् प्रतिनच्छति मायात्] gives māṣhas in return for sesamum.[^1]

[^1]: Pāṇ. I. 4. 88-89, 92; II. 3. 10-11
