A noun in the Locative is compounded—

**(a)**[^1] with @deva[शौण्ड], @deva[धूर्त], @deva[कितव] a rogue, @deva[प्रवीण], @deva[संवीत] adorned, invested with, @deva[अन्तर], @deva[आधीन], @deva[पट], @deva[पण्डित], @deva[कुशल], @deva[चतुर], @deva[निपुण], @deva[सिद्ध], @deva[शुष्क], @deva[पक्व], and @deva[बन्ध]; as @deva[अक्षेषु शौण्डः अक्षशौण्डः] skilled in dice; @deva[ईश्वरे आधीन ईश्वराधीनः] dependent on God (when compounded the affix @deva[स] i. e. @deva[ईन] is compulsory; so the compounded form is always with @deva[अधीन]); @deva[आतपशुष्कः] dried in the sun; @deva[स्थालीपक्वः] cooked in a pot; @deva[वक्रबन्धः] a kind of poetic composition; &c.

**(b)**[^2] with words meaning a 'crow' when contempt is implied; @deva[तीर्थे काकं इव तीर्थकाकः] said in contempt of one who is very greedy; so @deva[तीर्थकाकः]; similarly @deva[नगरकाकः]:—@deva[वायसः] &c. &c. There are a few Loc. Tat. compounds given in the list @deva[धावेसमिताद्] having a similar sense; e. g. @deva[कूपे मण्डूक इव कूपमण्डूकः] a well-abiding frog i. e. one who knows nothing of the world outside the pale of his own village; so @deva[कूप-उदपान-मण्डूकः]; @deva[उदुम्बरष्टाभिः-मत्स्याकः] lit. an insect in the fruit of the Udumbara tree; metaphorically it means the same as @deva[कूपमण्डूकः]; @deva[कूपकेश्यगः] &c. In some cases there is an aluk of the Loc., as @deva[गेहेशूरः] 'a boasting coward', @deva[गेहेख्यातः] 'a braggadocio', @deva[गेहेनद्री] 'a househero', 'a carpet knight', @deva[ग्रासेकुशलः] 'clever in eating only' i. e. 'a good-for-nothing fellow'; @deva[पायसेपटुः]; @deva[गोष्ठेयुधः]; @deva[गोष्ठेविजयी]; @deva[गेहेमृगः], &c.

**Note:**—Words of this class do not enter into further combination with other words.

**(c)**[^3] with another, when they both imply a name (@deva[संज्ञा]); there is an aluk of the Loc. term. when the noun ends in a consonant or @deva[अ]; as @deva[अरण्येतिलकः] lit. 'wild sesamum yielding no oil, hence anything not answering to one's expectations:' similarly @deva[वनेकुरुन्टका]; @deva[स्थलिसारः] 'a bamboo' (also @deva[स्थकुसारः]; vide Si. IV. 61.). These compounds are nitya (obligatory) as remarked by Bhaṭṭoji (@deva[वाच्येन संज्ञायावगमादित्यतमनित्यम्]).

**(d)** with potential p. participles in @deva[य] implying an obligation; @deva[मार्गेदेयं] (@deva[कपर्द]); @deva[पुरोहितेयं] (@deva[साम]); the aluk by @ref[217] (b) below.

**(e)**[^4] with p. p. participles when it expresses a part of the day or night; @deva[तत्र] may take the place of the Loc.; as @deva[पूर्वाह्णे कृत्ते पूर्वाह्णकृतम्]; @deva[अपराह्नकृतम्] @deva[तत्रकृतम्] &c; but @deva[अह्नि दृष्टं]; and with the p. p. participles when reproach is implied, with an aluk of the Loc; as '@deva[अवसन्नेनकुलरिपुते तवैतत्]' 'this thy action is similar to that of a mungoose on the hot ground.' @deva[अवसन्नेनकुलरिपुते] is used to express the inconstancy of a person.
