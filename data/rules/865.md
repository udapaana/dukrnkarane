## Locative/Genitive with स्वामिन्, स्वार and Related Words

The Locative or the Genitive is used with the words @deva[स्वामिन्] a master, @deva[स्वार], @deva[अधिपति] a lord, @deva[वारिस्] a heir, @deva[साधन], @deva[प्रतिबलि] a ball, and @deva[निभव] born for; @deva[गोषु गवां वा स्वामी] the master of cows; @deva[गोषु] or @deva[ग्रामेषु] or @deva[अधिपतिः] owns the ball from the ten kings; @deva[ग्रामेषु आधिपत्यः] the lord of villages, similarly @deva[तेषामस्य निवदने वा वासुदेवै], @deva[निभवर्णों यथादित्यस्य वा शाकुंतः], @deva[दानानं दानस्य वा वासुकः] (surety for appearance in a court); @deva[गोषु गवां वा प्रतिबलि]. @deva[गोपः] a cowherd is born for cows.[^1]
