The root @deva[अर्ह्] 'to deserve' when used (in the second person) in combination with the infinitive expresses 'a request, a respectful entreaty,' and is used the same way in English 'I pray, be pleased, &c.;' @deva[अग्निं शाप्तुमर्हसि] (Meg. 55) please put out the fire; @deva[न चेदस्ति न शाप्तुमर्हसि] (Kum V. 40) if you have nothing to conceal from me, please answer me: @deva[विद्यामय वक्तुमर्हसि सौरभेयम्] (Rag. V. 25) you be pleased to tell these days; @deva[किं अनुमन्तव्यम्] one &c. Sometimes it is equivalent to a gentle command: @deva[प्रियाः प्रशाप्तुमर्हसि] (Rag. I. 89) you ought to please her; @deva[न तं चार्थुमर्हसि] you ought not to bewail him. In the third person and under the same circumstances it expresses power or ability and may be translated by 'can;' @deva[शापो हि समर्थो कोऽन्यो सोढुमर्हति फलादपि] Māh. Bhar. IV. 58. 27. @deva[वेदां तत्त्वविशेषेण को निश्चेतुमर्हति] Ibid I. 1. 243.
