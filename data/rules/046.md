When @deva[अः] at the end of a word is followed by soft letter, it is changed to @deva[ओ];[^1] e.g. @deva[रामः] + @deva[गच्छति] = @deva[रामो गच्छति] Rāma goes.

**(a)** When @deva[अः] is followed by a soft guttural or labial it is optionally changed to @deva[ओ];[^2] e.g. @deva[देवः] + @deva[गच्छति] = @deva[देवो गच्छति] and @deva[देव गच्छति] the deity goes; @deva[रामः] + @deva[बलवान्] = @deva[रामो बलवान्] and @deva[राम बलवान्] Rāma is strong.

**(b)** Before a sibilant (i. e. @deva[श्], @deva[ष्], @deva[स्]), @deva[अः] at the end of a word is changed into the Visarga or the sibilant which follows it;[^3] @deva[रामः] + @deva[शेते] = @deva[रामः शेते] and @deva[रामश्शेते] Rāma sleeps; @deva[हरिः] + @deva[षष्ठः] = @deva[हरिः षष्ठः] and @deva[हरिष्षष्ठः] Hari the sixth; @deva[रामः] + @deva[सेवते] = @deva[रामः सेवते] and @deva[रामस्सेवते] Rāma worships. Formerly there was only this option of changing the Visarga into the sibilant which followed, or of letting it remain unchanged. But later grammarians have added a third change, viz., of changing the Visarga optionally into the very breath-sound itself; and this has now been generally current in most parts of India; e.g. @deva[रामः शेते], @deva[रामश्शेते] and @deva[राम शेते] Rāma sleeps; so @deva[हरिः षष्ठः], @deva[हरिष्षष्ठः] and @deva[हरि षष्ठः], @deva[रामः सेवते], @deva[रामस्सेवते] and @deva[राम सेवते].

**(c)** When @deva[इ] or @deva[उ], except @deva[अ], precedes Visarga, it is changed to @deva[ष्] before @deva[क्] or @deva[प्];[^4] e.g. @deva[हरिः] + @deva[करोति] = @deva[हरिष्करोति] Hari makes; @deva[साधुः] + @deva[पचति] = @deva[साधुष्पचति] a good man cooks; but @deva[रामः] + @deva[करोति] = @deva[रामः करोति] Rāma makes, as @deva[अ] precedes the Visarga.

**(d)** When the preceding vowel is @deva[अ], it is optionally changed to @deva[स्] in place of Visarga;[^5] @deva[रामः] + @deva[करोति] = @deva[रामः करोति] and @deva[रामस्करोति]; but when the preceding vowel is @deva[आ], no such change takes place; @deva[रामाः] + @deva[पतन्ति] = @deva[रामाः पतन्ति] Rāmas fall.

**(e)** When the Visarga at the end of a word is preceded by @deva[अ] or @deva[आ], and followed by any other vowel except @deva[अ], it is changed to @deva[र्] by the best authors; other writers substitute, however, it by @deva[य्], @deva[व्] or @deva[ल्] or leave the Visarga altogether, though irregularly;[^6] e.g. @deva[देवाः] + @deva[उत्तिष्ठन्ति] = @deva[देवा उत्तिष्ठन्ति] or @deva[देवा रुत्तिष्ठन्ति] the gods rise; @deva[रामः] + @deva[इच्छति] = @deva[रामा इच्छति] or @deva[राम इच्छति] or @deva[रामय्इच्छति] or @deva[रामरिच्छति] or @deva[रामलिच्छति] or @deva[रामविच्छति]; but @deva[रामा रिच्छति] is the best. (See Pāṇ. VIII. 3. 15 and Siddh. Kaum. on Pāṇ. VIII. 3. 19).

**(f)** When such a Visarga is followed by @deva[अ], it may be entirely dropped;[^7] e.g. @deva[रामः] + @deva[अस्ति] = @deva[रामोऽस्ति] (by applying preceding sub-rule c) or @deva[रामाऽस्ति] or @deva[राम अस्ति] by this (f), Rāma is.

**(g)** Before @deva[र्], the Visarga is dropped;[^8] @deva[रामः] + @deva[रजते] = @deva[रामो राजते] or @deva[राम राजते] Rāma shines.
