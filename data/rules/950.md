[^1] The Potential Mood expresses the sense of @deva[विधि] (command,, directing a subordinate, &c.), @deva[निमन्त्रण] (pressing invitation), @deva[आमन्त्रण] (giving permission), @deva[अधिकार] (telling one to attend to an honorary office or duty), @deva[सम्प्रश्न] (courteously asking a person a question), and prayer; @deva[याज्ञिकः] go on should perform a sacrifice; @deva[त्वं ग्रामं गच्छेत्] go to the village; @deva[इह भवान् प्रभुञ्जीत] your honour should take food here; @deva[इहासीत भवान्] you may sit here; @deva[पुत्रं पाठयेदधार] you may teach my son (as an honorary duty); @deva[किं मे भैद्रपादेनु पठ भक्तम्] I see; @deva[अहं], oh! shall I learn the Vedas or logic? @deva[को स्यात् कुरोमि] good Sir, can I get food here? i. e. will you kindly give me food? (All these senses are optionally expressed by the Imperative also).

(a) [^2] In the case of the first two senses viz. @deva[विधि] and @deva[निमन्त्रण] and in that of 'proper time', the potential participle may also be used for the Potential; @deva[यज्ञस्य पाटयिष्यन्], &c.

[^1]: @deva[विधिनिमन्त्रणामन्त्रणाधिकारप्रश्नप्रार्थनेषु लिङ्] । Pāṇ. III. 3. 161
