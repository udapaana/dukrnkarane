## Adjective Agreement with Multiple Substantives

When an adjective qualifies two or more substantives it agrees with them in their combined number; when the substantives differ in gender, the adjective will be masculine when the substantives are masculine and feminine; and neuter when they are masculine, feminine and neuter @deva[राजा राज्ञी च स्तुत्यचरितौ स्तः] the king and his queen are of laudable conduct; @deva[धर्मः कामश्च दर्पश्च हर्षः क्रोधः सुखं वयः । अर्थादेतानि सर्वाणि प्रवर्तन्ते न संशयः] fulfilment of duty, satisfaction of desires, pride, anger, happiness and long life; all these proceed undoubtedly from wealth.

(a) Sometimes the adjective takes the gender of the majority of the substantives: @deva[वृद्धौ च मातापितरौ साध्वी भार्या सुतः शिशुः । अप्यकार्यशतं कृत्वा भर्तव्या मनुरब्रवीत् ॥] aged parents, a good wife and a young son should be maintained even by doing a hundred foul deeds; so has Manu spoken (laid down).

(b) And sometimes it takes the gender and number of the substantive nearest to it when the particle @deva[च] is used; @deva[शोकः कलहः कण्डूः सेव्यमाना च वर्धते] dejection, quarrel and an itching sensation prevail all the more they are attended to; @deva[येन कृतिनो वयं च भुवनानि च (कृतीनि )] by whose valour we have become happy and so have the three worlds.
