Instances of the Aluk of the Gen. The noun in the Genitive may retain its case ending—[^1]

**(a)** when reproach is to be implied, as @deva[चोरस्यकुलम्], but @deva[ब्राह्मणकुलम्]. Also when @deva[देवानां] is followed by @deva[प्रिय] and the whole means a fool; otherwise @deva[देवप्रियः].

**(b)** when the words @deva[वाच्], @deva[दिश्] and @deva[पक्षत्] are followed by @deva[पति], @deva[दण्ड] and @deva[हर] respectively; as @deva[वाचस्पतिः] 'skilful speech,' @deva[दिशोदण्डः]: 'a particular appearance of stars in the shape of a staff in the sky;' @deva[पक्षतोहरः] 'one who robs another in his very presence while he is looking on,' hence 'a goldsmith, a rogue.'

**(c)** in the following cases:—@deva[विशेषसः]: 'a king of Kāśhī,' @deva[दिवस्पतिः] 'Indra,' @deva[वाचस्पतिः] lord of speech, Brihaspati; @deva[शुनःशेपः], @deva[शुनःपुच्छः] and @deva[शुनोलाङ्गूलः]: 'sons of Ajigarta.'

**(d)** and optionally when followed by @deva[पुत्र] and censure is implied;[^2] @deva[दास्याः पुत्रः] or @deva[दासीपुत्रः] a bastard, a knave; but @deva[ब्राह्मणीपुत्रः].

**(e)** When a word ending in @deva[तृ] is compounded with another and there is the relation by blood or some kind of learning, the aluk is necessary; and optional when it is followed by @deva[स्वसृ] and @deva[पति]. The initial @deva[स] of @deva[स्वसृ] is optionally changed to @deva[ष] when preceded by @deva[मातृ] or @deva[पितृ] and when there is aluk, and necessarily when there is no aluk in a compound; e. g. @deva[होतुःपुत्रः], @deva[होतृपुत्रोपवासी], a pupil of the hotri; @deva[मातुःस्वसा], or @deva[मातुःषसा] and @deva[मातृष्वसा]; so @deva[पितुःस्वसा], &c. (but @deva[मातः स्वसा], @deva[पितः स्वसा] when uncompounded); @deva[स्वसृपतिः] or @deva[स्वसुः-पतिः]; but @deva[होतृपूजनम्].
