## Irregular Perfect Participles from ऋ-Ending Roots

Roots ending in @deva[ऋ] (@deva[ऋ] and @deva[लृ] included) form their Perfect Participle irregularly.

### Parasmaipada Formation

@deva[वत्] is added to the root, which then undergoes the changes given in @ref[394], and in @ref[537] (a).

### Ātmanepada Formation

When a root is Ātmanepada it is first reduplicated and @deva[आन] afterwards added, the final @deva[ऋ] changing as before.

### Examples

@deva[कृ] + @deva[वत्] = @deva[कृतुवत्], by reduplication @deva[चिकृतुवत्]

@deva[कृ]—by reduplication @deva[चकृ] + @deva[आन] = @deva[चक्रुआन]

Similarly:

@deva[कृ]—@deva[कृतुवत्], @deva[चिकृतुवत्]

@deva[तृ]—@deva[तितीर्ष], @deva[तितीर्ष्वात्]

@deva[धु]—@deva[तिथिवत्र्], @deva[तथितिवत्], @deva[तीथुरिवत्], @deva[पश्यावत्], etc.
