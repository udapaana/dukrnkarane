## Dative Case: Sampradāna (Indirect Object)

The primary sense of the Dative case is Sampradāna (@deva[संप्रदानं]). The indirect object of the root @deva[दा] is called Sampradāna; also the person or thing with reference to whom or which an action is done; @deva[हस्तं वरयति हरिं देवः] Give water to a Brāhmaṇa; @deva[शत्रवं संगाते] 'prepares for battle;' @deva[न ब्राह्मणं भर्तो दाटुति] 'The Śudra should not be instructed in the Veda, &c.'[^1]

Note: But in the case of the root @deva[यच्] the proper object is put in the Instrumental case and the indirect object in the Accusative; @deva[यड्णे रुद्रं यजते] 'he sacrifices a bull to Rudra.'

Note:—Though the root @deva[दा] ought to govern the Dat. of the indirect object, it is sometimes found used with the Gen. or the Loc. @deva[यस्य त्यत्रनों दवने वै वदरङयाट् सर्वकैचरु] 'Oh sky-wanderer, we will give rain at the prosperous kingdom of the Śibis;' @deva[मस्यवं रामं मधिविरी दाद्यारिकतुति] 'thou who art thinking of bestowing the earth upon Rāma,' &c.

[^1]: Pāṇ. II. 3. 13, I. 4. 32 and Vārtikas
