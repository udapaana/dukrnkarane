@deva[म्] at the end of a word is changed into an Anusvāra when followed by a consonant;[^1] e.g. @deva[हरिम्] + @deva[वन्दे] = @deva[हरिं वन्दे] I salute Hari. But @deva[गम्] + @deva[य्] + @deva[ते] = @deva[गम्यते] as @deva[म्] is not at the end of a word. The word @deva[सञ्जिह्वः] is an exception (see Pāṇ. VIII. 3. 23).

**(a)** @deva[म्] and @deva[न्], not at the end of a pada, are turned into an Anusvāra when followed by a consonant except a nasal or a semivowel or @deva[ह्]; @deva[आक्रम्] + @deva[स्यते] = @deva[आक्रंस्यते] he will overcome; @deva[यशस्] + @deva[सि] = @deva[यशांसि] (nom. pl. of @deva[यशस्] fame); but @deva[मन्यते] he thinks, as @deva[म्] is followed here by @deva[य] which is a semi-vowel; @deva[ग्रामम्] + @deva[गच्छति] = @deva[ग्रामं गच्छति] as @deva[म्] is at the end of a pada (grammatical form).

**(b)** This change is optional in the case of @deva[म्] when @deva[ह] followed by @deva[य] comes after it; e.g. @deva[किम्] + @deva[ह्रयति] = @deva[किंह्रयति] and @deva[किम्ह्रयति] what does he shake?; but if this @deva[ह] be followed by @deva[न], and if by @deva[य], @deva[ञ], @deva[न्], the nasalised @deva[य्], @deva[ञ्], @deva[न्], are optionally substituted for @deva[म्] e.g. @deva[किम्] + @deva[ह्नुते] = @deva[किंह्नुते] or @deva[किञ्ह्नुते] what does he conceal? @deva[किम्] + @deva[ह्यः] = @deva[किंह्यः] and @deva[किञ्ह्यः] what! yesterday! so @deva[किंह्रयति], @deva[किञ्ह्रयति]; @deva[किंह्रादयति] and @deva[किञ्ह्रादयति]; but @deva[अहम्] + @deva[आगतः] = @deva[अहमागतः] I have come.

---

[^1]: @deva[मोऽनुस्वारः। नश्चापदान्तस्य झलि] — Pāṇ. VIII. 3. 23, 24 and Pāṇ. VIII. 3. 27, I. 3. 10. (see sec. b).
