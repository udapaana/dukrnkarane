## Exceptions: When Genitive is NOT Used

The Genitive is not used with present participles except that of @deva[द्विष्], verbal derivatives ending in @deva[क्त] and @deva[उक्] except that derived from @deva[कृत्], verbal indeclinables, past participles, passive and active, nouns formed with the affix @deva[तुमुन्] (see p. 456) and with such as mean 'in the habit of, or having the properties of or doing any thing well;' @deva[कर्तुनों कर्तुनं] or @deva[कर्तुः] but @deva[मुरै दैत्यस्य वा हिद्धु हरिः] Hari, the enemy of Mura; @deva[हरै विद्वान्तुः] desirous of seeing Hari; @deva[मुरै अत्याहरिः], @deva[दैत्यानां हातको हरिः] Hari is the killer of demons; @deva[कृत्युः करणीयम्]; @deva[अविद्यस्य युधै तुम् युङ्गु न पश्यु द्यु दैत्यानां]; @deva[दैत्यानां हवनकः हकन्तुः प्रयच्छो हरेण] worldly life goes astray with Hari; @deva[आत्मनान् अङ्कतृवरीष्णुः] in habit of decorating oneself; @deva[अर्थं विद्वान्] a habitual beggar; @deva[कर्तुनो कटं] one who prepares a mat; @deva[फलै पुननम्] 'killing the fruit' means the fruit by terminations @deva[अक्] showing futurity and @deva[हन्] showing necessary payment; @deva[हरिरै वन्दको यात्यति] he goes desiring to see Hari; @deva[शतं दार्णों] one who has to pay a hundred (rupees.).[^1]
