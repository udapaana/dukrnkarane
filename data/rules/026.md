No sandhi is possible in the case of[^1]—

**I.** What are called the @deva[प्रगृह्य] (@[Pragṛhya]) exceptions; viz.—

**(a)** When a dual form (whether of a noun, pronoun or verb) ends in @deva[ई], @deva[ऊ], or @deva[ए];[^2] e.g. @deva[हरी एतौ]; @deva[विष्णू इमौ]; @deva[गुरवे अयं]; @deva[पश्यैते इमौ].

**(b)** When @deva[ई] or @deva[ऊ] follows the @deva[इ] of the pronoun @deva[अदस्][^3] e.g., @deva[अमी ईशाः] these lords; @deva[अमू आसाते] the two sit down.

**Obs.** The @deva[इ] of the Vedic forms @deva[अस्मे] and @deva[युष्मे] also does not blend with a following vowel (@deva[सेध] Pāṇ. I. 1. 13.); e.g. @deva[अस्मे इन्द्राबृहस्पती] &c. Ṛg. IV. 49. 4. Similarly a Vedic word having the sense of the loc. (but not ending in the termination of the loc.) remains unaffected by a following vowel; @deva[सोमो गौरी अविष्टितः] &c. Ṛg. X. 12. 3. where @deva[गौरी] stands for @deva[गौर्याम्] the loc. term, being dropped by @deva[पुङ्ग वुञ्च] Pāṇ. VII. 1. 30.

**(c)** Particles consisting of a single vowel, except @deva[आ] meaning 'a little, or a limit' exclusive or inclusive or used as a preposition, when followed by a vowel,[^4] e.g. @deva[इ इन्द्र] Oh Indra. @deva[उ उद्येध], @deva[आ पूर्वं न मन्यसे] ah, you think so; but @deva[आ] + @deva[उष्णम्] = @deva[औष्णम्] (a little warm) &c.

**(d)** When an indeclinable ends in @deva[ओ]; e.g. @deva[अहो ईशाः]; the final @deva[ओ] of a noun in the vocative case optionally combines when followed by @deva[इति]; e.g. @deva[विष्णो इति] = @deva[विष्णविति], and @deva[विष्णो इति]; also @deva[विष्ण इति] (see @ref[24] a.).

**N.B.** All these vowels which do not blend with others, and sometimes the words in which they occur are technically called @deva[प्रगृह्य].

**II.** Protracted and pluta vowels; e.g. @deva[पश्य छागा३ अत्र गोष्ठगताः] Come, Kṛṣṇa; here is the cow grazing.

A vowel becomes pluta under the following circumstances:[^5]

**(1)** The last vowel of a sentence uttered in reply to a salutation from a male person other than a Śūdra when the name or the family is actually mentioned, e.g. @deva[धो आयुष्मानेधि देवदत्त] live long, O Devadatta, which is said in reply to "@deva[अभिवादये देवदत्तोऽहम्]" I, Devadatta, bow to you; but the last @deva[अ] in "@deva[धो आयुष्मती भव भार्ये]" or in @deva[आयुष्मानेधि] is not pluta; for in the 1st we have the name of a female and in the 2nd no name at all.

But if the sentence end in the word @deva[भोः] or in the name of a Kṣatriya or Vaiśya the last vowel becomes optionally pluta e.g. @deva[आयुष्मान् एधि भोः] or @deva[भो३]; @deva[आयुष्मानेधि अर्धवर्धन] or @deva[र्ध३]; @deva[आयुष्मानेधि अर्धवर्षिष्ठ] or @deva[ष्ठ३].

**(2)** The final vowel of a sentence addressed from a distance to a person, and that of the particles @deva[है] and @deva[हे] used in such a sentence; e.g. @deva[आगच्छ देवदत्त३]; @deva[हे रामा३]; @deva[है३] @deva[हे३].
