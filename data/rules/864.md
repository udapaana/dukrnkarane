## Locative Case: Adhikaraṇa (Place and Time)

## THE LOCATIVE CASE

The place where an action takes place with reference to the subject or object is called Adhikaraṇa (@deva[अधिकरण]) and is put in the Locative case; @deva[स्वापति निशायाम्] (Bham. 1. 60); @deva[भक्तो नन्दनकानने] (Ibid 64); @deva[सत्त्रे ओदनम् पचते] cooks food in a cooking utensil; @deva[कर्णे कथयति] tells (something) into the ear; @deva[यदा इष्टे अपि], &c. The Locative also denotes the time when an action takes place; @deva[तास्माच् विबोध्यमानः कालं दिवसैकः] (Kum. II. 1.) the gods being harassed at that time; @deva[दिवसान्ते निद्रायाम् गन्तृदुङ्] (Rag. II. 15.)[^1]

(a) Verbal derivatives in @deva[हन्] and having the sense of the P. P. participle govern the Locative of their object; @deva[अर्थितिरु]...[^2]
