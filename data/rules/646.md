## Non-Desiderative Uses of य Nominal Verbs

This form of the Nominal verb has not always a desiderative meaning.

### (a) Treating or Considering Like

The affix @deva[य] is added in the sense of 'treating or considering like':

@deva[पुत्रीयति छात्रम्] 'he treats the pupil as a son'

@deva[विष्णुयति विप्रं] 'he treats the Brāhmaṇa like Vishṇu'

@deva[कुटीयति कुटिकया भिक्षुः] 'the beggar considers his hut a palace'

@deva[कुटीयति प्रासादं नृपः] 'the king in his palace considers himself to be in a hut'

### (b) Specific Uses

It is added to @deva[नमस्], @deva[वरिवस्] and @deva[चिच्] in the senses of 'adoring', 'serving' and 'striking with wonder' respectively:

@deva[नमस्यति देवान्] 'he adores the gods'

@deva[वरिवस्यति गुरुं] 'he serves the preceptor'

@deva[चिच्यीयते लोकान्] 'he strikes the people with wonder'

It is also added to @deva[तपस्] in the sense of 'practising': @deva[तपस्यति]
