## Dative with ऋण् (Debt) and स्पृह् (Desire)

The creditor (or the person to whom something is due) in the case of the root @deva[ऋण्] 'to owe;' and the person or the thing desired in the case of @deva[स्पृह्], are put in the Dative case; @deva[दुययसंचनं तं अर्पयदि मे] 'you owe me two sprinklings of trees' (Ś'ak.); @deva[शकत्युयु अर्पयति कोयं हरिः] (Sid. Kau.) @deva[तस्यं स्पृहयमाणाती] 'he is longing for her (Bhaṭṭi VIII. 15.);' @deva[पुयसे स्पृहः सदयति] 'he longs for trees;' but @deva[यरुययगुम् स्पृहयति] where no longing is implied.[^1]
