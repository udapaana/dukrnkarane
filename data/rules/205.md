A noun in the Instrumental may be compounded—

**(a)**[^1] with another expressive of an effect produced by what is denoted by the noun in the Instrumental case, and with @deva[अर्थं], as @deva[शस्त्रेण भिन्नः शस्त्रभिन्नः] severed by means of a knife; @deva[धान्येन अर्थः धान्यार्थः] wealth obtained by means of grain.

**(b)**[^2] with verbal derivatives when the Instrumental has the sense of the agent or the instrument or means of the action; as @deva[हरिणा त्रातः हरित्रातः] saved by Hari; @deva[नखैर्भिन्नः नखभिन्नः] torn with the nails &c.

**(c)** with the words[^3] @deva[पूर्व], @deva[सदृश], @deva[सम], @deva[तुल्य] and words having the sense of @deva[कृत], @deva[कल्प], @deva[निपुण], @deva[मिश्र], @deva[शीतपूर्व] and @deva[अवर]; as @deva[मात्रेण पूर्वः मातृपूर्वः]; @deva[मात्रा सदृशः मातृसदृशः] like his mother; @deva[पितृसमः] equal to his father; @deva[सरेण ऊनं मासूनम्]; so @deva[मासव्यकल] ( less by one māsha, a particular weight ); @deva[वाचा कलहः वाक्कलहः] a quarrel ( in words ); @deva[आचारनिपुणः]; @deva[युद्धभिक्षः], @deva[आचारक्षीणः] 'thin by the discharge of religious duties' @deva[मासेन अवरः मासावरः] 'younger by a month.'

**(d)**[^4] With another noun signifying an article of food or something eatable, the noun in the Instrumental case denoting some kind of condiment used to season it or another article of food mixed with it; as @deva[दध्ना ओदनः दध्योदनः] rice mixed with curds; @deva[गुडेन भक्तः गुडभक्तः] fried rice mixed with treacle.

**(e)** Sometimes @deva[स्वयं] may take the place of the Instrumental as @deva[स्वयंकृतः] done by one's self.
