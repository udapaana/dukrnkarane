[^1] There is a peculiar use of the Imperative which ought to be noticed. The Imperative second person sigular is repeated when frequency of an act is indicated and the whole used with the root in any tense; @deva[याहि याहि एति गति] (Sid. Kau.) he goes every now and then; @deva[पकाहि पकाहि पाकं पकति] (Sid Kau) he @deva[भिदट्]; @deva[अध्ययनमधीयानै पठेत] be studies steadily. The Imperative second person is also used when several acts are described as done by the same person; @deva[शकुन्च फिर, पानः सुबहारारति] (Sid. Kau.) he takes his meal, now eating fruit, now eating fried rice; similarly @deva[अशकुनं वदै दक्षिणामवहामरते] (Sid. Kau.).
