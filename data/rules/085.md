Here simply the terminations should be added and Sandhi rules observed.

@deva[से] (@deva[सेह् इना कामेन वर्त्तेऽस्सौ])

| | Sing. | Dual | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| N. | @deva[सेः] | @deva[सयौ] | @deva[सयः] |
| V. | @deva[से][^1] | ,, | ,, |
| A. | @deva[सयम्] | ,, | ,, |
| I. | @deva[सया] | @deva[सेभ्याम्] | @deva[सेभिः] |
| D. | @deva[सये] | ,, | @deva[सेभ्यः] |
| Ab. | @deva[सेः] | ,, | ,, |
| G. | ,, | @deva[सयोः] | @deva[सयाम्] |
| L. | @deva[सयि] | ,, | @deva[सेषु] |

Decline similarly @deva[स्थते] (@deva[स्थतः तत्]) one who has thought of Kāma.

@deva[रै] m. f. wealth.

|---|---|---|---|
| N. V. | @deva[राः] | @deva[रायौ] | @deva[रायः] |
| A. | @deva[रायम्] | ,, | ,, |
| I. | @deva[राया] | @deva[राभ्याम्] | @deva[राभिः] |

The neu. @deva[प्रवरे] (@deva[प्रकृष्टा वै यस्य]) changed to @deva[प्ररि] (by @deva[पररिरयप्रसङ्देश] Pāṇ. I. 1. 48. and I. 2. 47.) should be declined like @deva[वारि] except before the consonantal terminations, when it should be declined like @deva[रै] m. f.

[^1]: The Kau. does not specially mention this form. Just as it stops after giving the Nom. forms in the case of words like @deva[रै], @deva[गौ], @deva[स्त्रियौ] &c., so does it do in the case of @deva[से] also, implying thereby that the Voc. forms of @deva[से] are also like the Nom. ones. But the Sutra @deva[एङ् ह्रस्वात्सम्बुद्धेः] Pāṇ. VI. 1. 69 (@deva[एङन्तात् ह्रस्वान्तात् अङ्गात् हल् उच्यते सम्बुद्धेः चेत्]) (Kau.) seems to apply in the present case and by dropping the final @deva[म्] we get the form @deva[से].
