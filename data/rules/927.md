[^1] The Imperfect is optionally used for the Perfect when the particles @deva[कु] and @deva[स्माट्] are used in a sentence, @deva[इति हि अकरोत्] or @deva[चकार राम हि अकरोत्] or @deva[चकार]

(a) [^2] It may also be optionally used in asking questions referring to a very recent time; @deva[अगच्छः किम्] (Qu.) (Ans) @deva[अगच्छम्] or @deva[कथं किम्]? @deva[कथम्] but when the question refers to a very remote time the Perfect alone ought to be used; @deva[शशाङ्कवारे अगम् किम्]? @deva[अगाम्].

[^1]: @deva[महर्षादौ लुङ्लिटावकर्मकात्।] Pāṇ III. 3 142.

[^2]: @deva[इङ्यातो लुट् च। भूतै चादवैदे कुट्युट्।] Pāṇ III, 2. 116, 117.
