## Futures, Conditional and Benedictive in Passive

### (a) Forms in Impersonal Passive

The forms of the two Futures, Conditional and also the Benedictive (especially @deva[भावे]—Passive, briefly stated below.

### (b) Optional Forms with Vṛddhi

The Aorist optionally forms the Futures, Conditional as well as the Benedictive of the Passive by changing their vowels to Vṛddhi and then by @deva[आ] regarded to them invariably; in the case of roots ending in a vowel, the vowel is lengthened before @deva[इ]; and the @deva[य्] inserted between the root and this @deva[इ].

**Examples:**
- @deva[दा]—@deva[दायिष्यते], @deva[दायि], @deva[दायात्]
- @deva[वच्]—@deva[वच्य]@deva[च्यते]
- @deva[युज्]@deva[यते]—@deva[युज्+य]
- @deva[चकरे] 'to do'—@deva[कारिष्यते], @deva[कारि], @deva[कार्यात्]

@note[type=exception]{See @ref[468]; these forms strictly forbidden with them; only @deva[इस्] used.}

### (c) Additional Forms

The First Future, Conditional and the 5th Variety of the Aorist containing a nasal or a @deva[र्], @deva[त्], @deva[स्] of the Passive, &c.
