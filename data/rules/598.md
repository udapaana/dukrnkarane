## Passive Forms of Tenth Class Roots

### (a) Optional Dropping of अय्

The @deva[अय्] (i. e. @deva[अय्] with the final @deva[अ] dropped) is optionally dropped in the General Tenses, except the Perfect. The Aorist forms, except that of the 3rd sing., are made up by adding the Âtmanepada terminations that of the Fifth Form.

**Examples:**
- @deva[चोर]—@deva[चोरयाति], @deva[चोरयामास] Perf.
- @deva[चोरयिष्यते], @deva[चोरितास्यत्], 1st Fu.
- @deva[चोरयिष्यते], @deva[चोरयिष्यते] 2nd Fu.
- @deva[अचोरयिषि], @deva[अचोरयिषि], Aor. 1st sing., &c.

### (b) Optional Lengthening

Roots which do not lengthen thier penultimate @deva[अ] (see also @ref[603]) lengthen it optionally in the general Tenses of the Passive, except in Perf., when @deva[अय्] is dropped.

**Example:**
@deva[कम्]—@deva[अकम्यिषि], @deva[अकाम्यिषि], Aor. 1 sing., &c.

### (c) Third Person Singular

The 3rd per. sing. of the Pass. Aorist is formed by dropping @deva[अय्], necessarily and adding @deva[इ].

**Examples:**
- @deva[चोर]—@deva[अचोरि]—@deva[अचोरायि]
- @deva[पीड़]—@deva[अपीड़ायि]
- @deva[पार]—@deva[अपारि], @deva[अपारिषि] Aor. 3 sing.
- @deva[यह्]—@deva[अपारि], @deva[अपार्षि], @deva[अपार्षि] Aorist, 3rd sing.
