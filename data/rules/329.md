When a compound has for its second member a word denoting a limb of the body of an animal and having no conjunct consonant preceding its final vowel, the fem. is formed by the addition of @deva[आ] or @deva[ई];[^1] @deva[अतिकेशा-शी]; 'a women having abundant hair;' @deva[सुकेशा-शी]; @deva[चन्द्रमुखा-खी]; but @deva[सुगुल्फा] having beautiful ankles; @deva[हस्तनी] or @deva[हस्तना] (@deva[आ वा प्रतिषेधा वा];) @deva[हट्टका शाला] 'an edifice with a beautiful front'.

**(a)** If the word denoting the limb, however, be one of the words @deva[कोष], @deva[नास्] @deva[जङ्घ], @deva[उस्रा], @deva[शिखा], @deva[वाल], @deva[शाफ], @deva[शुक], @deva[भुज], @deva[कर] &c. or have more than two syllables, the fem. is formed in @deva[आ] alone;[^2] @deva[काश्यपात्कोषा] (@deva[अद्रिमाचूर्णः कोषा] Sid. Kau.), @deva[पृथुजघना], having large hips; @deva[बहुलनयना], &c.

**(b)** Also when the first member is one of @deva[स], @deva[सह], or @deva[विष्वमात्], the fem. is formed in @deva[आ] necessarily;[^3] @deva[सकेशा], @deva[अकेशा], @deva[विष्वमात्नासिका], @deva[सहनासिका] &c.
