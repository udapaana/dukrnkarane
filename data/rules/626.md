## Exceptions and Special Uses of Frequentatives

### (a) Exceptional Roots

A Frequentative may be formed from @deva[इ] 'to go', @deva[ऋ] 'to go', @deva[अन्] 'to eat' and @deva[जुप्] 'to conceal', though these begin with a vowel; and from @deva[सत्त्] cl. 10 'to hint', @deva[दूष्] cl. 10 'to string together', and @deva[मुख्] cl. 10, though they belong to the 10th class.

### (b) Special Semantic Uses

The Frequentative of roots signifying motion conveys the notion of tortuous motion and not of repetition.

The Frequentative of roots @deva[छुअ्] 'to cut', @deva[सद्] 'to sink down', @deva[वत्] 'to go', @deva[गम्] 'to mutter prayers', @deva[यच्] 'to yawn', @deva[दह्] 'to burn', @deva[दंश्] 'to bite', and @deva[गृ] 'to swallow' imply reproach on the manner of doing the act: @deva[अडोलच्छति] 'he cuts awkwardly', @deva[सासत्यते] 'falls down badly', @deva[संवद्यते], etc.
