When @deva[अ] or @deva[आ] is followed by @deva[इ], @deva[उ], @deva[ऋ], @deva[ऌ], short or long, the @deva[गुण] letter corresponding to the latter takes the place of both[^1] e.g. @deva[उप] + @deva[इन्द्र] = @deva[उपेन्द्र:] Vishnu; @deva[परम] + @deva[ईश्वर:] = @deva[परमेश्वर:] the great lord; @deva[रमा] + @deva[इच्छा] = @deva[रमेच्छा] the wish of Ramā; @deva[यथा] + @deva[इच्छित] = @deva[यथेच्छितम्] according to wish or desire; @deva[हित] + @deva[उपदेश:] = @deva[हितोपदेश:] a friendly instruction; @deva[तव] + @deva[ऊरु] = @deva[तवोरु:] Kṛṣṇa's thigh; @deva[गङ्गा] + @deva[उदक] = @deva[गङ्गोदक], @deva[मङ्ग] + @deva[ऊरु] = @deva[मङ्गोरु]; @deva[कृष्ण] + @deva[ऋद्धि:] = @deva[कृष्णर्द्धि:] Kṛṣṇa's prosperity; @deva[महा] + @deva[ऋषि:] = @deva[महर्षि:] a great sage; @deva[तव] + @deva[ऌकार:] = @deva[तवल्कार:] The ḷkāra pronounced by you.

**(a)** If @deva[ऋ] or @deva[ऌ] be followed by homogeneous consonants, except the nasals, the semivowels, and @deva[र], the first of them is optionally dropped;[^2] @deva[कुम्भ] + @deva[ऋद्धि:] = @deva[कुम्भर्द्धि:] by the general rule; then @deva[कुम्भर्] + @deva[उ] + @deva[द्] + @deva[धि] = @deva[कुम्भोद्धि:] by this rule, the @deva[ऋ] being dropped; and also a third form @deva[कुम्भॠद्धि:] (vide @ref[22], sec. d., below).

**(b)** A consonant of the first five classes except @deva[र] coming after a semivowel is optionally doubled; so @deva[तवल्कार:] also becomes @deva[तवल्ल्कार:], @deva[तवल्ल्लकार:] and @deva[तवल्ल्ल्लकार:], 4 forms altogether.

**Exceptions:** Vṛddhi substitute takes place in the following cases:[^3]

**(a)** When a word ending in @deva[अ] is followed by @deva[ऊह], and @deva[अ] by @deva[ऊढ], @deva[ऊढा] and @deva[ऊहि]; e.g. @deva[प्रध] + @deva[ऊह:] = @deva[प्रधौह:], chief guess (or this may be the acc. pl. of @deva[प्रधवाह] a young bull trained to the yoke); @deva[प्र] + @deva[ऊह:] = @deva[प्रौह:] principal reasoning. Similarly @deva[प्रौढ:] full grown; @deva[प्रौढि:]; but @deva[प्रोहवान्] (as @deva[ऊहवान्] is not mentioned in the Sūtra).

**(b)** @deva[अक्ष] + @deva[ऊहिनी] = @deva[अक्षौहिणी] a complete army.[^4] (The change of @deva[उ] to @deva[ऊ] will be explained further on).

**(c)** When @deva[स्व] is followed by @deva[ईर] and @deva[ईरित्] both derived from @deva[ईर्] to go; e.g. @deva[स्व] + @deva[ईर:] = @deva[स्वैर:], one acting wilfully, self-willed; @deva[स्व] + @deva[ईरिणी] = @deva[स्वैरिणी]; so @deva[स्वैरी], @deva[स्वैरी] (@deva[स्वेन ईरितं कीलसदृशं इति]).

**(d)** When @deva[अ] is followed by @deva[कृत:] and there is an inst. Tat.; as @deva[सुख] + @deva[कृत] = @deva[सुखैकृत:] blessed with happiness (@deva[सुखेन कृत:]), but @deva[परमत्कृत:] very adorable (@deva[परमश्रद्धया कृत:]).

**(e)** When the word @deva[ऋण] comes after @deva[प्र], @deva[वत्सतर], @deva[ऋण], @deva[दशन] and @deva[नदी]; @deva[प्र] + @deva[ऋण] = @deva[प्राऋण:] principal debts, so @deva[वत्सतराऋणे] debt for a calf; @deva[ऋणाऋणे] (a debt contracted to liquidate a previous debt); @deva[दशाऋणे:] (Name of a country; lit. the country with ten fortresses); also @deva[दशाऋणी नदी] (lit. the river into which ten other rivers flow).

**(f)** When[^5] a preposition ending in @deva[अ] is followed by a root having an initial @deva[ऋ]; as @deva[उप] + @deva[ऋच्छति] = @deva[उपार्च्छति]; @deva[प्रार्च्छति]; but if the root be a denominative, the Vṛddhi is optional; @deva[प्र] + @deva[ऋषभीयति] = @deva[प्रर्षभीयति] or @deva[प्रार्षभीयति] acts like a bull: but @deva[उप] + @deva[ऊकरीयति] = @deva[उपोकरीयति], as the @deva[ऊ] is long; also @deva[प्र] + @deva[ऌकारीयति] = @deva[प्रा] + @deva[ऌकारीयति] very much wishes for @deva[ऌकार] lkāra; for @deva[ऌ] is grammatically considered to be similar to @deva[ऋ].
