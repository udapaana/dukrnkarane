The affixes @deva[च्विन्] and @deva[सात्] form adverbial words (@deva[अव्यय]).

## @deva[च्विन्]

Of this affix @deva[च्] and @deva[इन्] disappear and @deva[व्] remains.[^1] It is added to the following roots when preceded by a preposition and meaning 'in honour of.'[^2]

### Roots Taking च्विन्

**(1)** The roots @deva[कृ] 'to do', @deva[भू] 'to be', @deva[अस्] 'to be', when preceded by the prepositions @deva[नमस्], @deva[तिरस्], @deva[पुरस्], and @deva[अधि].[^3]

Thus @deva[नमस्+कृ] forms @deva[नमस्कृत्य] 'having saluted'; @deva[तिरस्+कृ] forms @deva[तिरस्कृत्य] 'having dishonoured', 'having neglected'; @deva[पुरस्+कृ] forms @deva[पुरस्कृत्य] 'having placed before', 'having honoured'; @deva[अधि+कृ] forms @deva[अधिकृत्य] 'with reference to', or 'about' or 'concerning,' as @deva[देवदत्तं अधिकृत्य] = 'concerning Devadatta.'

**(2)** The root @deva[क्रम्] 'to step', when preceded by the prep. @deva[परि]; @deva[परिक्रम्य] 'having walked round' or 'round'.

**(3)** @deva[स्था] 'to stand' when preceded by the prep. @deva[उप]; @deva[उपस्थाय] 'having approached', or 'after approaching.'

**(4)** @deva[नम्] 'to bow', when preceded by the prep. @deva[प्र]; @deva[प्रणम्य] 'having bowed to.'

**(5)** @deva[वद्] 'to speak', when preceded by @deva[अभि], in the sense of 'salutation'; @deva[अभिवाद्य] 'having saluted', or 'saluting.'

**(6)** @deva[गम्] 'to go', when preceded by the prep. @deva[अभि]; @deva[अभिगम्य] 'having approached.'

**(7)** @deva[आस्] 'to sit', when preceded by the prep. @deva[परि] and @deva[उप]; @deva[पर्युपास्य] 'having honoured or worshipped.'

**(8)** @deva[ईक्ष्] 'to see', when preceded by the prep. @deva[अव], in the sense of 'consideration'; @deva[अवेक्ष्य] 'having considered, or 'considering.'

**(9)** @deva[धृ] 'to hold', when preceded by @deva[उप], in the sense of 'determining'; @deva[उपधार्य] 'having determined', or 'determining.'

**N. B.**—The words formed by the above rule are indeclinable, and are adverbs. Hence they are also called @deva[अव्यय] 'indeclinables.'[^4]

---

**N. B.**—The affix @deva[ल्यप्] is a synonym of @deva[च्विन्], and like the latter is employed in forming adverbial indeclinables. Here also of the letters @deva[ल्], @deva[य्] and @deva[प्] of the affix @deva[य] alone remains, as @deva[नमस्कृत्य], &c. There is however the following distinction:—When the root is preceded by any other preposition or prepositions than those mentioned above, the affix @deva[ल्यप्] is employed, but not @deva[च्विन्]; @deva[यावत्] + @deva[जीव] form @deva[यावज्जीवम्] 'for life', 'as long as one lives', @deva[समाधिकृत्य] 'including', &c.[^5]

---

## @deva[सात्]

The affix @deva[सात्] may be added in forming the word @deva[मस्तकसात्] from @deva[मस्तक], which means "reducing to atoms, destroying utterly." The words @deva[सततम्], @deva[संततम्] and @deva[संमितम्], meaning 'always', 'for ever', 'together' &c., may optionally take this affix. Thus @deva[सततम्] or @deva[सततसात्]; @deva[संततम्] or @deva[संततसात्]; @deva[संमितम्] or @deva[संमितसात्] respectively. The word @deva[एक] may take this affix @deva[सात्] or @deva[शस्] in forming the word @deva[एकसात्] or @deva[एकशः] which means, 'one by one', 'each one.'

---

**N. B.**—The affix @deva[शस्] may also be added to @deva[शत] 'hundred', @deva[सहस्र] 'thousand', @deva[द्वि] 'two', &c., in the sense of 'a number of.' Thus we have @deva[शतशः] 'by hundreds'; @deva[सहस्रशः] 'by thousands'; @deva[द्विशः] 'by twos.'

---

### Irregular Formations

The following indeclinables are irregularly formed and must be remembered:—@deva[पुनः] 'again', @deva[नमः] 'salutation', @deva[जुषः] 'satisfactorily', @deva[मक्षु] 'quickly', @deva[अधः] 'below', @deva[प्रातः] 'in the morning', @deva[सायम्] 'in the evening' &c.

[^1]: The name of the affix is @deva[क्त्वा].

[^2]: When this affix is used, the root takes weak reduplication in the case of roots of the 3rd class. See §. 511.

[^3]: Before these roots when preceded by these prepositions, the affix @deva[क्त्वा] takes the form @deva[च्विन्], which explains the employment of the latter term in the Sūtra instead of the former.

[^4]: Words ending in @deva[च्विन्] or formed by means of @deva[क्त्वा] are indeclinable, and partake of the nature of the absolutive or gerund.

[^5]: The affix @deva[ल्यप्] is always used when more than one preposition is employed; as @deva[प्रत्युपकृत्य] 'having done a good turn in return.'
