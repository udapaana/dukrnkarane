## Genitive/Locative Absolute for Contempt, Disregard ('in spite of')

When 'contempt or disregard' is to be shown, the Genitive or the Locative Absolute is used; @deva[नर्तनस्य निरूपयस्य वा उन्मत्तः वा प्राव्राजीत्] he turned out a recluse disregarding or despising son i. e. in spite of the weeping of his son. In this sense the Genitive Absolute is used more often. The Locative or the Genitive absolute may thus have the sense of 'in spite of,' 'notwithstanding,' &c. in English.[^1]

(a) The Locative Absolute be made to express the idea of 'as soon as,' 'no sooner than,' 'the moment that' &c. by compounding it with the word @deva[एव] or @deva[मात्र]; @deva[निशिमितेव...संहितायाम् एव] (Rag. XVI. 78.) no sooner was the arrow fixed, &c. @deva[आसादितवचनम् एव मम] scarcely had I finished my speech when.
