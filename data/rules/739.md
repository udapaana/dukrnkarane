## § 739. Irregular Words Derived with Affix घ्यत्

The following words are irregularly derived by means of the affix @deva[घ्यत्]; @deva[आहात्यं] 'what ought to be brought from the Gārhapatya i. e. the Dakshiṇā-gni;' (fr. @deva[नी] with @deva[आ]) @deva[विद्युणामाविनांगे दृत्युतृद। य हि गाहपत्युगाम्यानात्रोऽक्षिनां स्य तत्संतप्रत्र्यूतोगाम्।] (Sid. Kau.); but @deva[नायं] in other senses 'to be led, &c.' @deva[मोपुनुमार्यं] (fr. @deva[नी] with @deva[मो]) @deva[चौरः मेनोवनुहे डुर्युथुर्येः] (Sid. Kau.); 'disgusted with worldly pleasures;' as @deva[प्रणायुयूनैवोचारो विरक्तो डुर्युथुर्येः]। but @deva[प्रणेयं] in other cases.

(a) @deva[गीतोत्यं अनेन इति भाव्यं] 'a sacrifice' (fr. @deva[या]); @deva[समुयक् तिङुवं होमवशुम्भरोर्यति इति भाज्यार्यं] (from. @deva[नी] with @deva[सम्]) @deva[द्वाविविकोर:] 'a kind of offering (See Śīs. XI. 41)'; @deva[तिन्नीविरोऽक्षिम्युणागुवत्कै निवुद्धुत्यः निवुत्ता:] (fr. @deva[वध्] with @deva[नि]); @deva[शुसीत अनेन संदाध्यते घाश्यः] (fr. @deva[धा]) @deva[चरुख:]; @deva[कुपुष्यन पाष्ये (अनिमित्रा सोम:) कुपुष्यार्यख: कृत्:]; @deva[संज्योषैविरोत्य: संज्यास्यु:] (a sacrifice); @deva[परिज्ञात्यं], @deva[उपत्युत्यं]; @deva[स्वयुत्यत्: पूनिष्पाषैविकायिनोङुयुते] (rites for depositing the sacrificial fire); @deva[परिचेत्युत्], @deva[डुयुचेत्युत्], @deva[संवूत्युत्] in other cases; @deva[चौवूते अतीं तिस्युत: आर्जते]; @deva[अतीरे चयनं आश्वातिस्युत्].

## @ref[741]. Participles with Active Sense

The roots @deva[भू] and @deva[गै] take the affix @deva[यत्]; @deva[भव्यः] and @deva[स्था] take @deva[अनीय]; and @deva[वद्], @deva[वृ] and @deva[पच्] take @deva[घ्यत्], in an active sense; @deva[अदेर्तो भव्यं] (also @deva[भव्युभूतनं]); @deva[गायुवो गेवः] 'one who sings' (also @deva[गेयं राम अनेनै]); @deva[प्रवचनीयः] 'one who speaks' @deva[उदु], @deva[वानीयः] 'one who stands for,' @deva[स्तुत्य], @deva[पुचोय युरितिय्येः], @deva[पाच्युयुं] 'food.'

## (3) By means of केलिमुर् (एलिम्).

## @ref[742]. Affix एलिमुर् for Transitive Roots

A few transitive roots take the affix @deva[एलिमुर्] having the same force as the affix @deva[यत्]; @deva[पचेलिमः] fr. @deva[पच्], 'fit to ripen or to be cooked; as @deva[पचेलिमा भावनः]; @deva[भिदेलिमाः] (fr. @deva[भिद्]) @deva[स्तम्बाः] 'the pine tress ought to be felled,' &c.

## @ref[743]. Declension of Potential Participles

The declension of these Participles follows that of nouns ending in @deva[अ].

## II. INDECLINABLE PARTICIPLES

## (a) Indeclinable Past Participles.

## @ref[744]. Introduction to Indeclinable Past Participles (Gerunds)

The Indeclinable Past Participles are of the nature of gerunds. They fall under two heads:—(1) Those derived by affixing @deva[क्त्वा] to the simple root, and (2) Those derived by means of @deva[य] affixed to the root compounded with prepositions or words used prepositionally; @deva[गम्]—@deva[गत्वा] 'having gone,' @deva[भू] with @deva[परिदेवस्त्र] 'having' 'having served;' &c.

---

## I. Indeclinable Participles formed with क्त्वा.

## @ref[745]. Formation of Gerund in क्त्वा

The Indeclinable Past Participle or gerund in @deva[क्त्वा] is formed of all roots or derivative verbal bases to which a preposition (nor a prepositional word) is prefixed. The affix @deva[क्त्वा] is of the same nature as the @deva[त्] of the Past Passive Participle, so that all the changes that take place before @deva[त्] generally take place before @deva[क्त्वा]. In other words from the P. P. Ptc. in @deva[त्] or @deva[न] separate the @deva[त्] or @deva[न] from it, add @deva[क्त्वा] instead, and this will be the form of the gerund in @deva[क्त्वा]; e. g.

## @ref[746]. Guṇa Before Intermediate इ

When the intermediate @deva[इ] is inserted, the preceding vowel takes its Guṇa substitute; @deva[शि]—@deva[शयित्वा], @deva[कु]—@deva[कवित्वा], @deva[जाग्]—@deva[जागरित्वा], &c.

(a) The roots @deva[तृप्], @deva[द्विष्], @deva[हृष्] and @deva[कुल्] take Guṇa optionally; @deva[तृपित्वा] or @deva[तृप्त्वा], @deva[द्विषित्वा] or @deva[द्विप्त्वा], @deva[हृषित्वा] or @deva[हृष्त्वा], @deva[कुल]—@deva[कवित्वा] or @deva[कुलित्वा].

(b) But the roots @deva[युध्], @deva[पुष्], @deva[द्युत्], @deva[कुश्], @deva[इष्], and @deva[किञ्छु], the roots mentioned at @ref[493] (i. e. @deva[किच्], &c.) do not take Guṇa; @deva[इष्]—@deva[युधित्वा] 'having rejoiced;' @deva[युध्]—@deva[पुषित्वा], @deva[द्युत्]—@deva[द्युतित्वा] 'having covered,' @deva[कुशित्वा], @deva[इङुषित्वा], @deva[किञ्छु]—@deva[किङ्छित्वा] or @deva[किद्युष्युः] @deva[कुर्]—@deva[कुदित्वा], @deva[विख्य]—@deva[विनित्वा]; &c.

## @ref[748]. Set Roots and Roots of the 10th Class

The roots @deva[शि], @deva[वी], @deva[सी], @deva[गृ] and @deva[ज्ञा], Seṭ roots ending in consonants, roots of the 10th class and all derivative verbs, admit @deva[इ] before @deva[क्त्वा]; roots of the 10th cl. preserve their @deva[अय] before @deva[क्त्वा]; @deva[शि]—@deva[शयित्वा], @deva[वी]—@deva[वयित्वा], @deva[ज्ञा]—@deva[ज्ञरित्वा] or @deva[जारित्वा], @deva[पद्]—@deva[पदित्वा], @deva[वि]—@deva[विचित्वा], @deva[गुरुच्]—@deva[गुरुचित्वा], @deva[जागृ]—@deva[जागरित्वा], &c., @deva[चुर्]—@deva[चोरयित्वा], @deva[कम्प]—@deva[कम्पयित्वा], @deva[बुध्]—cau. @deva[बोधयित्वा], desi. @deva[बुबुधिषित्वा]; Atm. fre. @deva[बोबुधयित्वा], &c.

## @ref[749]. Nasal Dropping Rules

(a) The roots @deva[स्कन्द्] and @deva[स्वृज्] do not drop their nasal; @deva[स्कन्दित्वा]—@deva[स्कद्य]—@deva[स्कन्दित्वा]; @deva[स्वृञ्जित्वा].

(b) The penultimate nasal of roots ending in @deva[म्] or @deva[न्] and that of @deva[वञ्च्] 'to roam abroad,' 'to deceive' and @deva[लुञ्च्] 'to tear out,' are optionally dropped, @deva[यम्]—@deva[यमित्वा], @deva[यतित्वा], @deva[भुज्]—@deva[भुक्त्वा], @deva[यक्षित्वा]; @deva[वञ्च्]—@deva[वञ्चित्वा], @deva[वचित्वा], @deva[वत्त्वा]; @deva[लुञ्च्]—@deva[लुञ्चित्वा], @deva[लुचित्वा].

(c) Roots ending in @deva[ञ्च्] such as @deva[भुञ्ज्], @deva[रञ्च्], @deva[सृज्], @deva[स्पृश्] &c. and the root @deva[तुञ्ज्] optionally drop their nasal before @deva[क्त्वा]; @deva[भुञ्ज्]—@deva[भुङ्क्त्वा] or @deva[भुक्त्वा], @deva[रञ्च्]—@deva[रङ्क्त्वा] or @deva[रक्त्वा]; @deva[अञ्ज्]—@deva[अङ्कित्वा], @deva[अक्त्वा] or @deva[अक्त्वा].

(d) @deva[मृज्] and @deva[नञ्च्] insert a nasal optionally; @deva[मार्क्त्वा] or @deva[मृष्ट्वा]; @deva[नश्कित्वा], @deva[नष्ट्वा] or @deva[नष्ट्वा].

## @ref[750]. Guṇa for Penultimate इ and उ

Guṇa is optionally substituted for the penultimate @deva[इ] and @deva[उ], roots beginning with any consonant and ending in any except @deva[ऋ] or @deva[व] when @deva[क्त्वा] has @deva[इ] prefixed to it; @deva[सिध्]—@deva[क्षुधित्वा] or @deva[क्षुद्धित्वा]; @deva[रुज्]—@deva[र्षुधिर] or @deva[क्षुदरित्वा]। also @deva[क्षुरित्त्वा]।; @deva[क्षुध्]—6. P. @deva[क्षुधित्वा] or @deva[क्षुधित्वा]; @deva[स्पृश्] @deva[यादितः] or @deva[निदुकोनिवदा]; @deva[पिष्]—@deva[निषित्वा], @deva[गुनित्वा] and @deva[निप], or @deva[रुप्], &c., but @deva[विद्]—@deva[वेदित्वा], @deva[मुत्वा].

## 2. Indeclinable Participles formed with य.

## @ref[751]. Formation of Gerund in य

When a root is compounded with one or more prepositions or words prefixed to verbs like prepositions, the indeclinable participle is formed by affixing @deva[य] immediately to it; the @deva[य] is changed to @deva[त्य] after a short radical vowel (even when it combines with the final vowel of a preposition to a long vowel); e. g.

| | | | | |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| @deva[दा] with @deva[आ]—@deva[आदाय] | @deva[प्र] with @deva[प्र]—@deva[प्रेत्य] |
| @deva[मिद्] with @deva[विश्]—@deva[विज्ञाय] | @deva[कृ] with @deva[सम्]—@deva[संस्कृत्य] |
| @deva[जि] with @deva[परा]—@deva[पराजित्य] | @deva[कृ] with @deva[विश्]—@deva[विभक्त्य] |
| @deva[नी] with @deva[वि]—@deva[विनीय] | @deva[वद्] with @deva[नि]—@deva[निवाच्य] |
| @deva[भू] with @deva[अनु]—@deva[अनुभूय] | @deva[मद्] with @deva[उत्]—@deva[उत्पद्य] |
| @deva[इ] with @deva[अभि]—@deva[अभीत्य] | &c. | &c. | &c. |

## @ref[752]. Application of Previous Rules

The rules given at @ref[394], @ref[395], @ref[459], @ref[502] and @ref[587] apply to the roots also in the gerund in @deva[य]:—

| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| @deva[विद्] with @deva[प्र]—@deva[प्रवीद्य] | @deva[अच्] with @deva[प्र]—@deva[प्राच्य] |
| @deva[कृ] with @deva[अव]—@deva[अवकीर्य] | @deva[वस्] with @deva[प्र]—@deva[प्रोष्य] |

## @ref[753]. Nasal Dropping for 8th Class Roots

Roots of the 8th class ending in a nasal, except @deva[यम्] and the roots @deva[मन्], @deva[यन्] and @deva[हन्], drop their nasal necessarily; @deva[तक्], @deva[तन्य], @deva[यम्य] and @deva[रुम्] do it optionally; @deva[तन्] with @deva[वि]—@deva[वितत्य]; @deva[म्रुष्] with @deva[प्रति]—@deva[प्रतिम्रुष्य]; @deva[तन्य] with @deva[वि]—@deva[वितन्य] or @deva[विततुम्]; @deva[रुम्] with @deva[वि]—@deva[विरुम्य] or @deva[विरत्य]; @deva[मन्] with @deva[प्र]—@deva[प्रणम्य] or @deva[प्रणत्य], &c.

## @ref[754]. Special Forms for सन्, जन्, सन्

The roots @deva[सन्], @deva[जन्] and @deva[सन्] have respectively @deva[निसत्युम्]—@deva[निसाय]; @deva[प्रजन्य]—@deva[प्रजाय]; and @deva[प्रसन्य]—@deva[प्रसाय].

## @ref[755]. द्विष् and जागृ

@deva[द्विष्] lengthens its @deva[इ] before the affix @deva[य] and @deva[जागृ] changes its final vowel to Guṇa; @deva[प्रदीय]; @deva[प्रजाय्य].

## @ref[756]. Samprasāraṇa Rules

(a), @deva[च्युत्] and @deva[छुध्] do not take Samprasāraṇa, @deva[प्रच्युत्य]; @deva[उच्युत्य] 'having become old,' @deva[उप्युत्य] 'having covered,' but @deva[ऋ] with @deva[परि] or @deva[समु], takes Samprasāraṇa optionally, @deva[परिस्युत्य] or @deva[परिदुर्य]; @deva[संस्युत्य] or @deva[संदीर्य].

## @ref[757]. Roots Not Changing आ to इ

The roots mentioned under @ref[486] do not change their @deva[आ] to @deva[इ]; @deva[प्रयाय], @deva[प्रमाय], @deva[प्रमाय], &c.

## @ref[758]. Causals and 10th Class Roots

Roots of the Tenth class and Causals preserve their @deva[अय] before @deva[य]; it being separated by a short penultimate vowel if not, it is dropped; @deva[चोरय]—@deva[परचोर्य]; @deva[बोधय]—@deva[परबोध्य]; @deva[कृ]—cau. @deva[विकारय]; @deva[नी] cau. with @deva[आ]—@deva[आनाय], &c.; but @deva[गृण]—@deva[विगृण्य], @deva[प्रकृपुम्य], @deva[प्रकल्पय]; @deva[प्रबोविदुर्य] 'having again and again caused to be broken.'

## @ref[759]. Causative गम्

(a) @deva[गम्] cau. may retain its @deva[अय] optionally—@deva[गम्य] or @deva[गामय].

## @ref[760]. Desiderative and Frequentative Bases

Desiderative bases add @deva[य] immediately and Freq. bases add it after dropping their @deva[य], when it is preceded by a consonant and dropping the final @deva[अ] when it is preceded by a vowel; @deva[बुबू]—@deva[परबुबुधिष्य]; Fre. @deva[परबोबुध्य]; @deva[भू] Fre. @deva[परबोभूय] &c.

## (b) THE ADVERBIAL INDECLINABLE PARTICIPLE OR The Gerund in अम्.

## @ref[761]. Formation of Gerund in अम्

There is another Indeclinable Participle having the same sense as that in @deva[क्त्वा], formed with the affix @deva[अम्]. Before this the vowel of the derivative verbal base generally undergoes the same changes as it does before the @deva[इ] of the Pass. Aorist 3rd sing.; @deva[नी]—@deva[नयाम्] 'having carried or led,' @deva[दा]—@deva[दायाम्] 'having given,' @deva[भू]—@deva[भावाम्], @deva[भिद्]—@deva[भेदाम्], @deva[भाह्]—@deva[भाह्राम्], @deva[गम्]—@deva[गमाम्], &c.

## @ref[762]. Usage at End of Compounds

This Participle is generally used at the end of compounds; @deva[श्वोन्यस्य हतः] 'he was pelted to death,' @deva[बन्दिनाथं गृहीतः] (Vīks. I. 1.) She was taken a captive;' @deva[प्रभङ्क्तुं प्रसमाम्] (Bhaṭṭi. I. 2.) 'He totally exterminated his enemies, &c.'

## @ref[763]. Repetition for Iterative Sense

The participles in @deva[क्त्वा] and @deva[अम्] when repeated yield the sense of repetition of the action or condition expressed by the verb; @deva[स्मृत्वा स्मृत्वा] or @deva[स्मरं स्मारयुम्] 'having repeatedly remembered; @deva[पीत्वा पीत्वा] or @deva[पाय पायाम्] 'having drunk again and again;' similarly @deva[अच्युत्वाच्युत्वा] or @deva[अचुयाचुयाम्] or @deva[पोजुत्यपोजुत्य] or @deva[पोयुपयम्], @deva[छोरयुछोरयुम् आपुद्य], @deva[गम्]—@deva[गन्त्वा गन्त्वा] or @deva[गम् गमाम्] or @deva[गत् गतम्], @deva[छत्युस्]—@deva[छत्युस्त्वा छत्युस्त्वा] or @deva[छत्युं छत्युम्] or @deva[छत्युं छत्युम्], @deva[पठत् पठतम्], @deva[जागृ]—@deva[जागरं जागरम्], &c.

## @ref[764]. Non-Repetitive Usage

The Gerund @deva[अम्] does not, in many instances, express the notion of repetition.

## @ref[765]. Usage with Prepositions

This gerund or the one in @deva[क्त्वा] is used with the words @deva[अचे], @deva[पुरुषं] and @deva[पुश्चं], used as prepositions, although the notion of repetition is not present in these compounds; @deva[अत्युहैसोद्युत्युत्वा हा स्त्रौयौ] 'having taken to goes out;' so @deva[यथागोत] or @deva[पुञ्चवा पुनु-भोरिते] or @deva[पुञ्चवा बुद्धे].

## @ref[766]. Gerund of कृ

The gerund in @deva[अम्] of the root @deva[कृ] is used

(a) with the noun governed by it, if it be compounded with it prepositionally when censure is implied; @deva[चौरुकारुते आक्रोशुते] (@deva[चौरुकृत्युय उत्युचिसुथुर्येः]) 'cries out (he is) a thief, a thief.' In this case @deva[य] is added to the noun governed.

(b) with the words @deva[स्वाद्], @deva[छुण], and @deva[संस्यद्], @deva[य] being added to these as before; @deva[आस्वादुकारु स्वादु छत्युवा पुकैं स्वादुकारुणुकारुं-संस्यदै—कारुं पुकैं] 'he eats having sweetened or seasoned his food.'

(c) with the words @deva[अत्युया], @deva[एव], @deva[किंयुं], and @deva[कुथुं], provided the root @deva[य] takes its sense; @deva[अत्युयाकारुं हुत्वो] 'he speaks in a coherent manner;' @deva[एवुं कृत्वा] the acts thus;' @deva[किंकारुते], @deva[कुथंकारुते हुयुभुरामु], but @deva[शिनैत्युयुत्या हुत्वा मुश्चैं].

(d) with the particles @deva[युथुं] and @deva[युथा] when an angry reply is given; @deva[युथुमुकारुं शोध्युं युथुमुकारुं शोध्युं किं तवौनैन] (Sid. Kau.); 'I will eat in this manner, I will eat in that manner, what have you do with that?'

## @ref[767]. Gerunds of हन् and विद्य with Objects

The Gerund of the roots @deva[हन्] and @deva[विद्य] is compounded with their objects and expresse totality; @deva[कन्यायुद्युच्युं वरुयति] 'chooses as many girls as he sees, i. e. all of them,' @deva[ब्रातुसातुवुदै भोज्ययति], @deva[यैयं ब्राह्मणं जानाति छुम्युते विन्चारुयत्ति हा तं सर्वै भोज्ययति इत्यर्थुर्येः] (Sid. Kau.) 'he feeds every Brāhmaṇa that he knows or comes across or remembers i. e. all of them.'

(a) The gerunds of @deva[विद्] 'to get' and @deva[जीव्] 'to live' are joined with @deva[यावद्] in the sam3 sense; @deva[यावद्वेद्यं खादुते] 'eats what he gets.'

(b) With the words @deva[पुनत्] and @deva[उदर] the gerund of @deva[पूर्] is used; @deva[चनैर्युर्युं स्थूलते]; @deva[उदर्युर्युं खादुते] 'eats so as to fill his belly.'

## @ref[768]. Gerund of पद् with Specific Words

This gerund of @deva[पद्] is used with @deva[युष्युकू], @deva[चूर्णं] and @deva[स्यदै]; @deva[मुष्युकूतैर्ये पदुत्त युन्नं पदुहीनुग्युर्येः] (Sid. Kau.); so @deva[चूर्णैर्युं पदुहुदि] 'he grinds to powder;' @deva[स्यदैरुयुम्].

## @ref[769]. Special Gerund Usages

With the words @deva[पुसुत्], @deva[अक्षत] and @deva[जीव] are used respectively the gerunds in @deva[अम्] of the roots @deva[हन्], @deva[कृ] and @deva[गह्] in a cognate sense; @deva[पुसुच्छुवातं हुन्ते] 'kills destroying the roots i. e. totally,' @deva[अक्षुतकुरुते करोति] 'does what ought not to have been done;' @deva[जीवुगाहै गृहाति] 'captures him so as to preserve him i. e. alive.'

Kau.; @deva[हुन्वुनुहुं गृहीते]; similarly @deva[गाणियाहु], @deva[कुरुग्राहु कु], &c.
