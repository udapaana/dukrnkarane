The following Avya. compounds are anomalous[^1]:-

@deva[तिष्टन्ति गावः वसितुम् काले स तिष्ठदु दोहजकालः] 'the time when cows stand for being milked;' see Bhatti. IV. 14. So @deva[वह्दु] 'the time when cows conceive or bulls bear the plough; @deva[आयवथाः गावः आस्मिन् काले आयन्तीत्ययम्] 'the evening time,' 'the time when the cows come home'; @deva[खलेवपदम्], 'the time when the barley is on the thrashing ground;' similarly @deva[खलेबुसम्]; @deva[छन्नवसम्] 'when barley is reaped,' @deva[दुग्धमातवसम्], @deva[सह्नेवसम्], &c. @deva[समशुद्धि] 'when the ground is even;' @deva[समपदाति] 'when the foot-soldiers are in right lines,; @deva[हस्तसम्], @deva[विषसम्], @deva[अपसमय्] at the end of the year; @deva[आपत्तीसमय्], @deva[पापसमय्] in an evil year; @deva[पुण्यसमय्], @deva[प्राह्नम्], @deva[प्रथयम्] 'when the chariots move forth,' @deva[प्रमृगम्] 'when the deer comes,' @deva[विषृगम्], @deva[प्रदक्षिणम्], @deva[संगति] and @deva[असंगति].

**N. B.**—According to all followers of Pân. compounds of this group are precluded from being further compounded though poets do not seem to respect the prohibition except in so far as to place such words at the end of these comps.; cf. @deva[प्रदक्षिण-क्रियाहीनाम्] । Rag. I. 76; also IV. 25, VII. 24, &c.
