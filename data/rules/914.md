[^1] The infinitive is also used with:—

(a) verbs meaning to be able, to make bold, to know, to be wearied, to strive, to begin, to set about, to bear, to be pleased, and to be proficient; @deva[चाण्डालः उत्तुं वचनं न शक्नोति] he is not able to support his neck; @deva[जानासि कोपं निवर्तयितुम्] you know how to restrain anger; @deva[आङ्गदेन समं युद्धं प्रवर्तयितुम्] (Bhattṛi. XV. 77) to strove to fight with Aṅgada; @deva[गन्तुं प्रयतिष्यसि] (Meg. 22) thou wilt try to go; @deva[तर्कं विहर्तुमारभे] (?Meg. 103) begin to speak; @deva[भोज्यमन्नमस्ति दियते वा भोजनाय] (Sid. Kau.) there is food to eat, &c.

(b) [^2] words like @deva[अर्ह्] and others meaning sufficient or able, proficient or skilled in, &c.; @deva[प्रजाः पालयितुं अर्हसि] (Rag. X. 25) thou art able to protect the creation; @deva[कः समर्थो दैवस्य विकृतिं कर्तुम्] who is able to change destiny; @deva[प्रासादानां मनोरमाणां] (Meg. 66) the palaces arc able to stand comparison with thee; @deva[भोज्ये पानीये कुशलः यदर्थे] (Sid. Kau.) skilled in eating.

(c) [^3] words having the sense of 'it is time to do anything?' @deva[कालः समर्थो वेला अवकाशो वा भोजनाय] (Sid. Kau.) it is time to take food.

[^2]: @deva[अर्हविवक्षायामेकदेशवचने] Pāṇ. III. 4. 66.

[^3]: @deva[कालसमर्थयोरवकाशे तुमुन्] Pāṇ. III. 3. 167.
