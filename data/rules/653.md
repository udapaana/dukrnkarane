## Vowel Changes Before णयच्

Before this affix the final @deva[अ] of a nominal base is lengthened, @deva[आ] remains unchanged, other final letters undergo the same changes as they do before the other @deva[य] (@deva[क्यच्]).

The final @deva[अ] of a noun is changed to @deva[आ] optionally and that of @deva[अन्तवन्] and @deva[ओजस्] necessarily.

### Examples

@deva[कृष्ण इव आचरति]—@deva[कृष्णायते] 'he acts like Kṛṣṇa'

@deva[यशस्]—@deva[यशस्यते], @deva[यशस्यते] 'he behaves like one who is famous'

@deva[विद्वत्]—@deva[विद्वायते], @deva[विद्वस्यते] 'he acts like a learned man', etc.

But @deva[ओजस्]—@deva[ओजायते] 'acts like one who is lustrous' (powerful)

@deva[अप्सरस्]—@deva[अप्सरायते] 'she behaves like an Apsaras'

### (a) Feminine Nouns

A feminine noun not having @deva[ऋ] for its penultimate drops its feminine termination:

@deva[कुमारी इव आचरति]—@deva[कुमारायते] 'he behaves like a girl'

@deva[द्वैतीयः स्त्री इव आचरति]—@deva[द्वैतीयायते] 'she acts like a female deer'

@deva[योषिता आचरति]—@deva[योष्ट्या] 'acts like a stout woman'

But @deva[पचिकारा इव आचरति]—@deva[पचिकारायते] 'she acts like a female cook', and not @deva[पचिकायते]

### (b) Special Form of सुषी

@deva[सुषी] has @deva[सुषायते], @deva[सुषेयते] and @deva[सुषीयते] 'she acts like a cow'; @deva[सुषायति] the masculine 'he acts like a cow'
