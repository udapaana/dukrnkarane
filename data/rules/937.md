[^1] When there is the idea of hope implied in a conditional form, the Aorist, the Present or the Simple Future may be used in both the clauses to denote a future time, @deva[दैवादवर्षदेव वर्षति वा वर्षिष्यति वा बपेत् बपति वा बपिष्यति वा।] (Sid. Kau.) If it were to rain it would it would sow corn.

[^1]: @deva[आशासमान्नं वर्तमानं लुट् च।] Pāṇ. III. 3. 132.
