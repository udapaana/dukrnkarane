## Nominative Case with Indeclinables Used Adjectivally

When an indeclinable used with an adjectival force takes the place of the verb, the object governed by it is put in the Nominative case.[^1] @deva[विषवृक्षोऽपि संवर्ध्यं घातं छेत्तुमर्हसि] (Kum. V. 71) it is not fit to cut down even a poisonous tree having first reared it up; here the indeclinable @deva[अलमुद्] is equivalent to @deva[न इच्छते] and the whole sentence to @deva[न इतं संवर्ध्यं छेत्तुयुद् अर्हति] (@deva[न इच्छते]) @deva[यस्ति विषवृक्षः अपि]।

**Concord of the Adjective with the Substantive.**
