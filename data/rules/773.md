[^1]A root may be used in its gerundive form in @deva[अम्] with its object prefixed to it if that object be a part of the body which can be severed without fatal effects; @deva[भ्रुवोर्घातं कथयति] 'narrates contracting the eyebrows,' but @deva[शिरो नीहदुर्य] (not @deva[शिरोघातं]) as the severance of the head would cause death.

(a) Similarly when a part of the body is completely hurt or pressed in the action, the gerund in @deva[अम्] is used with that part; @deva[उरगतितं युध्यते] i. e. @deva[कलुपय उरः पीड्यते] 'afflicting the whole bosom;' @deva[उद्गीर्णहिंसं त्रसतिरिष्यते ग्राहं अनुम्युर्थः]
