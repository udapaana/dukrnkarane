## Particles and Prepositions Governing Accusative

The particles @deva[उभयतः], @deva[सर्वतः], @deva[उभाध्यति], @deva[अन्यत्र], @deva[अभितः] and @deva[निकषा]; @deva[अन्तरा], @deva[परितः], @deva[समया], @deva[निकष], @deva[द्वे] and @deva[प्रति] meaning 'to,' and @deva[अन्तर] 'between,' and @deva[अनृतेण] 'without,' 'regarding to,' govern the Accusative; @deva[उभयतः कृष्णं गोपाः] the cowherds are on both sides of Kṛṣṇa; @deva[निकटं प्रासादं रक्षितिः रक्षन्ति] guards keep vigil on all sides of the palace; @deva[उपरि लोकं हरिः] Hari is over all the worlds; @deva[लोकं गतः] went below the world, except itself; Hari is around you; @deva[धिक् कुरूणां] fie upon the lord of the Kurus, with all his brothers; (@deva[धिक्]) is used sometimes with the Nom. and sometimes with the Voc. @deva[धिक् वित्तं] fie upon wealth which is attended with troubles; @deva[किं भाते तत् विनारण यच्च भर्ता पतितः अपि शरीरं न वर्तन्ते] (Bhaṭṭi I. 12) he dispelled the demons from around the altar and offered sacrifices to the minor deities arranged round the principal one. @deva[अन्तरं प्राप्य परितः तिष्ठति] (Kir. XI. 8), @deva[ग्रामं समया निकष चरति] goes near to the village. @deva[अतः अन्यत्र न कोऽपि] no one except Hari is not a devotee of Kṛṣṇa; @deva[मन्मथः अस्ति अल्पः अपि नगरं यति] I have but a faint desire to go to the town; @deva[अन्तर तनो हरिं हरि अनृतेण न सुखं] happiness is not possible without Hari; @deva[देवीं वसुमतीं अनृतेण] with reference to queen Vasumatī.

Some of the indeclinables given above are used with the Genitive; e.g. @deva[उपरि विद्यामतः सूर्यः इव सेत्यस्थौ] (he stood) very high above all by his learning, like the sun, &c.[^1]

[^1]: Vārtikas on Pāṇ. I. 4. 48. @deva[अन्तरा अनृतेण च] Pāṇ. II. 3. 4.
