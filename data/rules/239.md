The following words, when at the end of a Tatpurusha, drop the final vowel, together with the following consonant if there be any, and add @deva[अ] under the circumstances mentioned:—

**(a)**[^1] @deva[रात्रि] when preceded by a numeral, an indeclinable, a word expressive of a part of the whole, such as @deva[पूर्व], @deva[अपर], &c., or by the words @deva[सर्व], @deva[संख्यात] and @deva[पुण्य]; as @deva[द्वयो: रात्र्यो: समाहार: द्विरात्र] an aggregate of two nights (Dwi.); @deva[अतिक्रान्तो रात्रिरति-रात्र:] that has passed a night, the dead of night; @deva[पूर्वं रात्रे: पूर्वरात्र:] the first part of the night, @deva[सर्वा रात्रि: सर्वरात्र:], @deva[संख्याता रात्रि: संख्यातरात्र:], @deva[पुण्यरात्र:] an auspicious night.

The same happens when @deva[रात्रि] is preceded by @deva[अहर्] in a Dwandwa; as @deva[अहश्च रात्रिश्च अहोरात्र:].

**(b)**[^2] @deva[राजन्], @deva[अहर्] and @deva[सति]; as @deva[परम: राजा परमराज:] a great king, @deva[मह्राणं राजा महाराज:]; @deva[उत्तमं अह: उत्तमाह:] an excellent day, @deva[एकं च तदह: एकाह्]; @deva[द्वयोरह्नो: समाहार: द्वह:], so =@deva[एह:], @deva[सप्ताह:], &c. @deva[पुण्यं अह: पुण्याह्] a holy day, @deva[सदिताह्] (vide @ref[245], b.), @deva[कृष्णस्य सत्ता कृष्णसत्व:] &c.

**(c) Exceptions**[^3]—@deva[अहर्] when preceded by an indeclinable, or a word denoting a part of the whole, or by @deva[सर्वे] or by a numeral with a Taddhita affix added to the whole compound, becomes @deva[अह्न्]; as @deva[अतिकान्तमह्:] exceeding a day (in duration) @deva[अह्नह्:], @deva[पूर्वाह्न:] forenoon, @deva[सर्वाह्न:] the whole day, @deva[संख्यताह्न्:], @deva[द्वयोरह्नो: भव: द्व्यह्न:] (@deva[-ह्ना] fem.), @deva[त्र्यह्नीय:] &c.; with @deva[संख्यात्] the change is optional; as @deva[संख्यताह्न्:], or @deva[ह्न:].

**N. B.** The[^4]@deva[न्] of the substitute is changed to @deva[ण्] when preceded by @deva[र्] belonging to the first member ending in @deva[अ]; as @deva[सर्वाह्ण:]; but @deva[परगतं अह: पराह्न:] (as @deva[परा] ends in @deva[आ]).

**(d)**[^5] @deva[तक्षन्] when preceded by @deva[ग्राम] or @deva[कौट], and @deva[श्वन्] by @deva[अति] or an inanimate object with which it is compounded; e. g. @deva[ग्रामस्य तक्षा ग्रामतक्ष:] 'a village carpenter i. e. one not very skilful,' @deva[कुत्स्यो भव: कौट:] (@deva[स्वतन्त्र:] ) @deva[स चासौ तक्षा च कौटतक्ष:] 'an independent carpenter'; @deva[अतिश्व:] [@deva[दराह्:]] 'fleeter than a dog'; @deva[अतिश्वी] (worse than a dog's life) @deva[सेवा]; @deva[आकष्टे: श्वा इव आकर्षश्व:] 'a dog like i. e. bad or unlucky throw of dice,' but @deva[वानरश्वा] 'a monkey like a dog'.

**(e)**[^6] @deva[सक्थिन्] when preceded by @deva[उत्तर], @deva[मृग] or @deva[पूर्व] or by a word denoting an inanimate object with which it is compounded; @deva[उत्तरसक्थम्] 'the upper part of the thigh', @deva[मृगसक्थम्] 'the thigh of a deer,' @deva[पूर्वसक्थम्], @deva[फलकामिव सक्थिन् फलकसक्थम्] 'a thigh like a board'.

**(f)** A numeral when compounded in a Tat. @deva[त्रिंशति त्रिंशत्: त्रिंशति वर्षाणि] (@deva[चैत्रस्य]) more than thirty; @deva[त्रिंशत्: त्रिंशतांगुलिभ्य: त्रिंशत्: खड्ग:] a sword more than thirty fingers in length.
