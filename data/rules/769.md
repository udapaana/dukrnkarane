[^1]With the words @deva[पुसस्], @deva[अक्षत] and @deva[जीव] are used respectively the gerunds in @deva[अम्] of the roots @deva[हन्], @deva[कृ] and @deva[गह्] in a cognate sense; @deva[पुससघातं हन्ति] 'kills destroying the roots i. e. totally,' @deva[अक्षतकरं करोति] 'does what ought not to have been done;' @deva[जीवगाहं गृहाति] 'captures him so as to preserve him i. e. alive.'

(a) The gerund of @deva[हन्] and @deva[पद्] is used with words signifying instruments (of action); @deva[पादघातं हन्ति]=@deva[पादेन हन्ति] 'strikes (kicking) with the foot;' @deva[स्फरं पठति]=@deva[प्रहरण पठति] 'reads (with (using) the water.

(b) Similarly the gerund of @deva[वन्] and @deva[गह्] is used with @deva[हन्] and its synonyms, and that of @deva[पूर्] with @deva[स्पृ]; @deva[हनवनं पुस्यति]; so @deva[करग्राहं], @deva[हस्तेन ग्रस्तीतोर्यर्थः] Sid. Kau.; @deva[हन्वनं गृहीति]; similarly @deva[गाणिग्राहं], @deva[करग्राहं] &c.
