When @deva[स्] or a consonant of the dental class comes in contact with:—

**(a)** @deva[श] or a consonant of the palatal class, the corresponding letter of the latter class is substituted for the former, and @deva[श] for @deva[स्];[^1] @deva[हरिस्] + @deva[शेते] = @deva[हरिश्शेते] Hari sleeps; @deva[रामः] + @deva[चिनोति] = @deva[रामश्चिनोति] Rāma collects: @deva[सत्] + @deva[चित्] = @deva[सच्चित्] Reality and knowledge; @deva[शान्तिं] + @deva[जय] = @deva[शान्तिञ्जय] Be you victorious, Oh Kṛṣṇa.

**Exception:** (1) Dentals coming after @deva[श्] are not changed into the corresponding palatals: e.g. @deva[विश्र्यः] that which spreads lustre; @deva[श्राः].

**(b)** @deva[ट] or a consonant of the lingual class, @deva[ष] is substituted for @deva[स्] and the corresponding letter of the latter class for the former; @deva[रामस्] + @deva[षष्ठः] = @deva[रामष्षष्ठः] Rāma who is sixth. @deva[रामस्] + @deva[टीकते] = @deva[रामष्टीकते] Rāma goes; @deva[षट्] + @deva[टीका] = @deva[षट्टीका] A commentary on that; @deva[कृष्णस्] + @deva[टीकसे] = @deva[कृष्णष्टीकसे] Oh Kṛṣṇa, thou goest; @deva[पेष्] + @deva[ता] = @deva[पेष्टा] one who pounds.

**Exception:** (1) If a letter of the lingual class coming at the end of a word be followed by @deva[स्] or a letter of the dental class, the @deva[स्] as well as the dentals except the @deva[त्] of @deva[तात्], @deva[नवति] and @deva[मति] remain unaffected; @deva[षट्] + @deva[सन्तः] = @deva[षट्सन्तः] six good men; (see also @ref[39].); @deva[षट्] + @deva[ते] = @deva[षट्ते] these six; but @deva[षष्टुते] (since the @deva[ष] of @deva[षष्टु] is not at the end of a word) he praises. So @deva[षण्णवतिः] Ninety-six, @deva[षण्णगराः] six towns; but @deva[सर्पिस्] + @deva[तत्त्व] = @deva[सर्पिष्टत्त्व] abundance of ghee; as the exception excludes @deva[स्].

---

[^1]: @deva[स्तोः श्चुना श्चुः। च टि। ष्टुना ष्टुः] — Pāṇ. VIII. 4. 40. 44, 41.
