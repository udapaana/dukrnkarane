## Principal Conjunctions in Sanskrit

The following are the principal conjunctions in Sanskrit:

### (a) Copulative

@deva[अथ], @deva[अथो], @deva[उत], @deva[च], @deva[किञ्च], &c.

### (b) Disjunctive

@deva[वा], @deva[वा...वा], &c.

### (c) Adversative

@deva[अथवा], @deva[तु], @deva[किन्तु], @deva[किन्वा], &c.

### (d) Conditional

@deva[चेत्], @deva[यदि], @deva[यदापि], @deva[नेत्], @deva[नोचेत्], @deva[वेद्] (used in sacrificial ceremonies), &c.

### (e) Causal

@deva[हि], @deva[तत्], @deva[तेन], &c.

### (f) Interrogative

@deva[आहो], @deva[अहोस्विद्], @deva[उत], @deva[उताहो], @deva[कि], @deva[किन्नु], @deva[किन्तु], @deva[किञ्चित्], @deva[नञु], @deva[नघा], @deva[नु], &c.

### (g) Affirmatives and Negatives

@deva[अहो], @deva[अथ किम्], @deva[आम्], @deva[अद्धा], &c.

### (h) Conjunctions of Time

@deva[यावत्—तावत्], @deva[यदा], @deva[तदा], &c.

### (i) Beginning and End Markers

@deva[अथ] and @deva[इति] are used to mark the beginning and the close of a work respectively.
