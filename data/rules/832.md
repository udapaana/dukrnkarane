## Accusative/Dative with Motion Verbs (Actual Motion)

The object of roots implying motion (i.e. the place to which the motion is directed), if it be not a road, is put in the Accusative or the Dative, when actual motion is meant; @deva[ग्रामं ग्रामाय वा गच्छति] 'goes to a village;' but @deva[मनसा हरिं भजति], @deva[पुरुस्त्रीं गच्छति].[^1]
