When @deva[अ] or @deva[आ] is followed by @deva[ए] or @deva[ऐ] and @deva[ओ] or @deva[औ], @deva[ऐ] and @deva[औ] are respectively substituted for both;[^1] @deva[छुन्न] + @deva[एकान्त] = @deva[छुन्नैकान्त्य]; @deva[देव] + @deva[ऐश्वर्य] = @deva[दैवैश्वर्य] the majesty of god; @deva[सा] + @deva[एव] = @deva[सैव] she alone; @deva[भव] + @deva[औषधम्] = @deva[भवौषधम्] medicine against birth and re-birth. @deva[विद्या] + @deva[औत्सुक्यं] = @deva[विद्यौत्सुक्यं] longing for knowledge.

**Exceptions:**

**(a)** If a preposition ending in @deva[अ] be followed by a root beginning with @deva[ए] or @deva[औ], @deva[ए] or @deva[औ] is substituted;[^2] @deva[प्र] + @deva[एजते] = @deva[प्रेजते] quakes very much; @deva[उप] + @deva[ओषति] = @deva[उपोषति] burns something near; but if the root be a denominative, vṛddhi is optional; @deva[उप] + @deva[एककीयति] = @deva[उपेककीयति] or @deva[उपैककीयति]; @deva[प्र] + @deva[औषीयति] = @deva[प्रोषीयति] or @deva[प्रौषीयति].

**Counter-exception:** If a form of the root @deva[√इ] to go, or @deva[एध] to grow, with an initial @deva[ए], comes after @deva[अ], and @deva[इष] or @deva[इष्य] derived from @deva[इष्] of the 4th, 6th or 9th conj., after @deva[प्र], vṛddhi takes place; @deva[अधि] + @deva[एति] = @deva[अध्यैति]; @deva[अधि] + @deva[एषते] = @deva[अध्यैषते]; (but @deva[अधि] + @deva[इतः] = @deva[अध्यितः]; @deva[अवेषि] know; from @deva[अव] + @deva[एषि] which is equal to @deva[अव] + @deva[आ] + @deva[इषि], and not @deva[अवैषि]; या सञ्जाता; @deva[प्र] + @deva[एषिषत्] = @deva[प्रैषिषत्]); @deva[प्र] + @deva[एषः] = @deva[प्रैषः] sending or directing; @deva[प्र] + @deva[एष्यः] = @deva[प्रैष्यः] a servant. But @deva[प्रेष्य], @deva[प्रेष्यः] (from @deva[ईष्]).

**(b)** When @deva[अ] is followed by @deva[एष] in an indefinite sense, @deva[ए] is substituted for both:[^3] @deva[क] + @deva[एष] = @deva[केष भोक्ष्यते] where will you dine to-day? (The place being not definitely known); but @deva[तत्रैव] (why, I shall dine at your house).

**(c)** @deva[अ] followed by @deva[ओम्] and @deva[आ] (prep.) is dropped[^4] e.g. @deva[शिवाय] + @deva[ओं नमः] = @deva[शिवायों नमः]; @deva[शिव] + @deva[एहि] (@deva[आ] + @deva[इहि]) = @deva[शिवेहि].

**(d)** The vṛddhi is optional when the ending @deva[अ] of a word is followed by @deva[ओष्ठ] a cat, or @deva[ओष्ठ] the lower lip, in a compound:[^5] @deva[स्थूल] + @deva[ओष्ठः] = @deva[स्थूलौष्ठः] or @deva[स्थूलोष्ठः]; @deva[विम्बोष्ठः], @deva[विम्बोष्ठः].

**(e)** The final vowel or the final consonant with the preceding vowel is dropped in the case of the following words when followed by certain words in a compound:[^6][^7] @deva[शाक] the country of the Śakas + @deva[अम्बु] a well = @deva[शाकम्बु]; @deva[कम्बू] name of a country + @deva[अम्बु] = @deva[कम्बम्बु]; @deva[कुल] + @deva[अटा] = @deva[कुलटा] one who moves from one mansion to another, a woman of bad character; @deva[सीमन्] + @deva[अन्तः] = @deva[सीमन्तः] (the white line left by the parting of the hair on each side of the head); but @deva[सीम्नान्तः] when the meaning of the word is 'The last line or limit of a boundary.' @deva[मनस्] + @deva[ईश] = @deva[मनीश] wish; so @deva[लाङ्गलीश] the pole of a plough; @deva[हलीश:]; @deva[पतत्] + @deva[अञ्जलिः] = @deva[पतञ्जलिः] name of the celebrated author of the Mahābhāṣya (a vast commentary on the sūtras of Pāṇini); lit. 'fit to be adored with joined hands; or according to tradition 'who fell down from the hands of a certain sage as he was offering water to the sun at the time of Sandhyā adoration.' @deva[शार] + @deva[अङ्कः] = @deva[शारङ्कः] an antelope with a spotted skin or a kind of bird; but @deva[शाराङ्कः] one with a beautiful skin. This is an Ākṛtigaṇa i.e. words of similar formation and requiring grammatical explanation but not actually found in the gaṇa may be classed under it; e.g. @deva[मृत] + @deva[अण्डः] = @deva[मार्तण्डः] (derived from @deva[मृताण्ड]) lit. born of a dead egg, the sun.
