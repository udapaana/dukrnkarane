The words @deva[ऋच्], @deva[डर्], @deva[अप्], @deva[धुर्] when is does not mean 'the yoke of a carriage', and @deva[पथ्],[^1] at the end of any compound take the suffix @deva[अ];[^2] @deva[अर्धर्चं-चैक] 'half a Rik'; @deva[विष्णुपुरं][^3] 'the town of Vishnu;' @deva[विमलाप् सरः] 'a lake with pure water;' @deva[राज्यधुरा] 'the yoke of a kingdom', i. e. 'the heavy responsibilities of governing it' &c. @deva[रम्यपथो देशः] 'a country with pleasant roads', &c.

**(a)** But in the case of @deva[ऋच्] preceded by @deva[अन्] or @deva[बहु] the @deva[अ] is added only when the compound denotes a student of the Rig Veda[^4]; @deva[अनृचः] 'one who does not study the Rig Veda;' @deva[बहूचः] 'one that has studied the Rig Veda;' but @deva[अनृक् साम], 'the Sâma Veda that contains no riks,' @deva[बहूक् छन्दम्] 'a hymn consisting of many riks.'

**(b)** @deva[सुर] when it refers to @deva[अक्ष] 'a car' does not take this @deva[अ]; @deva[अक्षरूः] the yoke of a carriage; @deva[हङ्रूः अक्षः]

---

[^1]: Substituted for @deva[पथिन्].

[^2]: @deva[ऋच्डरपथुरामानधे] Pân. V. 4. 14.

[^3]: @deva[कौत्सवै लोकात्] Sid. Kau.

[^4]: @deva[भन्ऋचबहूचावध्येतर्यैव] Sid. Kau.
