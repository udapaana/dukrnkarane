[^1] When disbelief in or intolerance of an action is intended to be expressed, and especially in the form of a question, the Simple Future is used optionally in the sense of the Potential; @deva[न तं कोऽपि] or @deva[न समानः भवान् हरिं निन्दति] or @deva[निन्दिष्यति] I never believe, or cannot tolerate it, that you would or should speak ill of Hari; @deva[कः] or @deva[कस्त्वः कथं हरिं निन्दति] or @deva[निन्दिष्यति] who will speak ill of Hari (I do not believe that any body will, &c.); @deva[कदा याजनं भवान् याजयेत्] or @deva[याजयिष्यति], &c.; when the word @deva[किंकर्तव्यम्] [^2] (a particle, showing great anger) and roots having the sense of 'to be' precede, the Simple Future only should be used; @deva[स नं तवभानो भवान् यज्ञेन शूद्रान् सत्करिष्यति] I do not believe or like that you should make a Śudra perform a sacrifice; so @deva[अतथं भवतो विपदं वा भवान् इमेन याजयिष्यति].

[^1]: @deva[विदानने लिट्लुट्युटौ च। अविनिश्चयार्थे किमच्विभिनै।] Pāṇ. III. 3.144,145.

[^2]: @deva[किंकालस्यात्युदन्तत्भावेषु लुट्] । Pāṇ. III. 3. 146.
