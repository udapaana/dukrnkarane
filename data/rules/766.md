The gerund in @deva[अम्] of the root @deva[कृ] is used[^1]

(a) with the noun governed by it, if it be compounded with it prepositionally when censure is implied; @deva[चौरकाराम् आक्रोशति] (@deva[चौरकृत्य उच्चैः स्वरेण]) 'cries out (he is) a thief, a thief.' In this case @deva[य] is added to the noun governed.

(b) with the words @deva[स्वाद्], @deva[छण], and @deva[संस्याद्], @deva[य] being added to these as before; @deva[आस्वाद्यकाराम् स्वादु छत्वा पचति], @deva[स्वाद्यकारम्], @deva[छणयकारम्]—@deva[संस्यादयकाराम् पचति] 'he eats having sweetened or seasoned his food.'

(c) with the words @deva[अन्वया], @deva[एवम्], @deva[किम्], and @deva[कथम्], provided the root @deva[य] takes its sense; @deva[अन्वयाकाराम् वक्ति] 'he speaks in a coherent manner;' @deva[एवं कृत्वा] he acts thus;' @deva[किंकाराम्], @deva[कथंकाराम् वक्ति], but @deva[शनैर्वदित्वा स्वाति मुञ्चति].

(d) with the particles @deva[यथम्] and @deva[यथा] when an angry reply is given; @deva[यथमकाराम् भोक्ष्यं यथमकाराम् भोक्ष्यं किं तवानेन] (Sid. Kau.); 'I will eat in this manner, I will eat in that manner, what have you do with that?'
