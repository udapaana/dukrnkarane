[^1] With interrogatives the Present is often used in the sense of the Future and has also to be regarded as implying @deva[किं कु] i. e. (@deva[करिष्यामि]), @deva[किं गच्छामि] i. e. (@deva[गमिष्यामि]) What shall I do? Whither shall I go? @deva[एतेषां कतमं] or @deva[एतेषां किमेव भोजयसि] (i. e. @deva[भोजयिष्यसि] or @deva[भोजयितुमिच्छसि]) which of these persons will you feed? so @deva[को नु विदधाति प्राणान्], &c.; but @deva[कः प्राप्नोति मनोरथम्]।

(a) It is also used in the sense of the Future in conditional sentences as implying a condition of the occurrence of the desired object; @deva[अन्नं ददाति] (@deva[दास्यति दद्यात् वा]) @deva[स स्वर्गं याति] (@deva[यास्यति वा]) he, who offers or (will offer) food, goes (or will go) to heaven i. e. if one offers &c.

[^1]: @deva[किमर्थे लिप्लुङ्लोटः। लिपः स्यातामनवश्ये च।] Pāṇ. III. 3, 6. 7.
