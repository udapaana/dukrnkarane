The following words have an @deva[अ] added to them when at the end of a Tatpurusha compound:—

**(a) गो** except when a Tad. aff. is added and then dropped;[^1] e. g. @deva[परमगवः] an excellent bull, @deva[पञ्चगवम्]-( a collection of five cows ) @deva[धनः], but @deva[द्विगुः] exchanged or bartered for two cows.

**(b)**[^2] @deva[उरच्] meaning 'chief or eminent'. @deva[अश्वानां उर एव अश्वोरसम्] 'the chief of horses i. e. an excellent horse.'

**(c)**[^3] The words @deva[अनच्] @deva[अह्नच्], @deva[अपच्] and @deva[सच्] when they denote a class or form names; @deva[उपानसम्] ( @deva[उपवर्त्यते अन:] ) 'a cart-load,' @deva[महानसः] 'a kitchen'; @deva[अह्नतामः] 'a kind of stone like the moon-stone' [ in this case the final @deva[अन्] is dropped ]; @deva[कालायसम्] 'black iron', @deva[मण्डूककरसम्] 'a lake full of frogs'; @deva[जलरसम्] 'a lake of that name.'

**(d)**[^4] @deva[नौ] at the end of a Dwigu, except when the Tad. affix is dropped ), as @deva[द्राख्मौ नौः पञ्चगासमगाः द्विनावख्यः] ( when the aff. i-not dropped ), @deva[द्विनावः] 'a collection of two boats', so @deva[त्रिनावम्] &c; but @deva[पञ्चभिः नौभिः क्रीतः पञ्चनौः], also when it is preceded by @deva[अर्थ]; as @deva[नावः अर्थेन अर्थनावम्] the neuter is irregular ( @deva[लौकात्] Sid. Kan. ).

**(e)**[^5] @deva[शारी] ( a measure of corn ) under similar circumstances takes the @deva[अ] optionally, the final @deva[ई] being dropped before it: @deva[द्विशारय-रि:] @deva[अर्धशारय-रि].

**(f)**[^6] @deva[अञ्जलि], preceded by @deva[द्वि] or @deva[त्रि] in a Dwigu, optionally takes @deva[अ], except when the Tad. aff. is dropped, before which the final @deva[इ] is dropped; @deva[द्व्यञ्जल्-द्वि] 'two handfuls'; but @deva[द्राख्याञ्जल-लिभ्यां क्रीतः पञ्चजलिः]

---

[^1]: @deva[गोतडितलुकि] Pāṇ. V. 4. 92.

[^2]: @deva[उरच्] meaning 'chief or eminent'.

[^3]: The words @deva[अनच्] @deva[अह्नच्], @deva[अपच्] and @deva[सच्] when they denote a class or form names.

[^4]: @deva[नौ] at the end of a Dwigu, except when the Tad. affix is dropped.

[^5]: @deva[शारी] ( a measure of corn ) under similar circumstances.

[^6]: @deva[अञ्जलि], preceded by @deva[द्वि] or @deva[त्रि] in a Dwigu.
