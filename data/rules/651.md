## Nominal Verbs with णिच् Affix - Vowel Changes

The penultimate @deva[अ] of a noun ending in a nasal is lengthened. The base is treated like a root of the last class, its last vowel only taking Guṇa before @deva[त], etc. (terminations of the Parasmaipada):

### Examples

@deva[विष्णु] @deva[इव आचरति]—@deva[अति] 'he acts like Vishṇu'

@deva[कृष्ण]—@deva[कृष्णाति] 'he acts like Kṛṣṇa' (@deva[कृष्णाति] 1st sing.)

@deva[कवि]—@deva[कवयामि] 'I act the poet or behave like a poet'

@deva[विड्]—@deva[विड्यति] 'he behaves like a bird'

@deva[माला]—@deva[मालाति] 'he or it acts like a garland' (Perf. @deva[मालाञ्च], etc.)

@deva[पितृ]—@deva[पिताति] 'he acts like a father'

@deva[भू]—@deva[भूवति] 'acts like the earth' (Perf. @deva[चुभूव], etc. as @deva[भूव] is the base)

@deva[राजन्]—@deva[राजनाति] 'he acts like a king'

@deva[पथिन्]—@deva[पयातति] 'it serves as a road', etc.

So @deva[इदमति] from @deva[इदम्], @deva[ऋत्विगस्त्रुति] from @deva[ऋत्विगश्व] (name of Indra)

### (b) Optional Affixes

The words @deva[असुगल] (a bold man), @deva[शीड] (a child) and @deva[विडाल] take the affixes @deva[स्] and @deva[णिच्] optionally and are conjugated in the Ātmanepada:

@deva[असुगलीते] or @deva[असुगलायते]

@deva[शीडते] or @deva[शीडायते]

@deva[विडाते] or @deva[विडायते]
