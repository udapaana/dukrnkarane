The vowel of the first member is lengthened in the following cases:—[^1]

**(a)** When the nouns derived from the roots @deva[नह्], @deva[सद्], @deva[इष्], @deva[व्यध्], @deva[रुच्], @deva[सह्], and @deva[तन्] with the aff. @deva[क्विन्] ( o ) follow prepositions termed Gati or nouns which they govern; @deva[उपनाह्], @deva[नीवृद्] an inhabited country, a realm, @deva[वीवृद्] the-rainy season, @deva[मर्माविद्] piercing the vitals; so @deva[मृगविद्] a hunter (cf. Bhaṭṭi II. 7). @deva[नीरुह्], @deva[अभीरुह्], @deva[शत्रूरुद्] overpowering an assailant @deva[परीसह्]. But @deva[परिधावनम्] as @deva[नह्] is not followed by Kvip.

**(b)** When followed by the aff. @deva[वल], and the whole is a name; @deva[कषीवलः] a husbandman.

**(c)** Words containing more than two vowels when followed by @deva[वत्] (@deva[मत्]) except in the case of @deva[अजिर], @deva[रविर], @deva[क्षिणिन], @deva[इरिच], @deva[कारणवत्] and @deva[चरणवाक] and the whole is a name; @deva[अमरावती] and @deva[इरावती] which are names; but @deva[अजिरवती], @deva[कृदिमती], @deva[बहुमती] which is not a name. Also in the case of @deva[हर], @deva[वंश], @deva[भूम], @deva[अति], @deva[कपि], @deva[तनि], @deva[शुचि], and @deva[इण]; @deva[इरावती] &c.

**(d)** When a verbal derivative with the aff. @deva[अ] (@deva[घञ्]) follows a preposition, in most cases, when the comp. does not signify a human being; @deva[परिधानम्]:-@deva[परीपाकः]; but @deva[निषादः] one of a mountain-tribe. Similarly @deva[प्रति-सी-वंशः]; @deva[प्रति-सी-कारः] &c.

**(e)** When @deva[काश] follows a preposition ending in @deva[इ]; @deva[नीकाशः], @deva[नीकाशः]; but @deva[प्रकाशः]

**(f)** When @deva[अट्टन्] is the first, and @deva[नर्] the second member of a compound, and the whole is a name; @deva[अटानर]: gold, @deva[अष्टानरः] a spider; but @deva[अष्टउत्तरः], @deva[विद्यातरः] an epithet of Savitṛi.

**(g)** When @deva[मित्र] is the latter member and the whole is the name of a ṛishi; @deva[विश्वामित्रः]; but @deva[विश्वामित्रो माणवकः] ।
