The pronouns @deva[अस्मद्], @deva[युष्मद्], @deva[यद्] and @deva[किम्] are often used in combination with other pronouns; @deva[कोहं]—@deva[रघूणामन्वयं वक्ष्ये] that I will describe the race of the Raghu; @deva[कोहं सर्वस्मात्तो लोके] that I am the most degraded of all the people; @deva[स त्वं मद्गृहस्थे मद्गृहे]—@deva[अस्मद्गृहे]—@deva[वस] that thou dwelling in my fire-sanctuary; &c.; @deva[ते वयं दमयन्त्यायाः प्रार्थयन्तिमाग्रह] we, of this description, cannot over the earth; for (in search of) Damaryanṭi sometimes @deva[अस्मद्] and @deva[युष्मद्] may be understood; @deva[सा क्षिप्रमासनं रथं गजं वा] i. e. @deva[सा त्वं] that thou quickly sit in a chariot or mount an elephant, @deva[एषं पुत्त्रस्तव यद्गजेन्द्रो वाहुग्रं वाहनं दिग्जम्] this is that son of thine, the mother of elephants shaking water; @deva[तथा विनाकृतः पुत्रोबहुस्यमपि जीवितुम्][^1], who wish to live still, even though deprived of my sons in that manner, &c. &c.
