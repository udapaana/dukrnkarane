@deva[नुम्] is inserted in the case of the following compounds:—[^1]

**(a)** @deva[अपर] followed by @deva[पर] when continuity of an action is implied; @deva[अपरंपराः साङ्करा गच्छन्ति] । @deva[सततमाविच्छेदेन गच्छन्तीत्यर्थः]; but @deva[अपरपरा गच्छन्ति] । @deva[अपरे च परे च साङ्केष गच्छन्तीत्यर्थः] । and in the case of @deva[आ + श्चर्य] when wonder is meant; @deva[आश्चर्यं यदि उ दुर्जीते] । But @deva[आश्चर्ये कर्म दोषमयम्] ।

**(b)** @deva[अवकीर्णते इति अवस्करः] when it means @deva[वर्जितं] ( @deva[व्रन्तितं वर्ज्यं वर्जनीयसमतलम्] । Sid. Kau.) otherwise @deva[अवस्करः]; @deva[अपस्करः] when it means @deva[रथाङ्ग] 'part of a carriage'; @deva[विषिन्क] ( or @deva[विशिन्कः] ) 'a bird'. @deva[प्रतिष्कन्दः] ( @deva[संजातः पुरोगामी वा] Sid. Kau. ), but @deva[प्रतिकशः कशां प्रतिकशः] 'one who boldly faces the whip, a disobedient servant' &c. @deva[मस्करः] 'a bamboo', but @deva[मकरः] 'a shark', @deva[मस्करिन्] 'an ascetic' but @deva[मकरिन्] 'the sea'; @deva[कारस्करः] when it means 'a particular kind of tree,' otherwise @deva[कारस्करः] ।

**(c)** in the case of the words of the @deva[पारस्करादि] group, such as @deva[पारस्करः], @deva[किंस्कुः], @deva[किंस्कुना], &c. when they are names.

**(d)** and when the words @deva[तस्] and @deva[इन्द्र] are follow d by @deva[कर] and @deva[पति] and the meaning is 'a thief', and 'a deity' respectively, and @deva[र] and @deva[न] being dropped before @deva[ष]; @deva[तस्करः], @deva[इन्द्रपतिः]. Also @deva[प्रायश्चित्तम्], @deva[प्रायश्चित्तिः], @deva[वनस्पतिः] &c.
