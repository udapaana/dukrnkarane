## Formation of Perfect Participles

The participle of the Perfect Parasmaipada and Ātmanepada is formed by adding @deva[वस्] and @deva[आन] generally to that form of the root which it assumes before the termination of the 3rd person plural.

### Rules for इ Insertion

If this form consists of one syllable only or when the root ends in @deva[आ], @deva[वस्] has the intermediate @deva[इ] added to it.

@deva[इ] is optionally prefixed to @deva[वस्] in the case of the roots @deva[गम्], @deva[इय्], @deva[हुव्], @deva[विश्] and @deva[विद्य्] (6 cl.).

@deva[चक्] and @deva[स्पर्], and @deva[गुह्] and @deva[हृत्] when they do not take @deva[इ], form this participle from that base which they would assume before the 2nd person singular termination.

### Special Forms

@deva[जन्] has @deva[जजन्वस्]

@deva[सद्य]—@deva[चसह्वस्]

@deva[गभ्]—@deva[जग्मिवस्] or @deva[जगन्वस्]

@deva[हन्]—@deva[जघनिवस्] or @deva[जघन्वस्]

### (I) Roots Beginning with अ

In the case of roots beginning with @deva[अ], @deva[व] is not inserted, as it is in the Perfect: @deva[अञ्जु]—@deva[आञ्जिवस्]

For the declension of these participles see @ref[124].

These are declined like nouns ending in @deva[अ] m., f., n.
