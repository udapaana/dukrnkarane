The consonants are divided into @deva[स्पर्श] (@[sparśa]) or mutes (those involving a complete closure or contact and not an approximate one of the organs of pronunciation), @deva[अन्तस्थ] (@[antastha]) (or intermediate, i.e. the Semivowels,) and @deva[ऊष्मन्] (@[ūṣman]) or sibilants.

These are represented by thirty-three syllabic signs arranged as below:—

**(a) Mutes:**

(1) @deva[कवर्ग] or the group @deva[क]—@deva[क] @[k], @deva[ख] @[kh], @deva[ग] @[g], @deva[घ] @[gh], @deva[ङ] @[ṅ].

(2) @deva[चवर्ग] or the group @deva[च]—@deva[च] @[c], @deva[छ] @[ch], @deva[ज] @[j], @deva[झ] @[jh], @deva[ञ] @[ñ].

(3) @deva[टवर्ग] or the group @deva[ट]—@deva[ट] @[ṭ], @deva[ठ] @[ṭh], @deva[ड] @[ḍ], @deva[ढ] @[ḍh], @deva[ण] @[ṇ].

(4) @deva[तवर्ग] or the group @deva[त]—@deva[त] @[t], @deva[थ] @[th], @deva[द] @[d], @deva[ध] @[dh], @deva[न] @[n].

(5) @deva[पवर्ग] or the group @deva[प]—@deva[प] @[p], @deva[फ] @[ph], @deva[ब] @[b], @deva[भ] @[bh], @deva[म] @[m].

These are also called the five classes designated as @deva[कवर्ग] (@[kavarga]), @deva[चवर्ग] (@[cavarga]), @deva[टवर्ग] (@[ṭavarga]), @deva[तवर्ग] (@[tavarga]), and @deva[पवर्ग] (@[pavarga]) respectively.

**(b) Semivowels**

@deva[य] @[y], @deva[र] @[r], @deva[ल] @[l], @deva[व] @[v].

**(c) Sibilants**

@deva[श] @[ś], @deva[ष] @[ṣ], @deva[स] @[s].

**(d) Sonant Aspirate**

@deva[ह] @[h].

Besides these we have two more characters occurring in the Veda, viz. @deva[ळ] and @deva[ऴ] (often substituted for @deva[ल] and @deva[ळ]; as @deva[कॆळ] for @deva[कॆलॆ], @deva[सीळूरॆ] for @deva[सीलूरॆ] &c.). In Marāthi @deva[ळ] is generally substituted for the final @deva[ल] of Sanskrit words.
