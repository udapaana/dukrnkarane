A noun in the Dative is compounded[^1]—

**(a)** with another expressive of the material of which the thing expressed by the noun in the Dative case is made; as @deva[यूपाय दारु यूपदारु], 'wood for a sacrificial post.'

**(b)** and with the words @deva[अर्थ], @deva[बलि], @deva[हित], @deva[सुख] and @deva[रक्षित]. The compound with @deva[अर्थ] is a nityasamāsa and takes the gender and number of the noun it qualifies; @deva[द्विजाय अर्थं द्विजार्थः सूपः] broth for a Brāhmana; @deva[द्विजाय बलिं द्विजायै यवागूः] ( water-gruel ) @deva[द्विजाय बलिं द्विजायै पयः]; @deva[भूतेभ्यो बलिः भूतबलिः] an offering to the bhūtas( certain created beings ); @deva[गवे हितं गोहितम्] good for a cow; @deva[गवे सुखं गोसुखम्]; @deva[गवे रक्षितं गोरक्षितम्].
