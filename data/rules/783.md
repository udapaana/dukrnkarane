## Verb Concord with Multiple Subjects (and/or)

(a) When two or more subjects differing in number are connected by 'and' the verb must be plural; @deva[ततः कुन्ती च राजा च भीष्मः सह बन्धुभिः । ददुः श्राद्धं तदा पाण्डोः] &c. Mah. Bha. Sometimes the verb agrees with the nearest subject in number, @deva[सा च सत्यवती देवी गान्धारी च यशस्विनी । राजदारैः परिवृता गान्धारी चापि निर्ययौ ॥] Mah. Bhā. @deva[अहश्च रात्रिश्च उभे च सन्ध्ये घनोऽपि जानाति नरस्य वृत्तम् ॥]

(b) But when they are connected by 'or' and are all sing. the verb will be singular, and when the subject differ in number the verb will agree with the one nearest to it; @deva[रामः गोविन्दो वा व्रजतु] 'Let Rāma or Govinda go,' @deva[स वा इमे बालका वा] let him or these boys take the mango fruit.'
