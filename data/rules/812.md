## Instrumental Case: Agent and Instrument

The Instrumental case primarily expresses either the agent (when the construction is passive) or the instrument or means by which an action is done; @deva[तव महिमानं अज्ञात्वा मया अस्युत्तोऽऽसि] thou wast disrespected by me, not knowing thy greatness; @deva[रामेण बाणेन हतो वाली] Vāli was killed by Rāma with an arrow (here @deva[रामेण] is the agent; @deva[बाणेन] the instrument).[^1]

(a) The Instrumental is also used in the following senses. @deva[रुपेण दर्शनेन] lovely by nature, @deva[यावत् यात्मकः] he is almost pitiable; @deva[विप्रोभः गार्ग्यः] by family name; @deva[रणे पति कत्यति] he buys with ease; @deva[सो सुलभेन], @deva[विवक्षति], &c @deva[द्विद्रोणेन यान्यं कोणति] he buys two drones of corn at a time; @deva[सहस्रेण यसुयत् कोणति] he buys one thousand beasts at a time, &c.

(b) In the case of numerals and words expressive of measure the Acc. may also be used; @deva[द्विद्रोणं कोणति यान्यं], @deva[शतं घोटकं यान्यं हतं वसुयर घरेयं], &c.

(c) Obs.—The root @deva[दिव्] 'to play,' however, governs the Accusative or the Instrumental of the instrument used; @deva[अक्षैः यक्षानरु वा दीव्यति] he plays at dice.

(d) The root @deva[ज्ञा] with @deva[सम्] also governs the Accusative or the Instrumental; @deva[पितरं पितरा वा संज्ञानीते] he recognises or lives in peace with his father, but @deva[विष्णुं संजानीयाच्च] remember Viṣṇu.[^2]
