Two adjectives may also be compounded into what is called the @deva[विशेषणोभयपदकर्मधारयः] as @deva[शुक्लकृष्णः], @deva[कृष्णसारंगः].

**(a).**[^1] Two participles expressive of two consecutive actions of the same individual may be compounded together, the one expressive of the previous action being placed first; @deva[आदौ स्नातः पश्चादनुलिप्तः स्नातानुलिप्तः] 'first bathed and then anointed'; similarly @deva[पीतोद्गीर्णश] 'first drunk and afterwards vomited out' @deva[पीतप्रतिबद्धः], @deva[पहीतप्रतिष्ठक:] ( Rag. II. 1. IV. 43 ), &c.

**(b).** In the case of the two sets of words given above ( @ref[222].g. ) forming compounds with one another those beginning with @deva[पूर्व] are placed first. In the case of @deva[एक] compounded with @deva[वीर] it may precede or follow it; as @deva[वीरैक:] or @deva[एकवीर:]; the former, however, is preferable. In the case of the first set of words viz. @deva[एक]......@deva[केवल] the one succeeding precedes the one preceding when compounded together; as @deva[पुराणनवत्], @deva[केवलपु-राणम्], &c.

**(c)**[^2] A past participle is compounded with another with the negative particle prefixed to it; '@deva[कृताकृतम्],' what is done and not done i. e. 'partially done'.

**(d).**[^3] The word @deva[युवन्] ( m. f. ) enters into a Karm. comp. with the words @deva[खलति], @deva[पलित], @deva[वलित] having wrinkles, and @deva[जरती], and is placed first; i. e. @deva[युवा+खलतिः युवखलतिः] 'a young bald-headed man', @deva[जटी] 'a young bald-headed woman'; @deva[युवजरती] 'a young woman looking old'; @deva[युवपलितः] 'a young man whose hair is grey', &c.
