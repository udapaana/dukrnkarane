Roots having @deva[अ] for their penultimate and ending in a consonant of the labial class take the affix @deva[य]; @deva[शाप्]—@deva[शाप्यः]; @deva[दुष्]—@deva[दुष्यः]; @deva[रप्]—@deva[रप्यः], &c.

(a) When @deva[वध्] is preceded by @deva[अपि], @deva[य्] (changed to @deva[इ]) is inserted between @deva[इ] and @deva[प्]; @deva[अपिवधो]—@deva[आपेधयः] 'what ought or is fit to be killed;' @deva[य] is also inserted when @deva[द्रुष्] is preceded by @deva[उप] if the meaning be 'to praise;' @deva[उपद्रुष्यः साधुः] 'a Sādhu ought to be praised;' but @deva[उपकुष्यं धनं] 'wealth ought to be acquired.'
