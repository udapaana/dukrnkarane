## Interjections Expressing Emotions

"The interjection is not properly a part of speech, as it does not enter into the construction of sentences. It is a sudden exclamation prompted by some strong feeling or emotion. There are various utterances suited to the different emotions of the mind": Prof. Bain.

### (a) Wonder, Grief, or Regret

These are @deva[अः], @deva[इ], @deva[उ], @deva[ए], @deva[ऐ], @deva[ओ], @deva[अहो], @deva[अहहो], @deva[अहह], @deva[बत], @deva[ह], @deva[हा], @deva[हाहा], &c. expressive of wonder, grief or regret, &c.

### (b) Contempt

@deva[धिक्], @deva[धिक्], &c. expressive of contempt.

### (c) Sorrow, Dejection

@deva[हा], @deva[बत], &c. expressing sorrow, dejection, &c.

### (d) Grief

@deva[हा], @deva[हाहा], @deva[हन्त] showing grief.

### (e) Anger, Contempt

@deva[आ], @deva[हुम्], @deva[हुम्], &c. expressive of anger, contempt, &c.

### (f) Joy

@deva[हन्त], &c. expressive of joy.

### (g) Calling Attention

There are some interjections used to call attention. Of these:

**(1)** Some show respect; such as @deva[अरे], @deva[अये], @deva[अहो], @deva[अहह], @deva[बत], @deva[उ], @deva[ए], @deva[ओ], @deva[भोस्], @deva[भोः], @deva[हंहो], @deva[हे], @deva[हे], @deva[हो], &c.

**(2)** And others, disrespect or contempt; as @deva[अड], @deva[अरे], @deva[अबे], @deva[रे], @deva[रेरे], @deva[अरेरे], &c.

**(3)** The exclamations @deva[श्रौषट्], @deva[वौषट्] and @deva[वषट्] are used when offering oblations to gods or manes; and

**(4)** @deva[स्वाहा] and @deva[स्वधा] when offering oblations to gods and manes respectively.
