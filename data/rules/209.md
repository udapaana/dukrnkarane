A noun in the Ablative case is compounded—

**(a)** with words expressive of fear or rather with the words @deva[भय], @deva[भीत], @deva[भीति] and @deva[भी]:; as @deva[चोराद् भयं चोरभयम्] fear from a thief; @deva[वृकाद् भीतः वृकभीतः] afraid of a wolf, &c.

**(b)** in a few cases with the words @deva[अपेत], @deva[अपोढ], @deva[मुक्त], @deva[पतित], and @deva[अप्रवाह], as @deva[सुखादपेतः सुखापेतः] deprived of happiness; @deva[कल्पनाया अपोढः कल्पनापोढः] 'transcending thought' or 'a fool;' @deva[चक्रमुक्तः], @deva[स्वर्गपतितः] 'fallen from heaven, as a sinner'; @deva[तरङ्गाप्रवासः] 'afraid of waves'.

**(c)** in the case of the words @deva[स्तोक] a little, @deva[अन्तिक] near, @deva[दूर] after, and words having the sense of these; and @deva[कृच्छ्र] a difficulty, there is an Aluk of the Ablative; as @deva[स्तोकाद् मुक्तः स्तोकान्मुक्तः]; @deva[अल्पान्मुक्तः]; @deva[अन्तिकादागतः]; @deva[अभ्याशादागतः]; @deva[दूरादागतः]; @deva[विप्रकृष्टादागतः]; @deva[कृच्छ्रादागतः]
