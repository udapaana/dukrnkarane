# Chapter XI. Avyayas or Indeclinables

That in an Avyaya whose form remaining the same in all the genders, numbers and cases, undergoes no change.[^2] The Avyayas may be divided into simple and compound ones. The latter are treated of in the chapter on compounds (Avyayībhāvas and a few Bahuvrīhis and Tatpurusha).

The indeclinables comprise (I) Prepositions (II) Adverbs, (III) Particles, (IV) Conjunctions, and (V) Interjections.

Besides these there are in Sanskrit a few nouns having one inflection only which are treated as indeclinable (nipātas); e. g. @deva[अन्यत्] 'another (reason)', @deva[अस्तम्] 'setting', @deva[अस्ति] 'that exists', @deva[ओम्] 'the well-known sacred syllable om', @deva[चनस्] 'satisfaction, food', @deva[चाहु] 'coaxing', @deva[नमस्] 'a bow', @deva[नास्ति] 'non-existence', @deva[भूर्] 'the earth', @deva[भुवर्] 'the sky', @deva[बदि] 'the dark fortnight', @deva[यशस्] 'happiness', @deva[शुदि] or @deva[शुक्ति] 'the bright fortnight', @deva[संवत्] 'a year', @deva[स्वाहा] 'food offered to gods', @deva[स्वधा] 'food offered to the manes', @deva[स्वर्] 'heaven', @deva[स्वस्ति] 'happiness' &c.

## I. PREPOSITIONS

A preposition, styled 'Upasarga or Gati', in Sanskrit is an indeclinable word, having an independent meaning and prefixed to verbs and also to their derivatives. These prepositions modify, intensify and sometimes totally alter the senses of roots[^3]: e. g. @deva[प्रह] 'to strike', @deva[आह] 'to eat, to perform as a sacrifice', @deva[संह] 'to contract', @deva[विह] 'to sport', @deva[परिह] 'to avoid, &c.' Sometimes they are prefixed without any alteration in the sense.

The following are the prepositions (upasargas) commonly prefixed to roots:—

**अति**—'beyond, over'; @deva[अतिक्रमः] 'overstepping' or 'going beyond', @deva[अतिसर्जनं] 'a gift, &c.'

**अधि**—'over, above, &c.'; @deva[अधिगमः] 'going up, acquisition', @deva[अधिकारः] 'high office or power', @deva[अधिक्षेपः] 'censure', &c.

**अनु**—'after, behind, along, &c.' @deva[अनुक्रमणं] 'following', @deva[अनुच्छोति] 'imitation', @deva[अनुग्रहः] 'favour', &c.

**अप**—'away from, away.' @deva[अपनयनं] 'the act of taking away', @deva[अपह] 'to plunder, seize' &c., @deva[अपकारः] 'harm, wrong' &c.

**अधि** (sometimes **पि**)—'near to', 'over', 'taking to', &c.; @deva[अधिगम्] 'to be resolved into or reduced to',[^4] @deva[अधिधानं] or @deva[पिधानं] 'a covering', @deva[अध्ययः] 'destruction', &c.

This prep. is more commonly used as an independent adverb having a cumulative force in classical Sanskrit.

**अभि**—'towards, near to', &c; @deva[अभिगम्] 'to go towards', @deva[अभिजनः] 'noble descent or family', @deva[अभिमानः] 'self-respect', @deva[अभिरुच्] 'to defeat', &c.

**अव** (sometimes **व**; see **अधि** and **अव**, note)—'away, down', &c. @deva[अव]—or @deva[वगाह] 'to plunge into', @deva[अवतारः] 'descent', @deva[अवगतं] 'reproached', @deva[अवमन्] 'to disrespect', &c.

**आ**—'up to, towards, all round, a little', &c.; @deva[आच्छाद्] 'to cover all round', @deva[आकारः] 'form or shape (within due bounds)', @deva[आकाशः] 'that which shines all round' i. e. 'the ether'; @deva[आकम्प] 'to shake a little', &c.

**उद्** or **उत्**—'upon', &c.; @deva[उद्गम्] 'to go up', @deva[उद्यमः] 'industry', @deva[उत्सर्गः] 'pouring out, hence a gift, a general rule', &c.

**उप**—'near to, towards, by the side of', &c.; @deva[उपगा] 'to go near to', @deva[उपकृति] f. 'an obligation', @deva[उपरति] f. 'death', @deva[उपस्थानं] 'praise, worship', @deva[उपमिति] f. 'comparison', &c.

**दुस्** or **दुर्**—'bad, hard to be done', &c.; @deva[दुराचारः] 'bad conduct', @deva[दुष्कर] 'hard to be done', @deva[दुःसह] 'difficult to be borne', &c.

**नि**—'in, into, great, opposed to', &c.; @deva[निड्] 'to insult', @deva[निकेत] 'a house', @deva[निचय] 'a heap, a great collection', @deva[निपीत] 'drunk in', @deva[निदेश] 'a command', &c.

**निस्** or **निर्**—'out of, away from, without', &c.; @deva[निःसृ] 'to issue out', @deva[निर्गम] 'a passage out', @deva[निर्दोषः] 'out of i. e. free from blame', @deva[निः शंक] 'without doubt', &c.

**परा**—'away, back, opposed to', &c.; @deva[पराकृ] 'to reject, to despise', @deva[पराक्रम] 'to act bravely', @deva[परागत] 'gone away', @deva[पराञ्च] 'turn back', @deva[पराजय] 'what is opposed to victory, defeat', &c.

**परि**—'all round, about', &c.; @deva[परिधा] 'to place all round i. e. to put on or wear', @deva[परिधिः] 'a wall &c. that surrounds', @deva[परिणामः] 'ripening, maturity', @deva[परिगणना] 'counting all round, i. e. a complete enumeration', &c.

**प्र**—(sense to be added from context)

**प्रति**—'towards, back, in return, in opposition to', &c.; @deva[प्रतिगम्] 'to go towards', @deva[प्रतिभाषण] 'a speech in return, an answer', @deva[प्रति—वि—कारः] 'an act in opposition to i. e. a remedy' &c.

**वि**—'apart, separate from, reverse to', &c.; @deva[विचल्] 'to move apart', @deva[वियुज्] 'to be separated', @deva[विक्री] opp. of @deva[क्री] 'to sell', 'to buy' &c.; sometimes this has an intensive force.

**सम्** or **सं**—'together with, excellent, full', &c. @deva[संगम्] 'to be united', @deva[संस्कारः] 'perfection', @deva[संस्कृति] 'refinement', @deva[संहारः] 'destruction, contraction', &c.

**सु**—'well, thoroughly', &c. (in this sense it is opposed to @deva[दुस्]); @deva[सुकृतं] 'done well', @deva[सुशासित] 'thoroughly trained, well governed', &c. It is also used in the sense of 'very, excessively': @deva[सुमहत्] 'very great.'

Two or more of these prepositions may also be combined and prefixed; e. g. @deva[अभिनिविश्] 'to enter into with resolution', @deva[समुपगम्] 'to come in close contact with', &c.

When used in a compound the verb may be omitted after the prepositions @deva[अति], @deva[अधि], @deva[अनु], @deva[अप], @deva[अव], @deva[अभि], @deva[उप], @deva[परि] and @deva[प्रति]; @deva[अतिक्रान्तो मालां अतिमालः] &c. Vide @ref[232].

There are several other words, also styled Gati by Sanskrit grammarians, used prepositionally. These are prefixed to certain roots only. We give the more important of them below.

**(a)** @deva[अच्छ][^5] 'towards' is prefixed to @deva[वद्] and to roots implying motion; @deva[अच्छगम्य—गत्वा] 'going near', @deva[अच्छपतत्] 'flying towards', @deva[अच्छूद्य] 'having spoken to.'

**(b) (1)** @deva[अन्वाञ्च] 'giving strength to the weak', @deva[अलं] in the sense of 'decorating', @deva[ऊर्ती], @deva[उरर्ती], @deva[उत्ती] all implying 'assent, acceptance or promise', @deva[खात्] and similar imitative sounds, @deva[असत्] and @deva[सत्] in the sense of 'dishonouring or honouring', @deva[प्रास्त] denoting 'fastening', &c. are prefixed to @deva[कृ]; @deva[अन्वाञ्चकरणं], @deva[ऊर्तीकरणं], @deva[असत्कारः], @deva[खात्कृत्य], @deva[खाङ्कृत्य], @deva[प्रास्तकरणं], &c.

**(2)** @deva[नमः], @deva[भाषु], @deva[निष्या], @deva[वशो], @deva[साक्षात्] and some other words may be optionally prefixed to @deva[कृ] or remain separate; @deva[नमस्कारः], @deva[वशोकृ] or @deva[वशो कृ] 'to bring under subjection', @deva[साक्षात् कृ] or @deva[साक्षात्कृ] 'to make manifest', &c.

**(c)** @deva[अन्तर्] is prefixed to roots meaning 'to go', @deva[धा], @deva[भू], and similar roots; @deva[अन्तरित्य] 'having disappeared', @deva[अन्तर्धानं] 'disappearance', @deva[अन्तर्भूत], &c.

**(d)** @deva[अस्तम्] is prefixed to roots implying motion; @deva[अस्तमयः] 'setting', @deva[अस्तंगत] 'set', @deva[अस्तंनी] 'to cause to set, to lead to destruction', &c.

**(e)** @deva[आविः], and @deva[प्रादुः] are prefixed to @deva[कृ], @deva[अस्] and @deva[भू] and @deva[तिरस्] to @deva[भू], @deva[धा] and similar roots, and optionally to @deva[कृ], @deva[आविष्करणं], @deva[आविर्भवनं] 'manifestation', @deva[प्रादुर्]—@deva[आविर्भूत] 'manifested', &c.; @deva[तिरोभूय] 'vanishing out of sight', @deva[तिरोधानं] 'disappearance', &c.

**(f)** @deva[पुरः] is prefixed to @deva[कृ], @deva[भू], @deva[गम्] &c.; @deva[पुरस्कृत] 'placed before, headed by', @deva[पुरोगत] 'gone in the front', &c.

Several nouns, substantive and adjective, may be prefixed to the roots @deva[कृ], @deva[भू], and @deva[अस्] to form what are called in Sanskrit 'chvi-derivatives' (vide chap. IX. Sec. III.) @deva[कृष्णीकरणं] = @deva[कृष्णीकरणं]; @deva[धनी]=@deva[भूत]=@deva[धनीभूतः]. Such nouns are also termed 'Gati.'

Like prepositions may be further used the words to which the Taddhita affix @deva[सात्] is affixed, @deva[अग्निसात्कृ] 'to consign to flames'; @deva[भस्मसात्कृत] 'reduced to ashes', @deva[राजसात्कृत] 'made over to the king', &c. (vide p. 216.)

## III. PARTICLES

The particles are either used as expletives or intensives; some of these are @deva[किल], @deva[खलु], @deva[च], @deva[ह], @deva[इ], @deva[वै], @deva[हि], &c.

The following particles are used with certain words—

**अह**—@deva[अद्भुतम्] 'a wonder'.

**का**—@deva[कापुरुषः] 'a bad man', @deva[कौष्णम्] 'luke-warm', @deva[काजलम्] 'a little water'.

**कु**—@deva[कुकृत्यं] 'a bad deed'.

**चन**, **चित्**—@deva[किञ्चित्], @deva[कश्चित्], @deva[कञ्चन], @deva[किञ्चन] &c.

**न**—is generally changed to @deva[अ] or @deva[अन्] when prefixed to words beginning with a consonant or a vowel respectively. This particle has six different senses:[^6] (1) @deva[सादृश्यं] 'likeness' or 'resemblance'; as @deva[अब्राह्मणः] 'one not a Brāhmaṇa, but resembling a Brāhmaṇa' (wearing the sacred thread, &c.) i. e. 'a Kṣhatriya or a Vaiśya' (2) @deva[अभाव] 'absence or negation'; @deva[अज्ञानं] 'the absence of knowledge', (3) @deva[अन्यत्व] 'difference from'; as @deva[अयं अपटः] 'this is something different from a cloth' i. e. 'a jar' or so; (4) @deva[अल्पता] 'littleness' or 'smallness'; as @deva[अनुदरा कन्या] 'a girl having a slender waist'; (5) @deva[अप्राशस्त्य] 'badness, unfitness', &c.; @deva[अकार्यं] 'something unfit to be done', @deva[अकालः] 'an improper time, not a favourable opportunity', and lastly (6) @deva[विरोध] or 'opposition'; @deva[अनीतिः] 'non-morality'; 'immorality', @deva[असुरः] 'opposed to a god' i. e 'a demon'.

**म**—is generally used as an expletive. Used with a form of the present tense of a verb, it gives it the sense of the past tense; as @deva[भवति स्म] i. e. @deva[अभवत्]. When used with the particle @deva[मा] it has an intensive force; as @deva[मा स्म शोकं मनः कुर्याः] &c.

**स्विद्**—is added to @deva[कि] and other indeclinables and asks a question or implies a doubt; @deva[किन्विद्], @deva[अहोस्विद्], &c.

**स्वी**—is used with @deva[कृ] and its derivatives like a preposition in the sense of 'acceptance'; @deva[स्वीकारः], @deva[स्वीकृतम्], &c.

## IV. CONJUNCTIONS

The following are the principal conjunctions in Sanskrit:—

**(a) Copulative:** @deva[अथ], @deva[अथो], @deva[उत], @deva[च], @deva[किञ्च], &c.

**(b) Disjunctive:** @deva[वा], @deva[वा]...@deva[वा], &c.

**(c) Adversative:** @deva[अथवा], @deva[तु], @deva[किन्तु], @deva[किन्वा], &c.

**(d) Conditional:** @deva[चेत्], @deva[यदि], @deva[यदापि], @deva[नेत्], @deva[नोचेत्], @deva[वेद्] (used in sacrificial ceremonies), &c.

**(e) Causal:** @deva[हि], @deva[तत्], @deva[तेन], &c.

**(f) Interrogative:** @deva[आहो], @deva[अहोस्विद्], @deva[उत], @deva[उताहो], @deva[कि], @deva[किन्नु], @deva[किन्तु], @deva[किञ्चित्], @deva[नञु], @deva[नघा], @deva[नु], &c.

**(g) Affirmatives and negatives:** @deva[अहो], @deva[अथ किम्], @deva[आम्], @deva[अद्धा] &c.

**(h) Conjunctions of time:** @deva[यावत्]—@deva[तावत्], @deva[यदा], @deva[तदा] &c.

**(i)** @deva[अथ] and @deva[इति] are used to mark the beginning and the close of a work respectively.

## V. INTERJECTIONS

"The interjection is not properly a part of speech, as it does not enter into the construction of sentences. It is a sudden exclamation prompted by some strong feeling or emotion. There are various utterances suited to the different emotions of the mind":—Prof. Bain.

**(a)** These are—@deva[अः], @deva[इ], @deva[उ], @deva[ए], @deva[ऐ], @deva[ओ], @deva[अहो], @deva[अहहो], @deva[अहो], @deva[बत], @deva[ह], @deva[हा], @deva[हाहा], &c. expressive of wonder, grief or regret, &c.

**(b)** @deva[धिक्], @deva[धिक्] &c. expressive of contempt.

**(c)** @deva[हा], @deva[बत], &c. expressing sorrow, dejection, &c.

**(d)** @deva[हा], @deva[हाहा], @deva[हन्त] showing grief.

**(e)** @deva[आ], @deva[हुम्], @deva[हुम्], &c. expressive of anger, contempt, &c.

**(f)** @deva[हन्त], &c. expressive of joy.

**(g)** There are some interjections used to call attention. Of these,

**(1)** some show respect; such as @deva[अरे], @deva[अये], @deva[अहो], @deva[अहो], @deva[बत], @deva[उ], @deva[ए], @deva[ओ], @deva[भोसु], @deva[भोः], @deva[हंहो], @deva[हे], @deva[हे], @deva[हो], &c.

**(3)** The exclamations @deva[श्रौषट्], @deva[वौषट्] and @deva[वषट्] are used when offering oblations to gods or manes; and

**(4)** @deva[स्वाहा] and @deva[स्वधा] when offering oblations to gods and manes respectively.
