There are in Sanskrit—

**(a)** Two kinds of verbs, Primitive and Derivative.

**(b)** Six Tenses (@deva[कालः]) and four Moods (@deva[अर्थः]) which are as follow:—[^1]

| TENSES | TECHNICAL NAME | MOODS | TECHNICAL NAME |
|--------|----------------|-------|----------------|
| @deva[वर्तमानः] or Present | @deva[लट्] | @deva[आज्ञा] or Imperative | @deva[लोट्] |
| @deva[भूतः] or Aorist | @deva[लुङ्] | @deva[विधि] or Potential | @deva[विधिलिङ्] |
| @deva[अनद्यतनभूतः] or Imperfect | @deva[लङ्] | @deva[आशीः] or Benedictive | @deva[आशीर्लिङ्] |
| @deva[परोक्षभूतः] or Perfect | @deva[लिट्] | @deva[सत्ता] or Conditional | @deva[लृङ्] |
| @deva[अनद्यतन-भविष्यत्] or 1st Future | @deva[लृट्] | | |
| @deva[भविष्यत्] or 2nd Future | @deva[लुट्] | | |

@deva[लेट्] or the Subjunctive is used only in the Veda and is therefore termed 'The Vedic Subjunctive.'

**Note:**—The ten tenses and moods are technically called the ten Lakaras in Sanskrit grammars.

**(c)** Three Voices (@deva[प्रयोगः]), the Active Voice (@deva[कर्तरिप्रयोगः]) as @deva[पचति], the Passive Voice (@deva[कर्मणिप्रयोगः]), as @deva[पच्यते पक्ति यज्ञः], and the Impersonal Construction (@deva[भावेप्रयोगः]) @deva[तेषां गम्यते].

**(d)** Two sets of personal terminations; the one called 'Parasmaipada' or the Verb Active:—as @deva[भवति] 'he goes'; and the other 'Ātmanepada' or the verb Middle:—as @deva[वर्तते] 'he is'. Some verbs take the former, others the latter, while others take either. Several roots again, though Parasmai, become Ātmanepada in some of the senses they take, or when certain prepositions are prefixed, or in particular tenses. These will be considered in treating of Ātmanepada and Parasmaipada.

**(e)** Derivative verbs are those formed from nouns and those primitive verbs which exist in the language, while primitive verbs are those which are derived from roots given in the Dhātupāṭhas or lexicon of roots.

**(f)** Primitive verbs are conjugated in the Active and Middle, and may be conjugated in the ten tenses and moods given above.

**(g)** Primitive verbs are conjugated in six different ways or conjugations:—

[^1]: These technical names are given in the following Kārikā, @deva[लट् वर्तमाने लेट् वेदे भूते लुङ् लङ् लिटस्तथा । विध्याशिषोर्लिङ् लिङ्लोटौ लुट् लृट् लृङ् च भविष्यति ॥] This terminology of Pāṇini, it will be seen, is artificial. Other grammarians use different names. The ten Lakaras of Pāṇini are according to their nomenclature as follow.—@deva[वर्तती] (@deva[हतिः]), @deva[अवर्तती], @deva[हर्तती], @deva[परोक्षा], @deva[भविष्यती], @deva[भविष्यत्ती], @deva[पञ्चमी], @deva[षष्ठी], @deva[प्रियातिरूपी] and @deva[आशीः]— 'Apte's Guide'.
