The Taddhita affixes are added in various senses. They occasion various changes in the words to which they are added. The following general observations should be paid attention to.

**(a)** As a general rule the first vowel of a word takes its Vṛiddhi substitute before the terminations @deva[अ], @deva[य], @deva[एक], @deva[इन्], @deva[एय], @deva[ख], &c.; as @deva[अश्वपति + अ = आश्वपति + अ].

**(b)** Before terminations beginning with a vowel or @deva[य] (1) the final @deva[अ], @deva[आ], @deva[इ], and @deva[ई] are rejected; (2) @deva[उ] and @deva[ऊ] take their Guṇa substitute; (3) @deva[ओ] and @deva[औ] obey the ordinary rules of sandhi; @deva[आश्वपति + अ = आश्वपत] 'belonging to Aśvapati,' &c. n. n.; @deva[मनु + अ = मानवः] 'a descendant of Manu'; @deva[गो + य = गव्य] 'belonging to a cow', so @deva[नाव्य] fr. @deva[नौ] &c.

**(c)** In the case of derivatives form compound words sometimes the initial vowel of the second word takes its Vṛiddhi substitute, and sometimes the Vṛiddhi is double; @deva[पुरेवार्षिकः] 'belonging to the last year', similarly @deva[उपविशालकः] &c. @deva[सौहार्दम्] fr. @deva[सुहृद्], @deva[सौभाग्यम्] from @deva[सुभगम्], &c. When these terminations are added to a Dva. comp. both the words of which are the names of deities, Vṛiddhi is substituted for the initial vowel of both, @deva[आग्निमारुतं कर्म] 'a sacrifice offered to Agni and the Marut deities,' &c.

**(d)** If the initial vowel of a word be preceded by the @deva[य्] and @deva[व्] of a preposition the @deva[य्] or @deva[व्] is first changed to @deva[इय्] or @deva[उव्] before Vṛiddhi substitute can take place; as @deva[व्याकरणम्+अ=वियाकरणम्+अ=वैयाकरणः]; @deva[स्वप्+अ=सुवप्+अ=सौप्तः]; similarly @deva[सौवस्तिक] from @deva[स्वस्ति]; @deva[सौवरम्] fr. @deva[स्वर], &c.

**(e)** Before consonantal Tad. affixes, a final @deva[न्] is generally rejected; the final @deva[न्] with the preceding vowel is sometimes dropped before vowel terminations and before such as begin with @deva[य्]; @deva[युवन्-युवकम्], @deva[राजन्-राजकम्], &c. @deva[आत्मन्-आत्मय-आत्मीयम्]. There are various exceptions to this latter part of the rule; e.g. @deva[राजन्यः] fr. @deva[राजन्] &c.

**N. B.**—Other changes the student will easily note from the instances given.
