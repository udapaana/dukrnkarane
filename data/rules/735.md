Roots ending in @deva[ऋ], and those ending in a consonant take the affix @deva[घ्यत्] (@deva[य]) in the same sense as @deva[क्यप्]. Before this affix the ending @deva[चु] and @deva[व] of a root are changed to @deva[ऋ] and @deva[यु] respectively and the final vowel and the penultimate @deva[अ] take Vṛiddhi substitute; any other penultimate vowel generally takes Guṇa.

@deva[कृ]—@deva[कार्यं] 'what ought to be done,' @deva[धृ]—@deva[धार्यं] 'what ought to be worn,' @deva[दा]—@deva[देयः]; @deva[हृ]—@deva[हार्यं]; @deva[पद्]—@deva[दृदु]—@deva[पार्यः] 'what is to be thought of,' &c.; @deva[वच्]—@deva[वाच्यं] 'what is meant in a sentence,' @deva[पच्]—@deva[पाच्यं] 'what is to be cooked,' @deva[वुज्]—@deva[भोज्यं] 'what is to be purified,' &c.
