With the words @deva[यावत्], @deva[तावत्] and others having a similar sense, the Present is sometimes used in the sense of the Future perfect; @deva[यावत्त्वां न पश्यति तावदपयातम्] before he sees you, move away (before he shall have seen you, &c.).

(b) [^1] With the particles @deva[ह्य] and @deva[ध्रुवम्] the Present has the sense of the Future when certainty is indicated; @deva[ध्रुवमेते सारम् अवाप्स्यन्ति] (Rag. X. 25.) it will endeavour to accomplish your object. @deva[ह्य वसुधारो जयति सप्तद्वीपाम्] (S'ak. VII. 33) he will conquer the earth consisting of seven continents. @deva[प्रगृह्यते तव वचनं नमः।] Kir. VIII. 8.

[^1]: @deva[ध्रुवमर्थे विध्यानुमो लृट्] Pāṇ. III. 3. 4. @deva[निश्चये निश्चयनं लोदोट।] Sid. Kau.
