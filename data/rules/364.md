## Nouns with Single Inflection Treated as Indeclinables

Besides these there are in Sanskrit a few nouns having one inflection only which are treated as indeclinable (nipātas); e.g. @deva[अन्यत्] 'another (reason)', @deva[अस्तम्] 'setting', @deva[अस्ति] 'that exists', @deva[ओम्] 'the well-known sacred syllable om', @deva[चनस्] 'satisfaction, food', @deva[चाहु] 'coaxing', @deva[नमस्] 'a bow', @deva[नास्ति] 'non-existence', @deva[भूर्] 'the earth', @deva[भुवर्] 'the sky', @deva[पदि] 'the dark fortnight', @deva[यशस्] 'happiness', @deva[शुक्ल] or @deva[शुक्लि] 'the bright fortnight', @deva[संवत्] 'a year', @deva[स्वाहा] 'food offered to gods', @deva[स्वधा] 'food offered to the manes', @deva[स्वर्] 'heaven', @deva[स्वस्ति] 'happiness' &c.
