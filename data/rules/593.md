## Special Cases in Passive Base Formation

### (a) Nasal Dropping with Vowel Lengthening

The roots @deva[स्तञ्ज्], @deva[जन्], @deva[तन्], and @deva[सञ्ज्], 'to sprinkle' drop their @deva[न्] and at the same time lengthen their vowel.

**Examples:**
- @deva[स्तान्यते]
- @deva[जान्यते], &c.

### (b) Special Base Forms

@deva[शी] 'to lie down' and @deva[चि] form their bases as @deva[शय्] and @deva[चय्].

### (c) Vowel Shortening with Prepositions

@deva[ऊह्] shortens its @deva[ऊ] when a preposition is prefixed.

### (d) Final Vowel Elision

@deva[दरिद्रा], @deva[दौषी] and @deva[वेणी] drop their final vowel before @deva[य].

### (e) Special Substitutions

@deva[हू] and @deva[अस्] substitute @deva[हुय्] and @deva[अस्य्] for themselves; @deva[यज्] and @deva[भञ्ज्] also substitute @deva[इज्] and @deva[भज्] respectively.
