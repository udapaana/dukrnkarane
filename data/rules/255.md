**(a)** @deva[धर्म] when preceded by a single member in a Bahuvrīhi becomes @deva[धर्मन्];[^1] @deva[कल्याणी धर्मः यस्य स कल्याणधर्मा]; similarly @deva[समानधर्मी] ( see Māl. Māḍ. Act. I Intro. ); but @deva[परमः स्वः धर्म यस्य स परमस्वधर्मा]; @deva[परस्परधर्मी] may also be correct if @deva[परस्पर] be regarded as one word compounded in a Karmadhāraya. @deva[संहिष्णुतात्पर्यधर्मी], @deva[तिलुचिषधर्मी], @deva[अत्रनिष्ठतिधर्मी] are to be similarly explained.

**(b)** @deva[धनुस्] at the end of a Bah. becomes @deva[धन्वन्], as @deva[अजिज्यं धनुरस्य स अजिज्यधन्वा] 'one whose bow is strung'; so @deva[शार्ङ्गधन्वा] ( @deva[शृङ्गस्य इदं शार्ङ्गं] ) 'one whose bow is made of horn'; i. e. the god Vishṇu. This change is optional when the whole compound is a name; @deva[शातधन्वा-धुः].

**(c)** Similarly @deva[जन्भ] 'a tooth,' 'food,' &c. becomes @deva[जन्भन्] when preceded by @deva[शु], @deva[हरित], @deva[शुभ्र] or @deva[सोम]; as @deva[शोभनः जन्भः अस्य शुजन्भा] one having beautiful teeth; similarly @deva[हरितजन्भा] m.; @deva[पूर्णं भक्ष्यं यस्य], @deva[हृण्यमिव दन्ता यस्येति वा पूर्णजन्भा], @deva[सोमजन्भा] one whose food is the holy soma juice; but @deva[पतितजन्भाः].

**(d)** The word @deva[इर्म] n. a wound, preceded by @deva[दक्षिण] becomes @deva[इर्मन्] when the wound is inflicted by a hunter; @deva[दक्षिणे इर्म यस्य दक्षिणेर्मा हरः] a stag wounded on the right side by a hunter. See Bhaṭṭi VI. 44.
