## Locative with Verbs of Love and Attachment

Verbs having the sense of 'love, regard for, attachment to' such as @deva[स्निह्], @deva[अनुरञ्ज्], @deva[वाञ्छ्], @deva[रम्], &c. and their derivatives generally govern the Locative; @deva[तस्मै पुत्रे स्निह्यति] the father loves his son; @deva[अतिरमै सौदर्यैषि मृदिति] (Śak. I) I have a sisterly affection for them also; @deva[न सन्देहं निरक्तस्यन्काय्यानां मयत्येतते] I do not, indeed, love the ascetic's daughter, @deva[अङ्गुदरजितरत्यो राज्ञ जनता युहद्धरज्यनै] people do not love a king whose ministers are corrupt; @deva[मायत्यद्धौ निरतिरै निरन्तुकुत् क्तुम्] (Manu. S. III. 179), @deva[रहसि रमते] (Māl. Mād. III. 2.) takes pleasure in solitude; @deva[रत्युः श्यासै] (Bhaṭṭi. I.) devoted to his welfare.

Note:—@deva[अनुरञ्ज्] and @deva[भनद्धुहु] are sometimes used with the Accusative also; @deva[रामायुदूरञ्यन्ति] (Rāma.); @deva[मादवानाम्यदरञ्यन्तो] (Bhaṭṭi. IV. 29.).
