## Insertion of स् and अस् in Nominal Verbs

The consonant @deva[स्] and the syllable @deva[अस्] are inserted between the nominal stem and the affix @deva[य]:

@deva[मधु आदायं इच्छति]—@deva[मधुस्यति] or @deva[मधुस्यति] 'he wishes for honey'

So @deva[सुधिस्यति], @deva[सुधस्यति], etc.

Final @deva[अ] is dropped before @deva[अस्]: @deva[पुत्रस्यति]

### (a) Special Cases with अत् Insertion

@deva[अत्] is inserted:

- After @deva[मृग्] and @deva[अश्व] when the sense to be conveyed is that of longing for them
- After @deva[क्षीर] and @deva[लवण्] when the one is desired to be drunk and the other to be licked

#### Examples

@deva[मृगस्यति गौः] 'the cow longs for the ox'

@deva[अश्वस्यति वड्वा] 'the mare longs for the horse'

@deva[क्षीरस्यति बालः] 'the child desires to drink'

@deva[लवणस्यति ओष्ठः] 'the camel wishes for licking the salt'

But: @deva[मृषीयति] 'he desires to have a ball', @deva[अर्यीयति] 'he wishes for a horse', @deva[क्षीरायति], @deva[लवणायति]
