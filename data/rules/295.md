@deva[समान] becomes @deva[स]—[^1]

**(a)** when followed by the words @deva[ज्योतिस्], @deva[जनपद], @deva[राशि], @deva[नाभि], @deva[नामन्], @deva[गोत्र], @deva[रूप], @deva[स्थान], @deva[वर्ण], @deva[वयस्], @deva[बन्धन], and @deva[बन्धु]; @deva[सजातं ज्योतिः अस्य सञ्ज्योतिः] a kind of mourning which lasts from the rising of the sun or a particular collection of stars to its setting. (@deva[समानं ज्योतिरस्येति बहुव्रीहिः] । @deva[प्रातमें ज्योतिरि जाते तरौ वा नक्षत्रे वा संजाते तदस्तमयपर्यन्तमनुव्रतमानमाशौचं सञ्ज्योतिरित्युच्यते] Tat. Bod.); @deva[सजनपदः] belonging to the same country; @deva[सराशिः], @deva[सनाभिः] 'connected by the same navel, sprung from the same ancestor,' &c.

**(b)** when followed by the word @deva[ब्रह्मचारिन्][^2] 'a student of the Veda who belongs to the same S'âkhâ or branch as another,' @deva[समानः ब्रह्मचारी सब्रह्मचारी].

**(c)** when followed by @deva[तीर्थ] with the Tad. affix @deva[य] added to it; as @deva[समानतीर्थयं वासी सतीर्थ्यः] 'a disciple of the same preceptor,' and optionally when followed by @deva[उदर] under the same circumstances; @deva[समानोदर्यं शयितः सोदर्यः] or @deva[समानोदर्यः] 'born of the same womb,' 'a brother by whole blood.'

**(d)** when followed by @deva[हृक्], @deva[एश] and @deva[एष]; @deva[सहृक्], @deva[सएशः], @deva[सएषः] ।

**(e)** and in the case of compounds like @deva[सपक्ष], @deva[शाखा], @deva[स्वजातीय], &c.[^3]
