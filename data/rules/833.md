## Ablative Case: Motion Away From (अपादान)

The principal sense of the Ablative case is @deva[अपादानं] or 'motion away from'; hence the noun from which the motion, real or conceived, takes place is put in the Ablative case; @deva[ग्रामात् आगच्छति] 'comes from a village;' @deva[पतति] from a running horse; @deva[सदायजुत्रादु भयते].

(a) Words having the sense of @deva[जुगुप्सा], @deva[विराम] or 'cessation,' 'pause,' and @deva[व्यवायः] 'swerving from,' also govern the Ablative case; @deva[पापात् युक्तेति] 'he hates sin;' @deva[न नमः अधर्मात् विरराम] (Rag. VIII. 22) 'the monarch did not desist from efforts until they bore their fruit'; @deva[धर्मात् निवर्तते] 'swerves from duty'; @deva[स्वविकत्यान्यनुः] (Meg. 1.) 'careless, failing in the discharge of the duties (of his office)'; so @deva[प्रमाद्]; @deva[यदाश्रयः निवर्तत संवननस्यात् यदाशयात्] (Manu Smr. V. 49.), &c.

@deva[मद्] with @deva[प्र] in the sense of 'to be careless about' is used with the Loc. also; @deva[जीवितेषु प्रमादिन् यमदात् विषेक्तुयुः]. (Manu Smr. II. 213) on this account wise men are not careless about their wives.[^1]
