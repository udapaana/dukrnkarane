## Dative with कुप्, द्विष्, ईर्ष्, असूय (Anger, Hatred, Jealousy)

The roots @deva[कुप्], @deva[द्विष्], @deva[ईर्ष्], and @deva[असूय], and others having the same sense govern the Dative of the person against whom the feeling of anger, hatred, &c., is directed; @deva[द्रुयरे कुयस्यति द्विड्टिङ हययतिश्यदरतु वा] (Sid. Kau.) 'he is angry with Hari, or bears hatred towards him, or is jealous of him or finds fault with him;' @deva[रातोयु मोङक्तुयस्युययरुयत] (Bhaṭṭi. VIII. 75.) 'he did neither get angry with Śibā nor find fault with her;' but @deva[भार्यम् ईर्चति] 'he keeps a jealous watch over his wife' (that others may not see her @deva[प्रेमयर्तीः असूयर्त्योऽधेति] Sid. Kau.)

(a) But @deva[कुप्] and @deva[द्विष्] preceded by a preposition govern the Accusative case; @deva[किं मां सकुययसि] 'why do you get angry with me?' @deva[निरुययस्युत्छुटुरीसर्नवितरुयुङ वनते] (Mud. I.) 'he always endeavours to do harm to my body.'

Obs:— @deva[द्विष्] with @deva[अभि] is also found used with the Dat.; @deva[मया स्युम्युदुवड्स्योययवरुयरुर्नों] (Rag. XII. 52.)
