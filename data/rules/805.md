## Prepositions Used with Accusative: अति, अनु, अपि, उप, आविस्, परि, प्रति

The following prepositions are used with the Accusative.[^1]

(a) @deva[अति] 'superior to, higher than' @deva[अनु] 'just after, after by the side of, inferior to,' @deva[अपि] 'close to' and @deva[उप] 'near, inferior to;' e.g. @deva[अति देवान् कृष्णः] Kṛṣṇa excels gods in might; @deva[अति रामं गोविन्दः] Govinda is superior to Rāma; @deva[जपं प्रावर्तत] it rained just after the muttering of prayers; @deva[त्वं मनुष्यं ते] every thing of thee is after mine; @deva[अनु पितरं गच्छति सुतः] the son imitates his father; @deva[न भवान् अनु रामं चेत्] if you are not inferior to Rāma; so @deva[अनु हरि सुरः]; @deva[भक्तो हरि अपि] the devotee is close to Hari; @deva[उप न ते कर्म] your act is not like that of a hero (lit. is inferior to his); @deva[उप हरिं सुरः] &c.

(b) The prepositions @deva[आविस्], @deva[अनु], @deva[परि] and @deva[प्रति] are used with the Accusative when they refer to particular things; @deva[गिरिं आविस् अनु परि प्रति विद्योतते विद्युत] the lightning flashes towards the mountain; also when they mean 'to every one,' &c. @deva[वृक्षं अभि अनु परि प्रति सिञ्चति] waters each and every tree; so @deva[अभि अनु परि प्रति स्त्री स्त्री जातमुखः].

(c) @deva[अनु], @deva[परि] and @deva[प्रति] also govern the Accusative when they imply 'one's own share;' @deva[लक्ष्मीः हरिं अनु परि प्रति] Lakṣmī is the proper share (property) of Hari.

---

[^1]: Prepositions used by themselves and governing nouns are called Karmapravachanīya. Pāṇ. II. 3. 8 and I. 4. 85–87, 90, 91.
