## Formation of Adverbs from Various Cases

Adverbs are either primitive or derived from nouns, pronouns or numerals. As adverbs may also be further used the:

### Accusative Singular Neuter

Acc. sing. neu. of nouns and adjectives; and sometimes the sing. of other cases also:

@deva[सत्यम्] 'truthfully', @deva[मृदु] 'softly', @deva[सुखम्] 'happily', @deva[लघु] 'quickly', @deva[निर्भयम्], @deva[अवश्यम्], @deva[अत्यन्तम्], @deva[बलवत्] 'strongly', @deva[भूयस्] 'again', &c.

### Instrumental Singular

@deva[दुःखेन] 'painfully', @deva[सुखेन], @deva[धर्मेण] 'virtuously, justly', @deva[दक्षिणेन], @deva[उत्तरेण], @deva[अधरेण], @deva[चिरेण] 'after a long time', @deva[क्षणेन], &c.

### Dative Singular

@deva[चिराय], @deva[चिरतराय] 'for a long time', @deva[अर्थाय] 'for the sake of'

### Ablative Singular

@deva[बलात्] 'forcibly', @deva[हर्षात्], @deva[शोकात्], @deva[दूरात्], @deva[तस्मात्], @deva[अस्मात्], &c.; @deva[चिरात्] 'for a long time', @deva[दूरात्], @deva[उत्तरात्], &c.

### Locative Singular

@deva[स्थाने] 'properly', @deva[दूरे], @deva[प्रभाते], @deva[प्राह्णे], @deva[अग्रे], @deva[एकपदे] 'at once', @deva[सपदि], @deva[ऋते], @deva[समीपे], @deva[अभ्यासे] 'near', &c.

### Note

Adverbs formed from pronouns and numerals are given in their proper places; while those formed from nouns are given in Chapter IX.
