## Passive Construction with Double Accusative Roots

When these verbs are used in the passive, the secondary object in the case of the first twelve roots and the principal one in that of the last four is put in the Nominative, the other Accusative remaining as before; @deva[गावः पयः दुह्यते], @deva[ब्राह्मणः रामं यथार्थं लोकायितः], @deva[इन्द्रापिः द्वारं नीयते देवैः] &c, @deva[तेन गावः ग्रामं नीयन्ते विज्ञातं क्षयन्तं उदयन्ते वा], &c.
