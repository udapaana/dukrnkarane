The affixes given in §. 340 are added in the following senses also, and are therefore called @deva[मत्वर्थीय] 'affixes expressing possession.' These affixes are @deva[मतुप्] (@deva[मत्], @deva[वत्]), @deva[इनि], @deva[इलच्] and @deva[विनि].[^1]

**(1)** In the sense of @deva[गौरवे] 'respectful mention'; @deva[धनवान्] 'possessing wealth', @deva[श्रीमत्] 'possessing glory &c.', @deva[बुद्धिमान्] 'possessing wisdom', @deva[गुणवत्] 'possessing virtue' &c.

**(2)** In the sense of @deva[निन्दायाम्] 'disapprobation'; @deva[दुर्मतिः] 'having a bad mind', 'wicked.'

**(3)** In the sense of @deva[प्रशंसायाम्] 'eulogy'; @deva[सुमनाः] 'having a good mind.'

**(4)** In the sense of @deva[कौशल] 'skill'; @deva[वेदविदः] 'skilled in or knowing the Vedas.'

**(5)** In the sense of @deva[अभावे] 'negation'; @deva[अविद्वान्] 'unlearned', @deva[अबुद्धिमत्] 'not wise' &c.

**(6)** In the sense of @deva[आशिषि] 'blessing'; @deva[आयुष्मान्] 'possessed of long life', @deva[बलिनः] 'possessed of strength' &c.

**(7)** In the sense of @deva[उपमा] 'comparison'; @deva[अश्विव] (= @deva[अश्व इव]) 'like a horse' &c.

---

**N. B.**—When any one of the above seven senses is intended and the base denotes 'time,' then the base takes the affix @deva[अच्] which becomes @deva[अम्] in the Neuter and the word remains indeclinable.[^2]

The words formed in this manner are the following:—@deva[नक्तम्] 'night', and @deva[दिव] 'day'; form @deva[नक्तंदिवम्] 'possessed of night and day', i. e. lasting day and night; @deva[सायम्] 'evening', and @deva[प्रातः] 'morning' form @deva[सायंप्रातः] i. e. lasting evening and morning; @deva[मध्यम्] 'middle' and @deva[दिनम्] 'day' form @deva[मध्यंदिनम्] i. e. lasting from mid-day; @deva[अहन्] 'day' forms @deva[अन्वहम्] 'daily', @deva[संवत्सर] 'year' forms @deva[संवत्सरम्] 'annually.'

**N. B.**—When the base is @deva[वर्ष] 'year' and the following affix has the force of a Past Pass. Part.[^3] i. e. when the idea of expiry is denoted, @deva[पर्युषितम्] 'more than a year old' &c.

**N. B.**—When the base denotes, 'flesh', 'grain', 'water', 'ghee', 'milk', 'food' and the affix also has the sense of the Past Pass. Part. i. e. expresses a past state; @deva[पिशितम्] 'old meat', @deva[धान्यम्] 'old grain', @deva[उदकम्] 'old water', @deva[आज्यम्] 'old ghee', @deva[पयः] 'old milk', @deva[अन्नम्] 'old food.'

The same affix is also added in any of the above senses optionally with @deva[मतुप्] &c., when the base denotes 'sacrificial act', or the base is @deva[गो] 'cow', or any other word, in forming the adjectives @deva[धनिकः] 'rich', and @deva[तपस्विन्] 'an ascetic.'[^4] Thus @deva[गृहमेधी] or @deva[गृहमेधवान्] or @deva[गृहमेधिमान्] or @deva[गृहमेधविन्] 'one who performs the Gṛihamedha sacrifice'; @deva[सोमपः] or @deva[सोमपवान्] or @deva[सोमपिमान्] or @deva[सोमपविन्] 'one who performs the Soma sacrifice'; similarly @deva[वाजिनः], @deva[गोमान्], @deva[धनिकः], @deva[तपस्विन्] &c. &c.[^5]

---

@deva[क] is added in the sense of possession in the following cases:—

**(1)** When the base ends in a long vowel;[^6] @deva[हेमवान्] or @deva[हेमी] or @deva[हेमिमान्] or @deva[हेमविन्] or @deva[हेमकः] 'possessing gold' and so @deva[रत्नवान्] or @deva[रत्नी] &c. or @deva[रत्नकः], @deva[रत्नवत्], &c.

[^1]: By the word "also" in the text, it is implied that these affixes are added to a base in any one of these senses along with the sense of possession. Thus @deva[बुद्धिमान्] 'he who has understanding', 'wise', also conveys the idea of respectful mention.

[^2]: The only exception is @deva[रात्रिःवान्] or @deva[रात्रिमान्] 'lasting a night.'

[^3]: When 'exceeding' or 'more than' is meant to be expressed.

[^4]: The word @deva[तपस्विन्] is irregular because the base ends in a short vowel.

[^5]: Observe, what has been said before applies to this paragraph as well, namely that the idea of possession underlies all these formations.

[^6]: It is really an exception to the dictum that @deva[मतुप्] &c. are affixes of possession.
