## Vowel Changes in Fifth Aorist

(a) In the Parasmaipada, the final radical vowel and the penultimate @deva[अ] of roots ending in @deva[र्] or @deva[ल्] and that of @deva[वद्] and @deva[त्रज्] take their Vṛddhi substitute necessarily; @deva[लू]-@deva[अलाविषम्]; @deva[चर्]—@deva[अचारिषम्]; @deva[फल्]-@deva[अफालिषम्], &c.

(b) The penultimate short vowel of a root takes its Guṇa substitute; @deva[क्रुश्]-@deva[अक्रोषिषम्], &c.

(c) Vṛddhi is optionally substituted for the penultimate @deva[अ], not prosodially long, of roots having an initial consonant and not ending in @deva[र्] or @deva[ल्]; @deva[पद्]—@deva[अपादिषम्], @deva[अपदिषम्]; @deva[गह्]—@deva[अगाहिषम्]—@deva[अगहिषम्].

(d) But the vowel of roots ending in @deva[स्], @deva[व्], @deva[य], and of the roots @deva[क्षप्], @deva[क्षर्], @deva[नाघ्], @deva[चि], @deva[कट्] 'to cover, to surround,' @deva[वद्], 'to break, to pierce,' @deva[चत्], @deva[चर्], 'to ask, to beg,' @deva[वश्] 'to go or move,' @deva[मथ्] 'to churn,' @deva[लम्] 'to stick or cling to,' @deva[हुस्] and @deva[स्वस्] 'to sound or to be diminished,' does not take its Vṛddhi substitute.

(e) In the Ātmanepada the radical vowel takes its Guṇa substitute; @deva[लू]—@deva[अलेष्ट].
