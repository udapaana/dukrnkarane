Compounds of the following words with verbal indeclinables are called Gati.

**(a)**[^1] The particles @deva[उरी], @deva[उरती], @deva[बोल्ड], @deva[वष्ट], @deva[स्वाहा], @deva[स्वधा], @deva[प्राद्], @deva[आविष्] and @deva[अस्] and the word @deva[कारिका] meaning an action; as @deva[उरीकृत्य], @deva[उरतीकृत्य] 'having accepted; @deva[वषट्कृत्य] 'having uttered the word Vashat;' @deva[प्रादूर्भूय], @deva[कारिकाकृत्य] 'having done the act.'

**(b)**[^2] Onomatopoetic words not followed by @deva[इति], as @deva[खाट्कृत्य]; but @deva[खाटिति कृत्वा] (@deva[निरुक्तिवत्]).

**(c)**[^3] The words @deva[सत्] and @deva[असत्] showing respect and disrespect respectively, the prepositions @deva[अलं] in the sense of 'adorning,' @deva[पुर:], @deva[अग्र:] @deva[अंत:], @deva[कुणु], @deva[मन:], @deva[अस्तं], @deva[अच्छ], and @deva[तिर:]; as @deva[अलंकृत्य] 'having adorned', but @deva[अलं कृत्वा] doing enough (@deva[पर्याप्तिसिद्धयै:] Sid. Kau.); @deva[पुरस्कृत्य] 'placing in the front'; @deva[अग्र:कृत्य] (@deva[अग्रीकृत्य]) @deva[अन्तर्कृत्य] (@deva[मध्ये कृत्वा] Sid. Kau.); @deva[कुणुकृत्य], as @deva[कुणुकृत्य पय: पिबति] 'he drinks milk to his heart's content', @deva[अच्छोद्य] 'having gone up to and spoken' (@deva[अभिमुखं गत्वा उक्त्वा च इत्यर्थे:] Sid. Kau.); @deva[तिरोध्य], @deva[मनोहत्य] (till disgust is produced); @deva[अस्तंगत्य], @deva[अच्छुगत्य] 'going towards.'

**(d)**[^4] The words @deva[हस्ते], @deva[पाणौ] and @deva[प्राच्यै]; as @deva[हस्तेकृत्य], @deva[पाणौकृत्य], 'having married'; @deva[प्राच्यैकृत्य] 'having forced to yield.'

**(e)**[^5] The particles @deva[उपाजे], @deva[अन्वाजे], @deva[साक्षात्], @deva[मिथ्या], @deva[अधा], @deva[प्रादु:], @deva[आवि:] and @deva[नमस्], the words @deva[प्रदक्षि], @deva[उरसि], (except when there is the sense of juxtaposition or extreme contact), @deva[मध्ये], and the word @deva[पदे] form compounds with verbal indeclinables optionally; @deva[उपाजेकृत्य], @deva[उपाजे कृत्वा], @deva[अन्वाजेकृत्य] @deva[अन्वाजे कृत्वा] 'giving strength to the weak' [@deva[दुर्बलस्य बलमाधाय इत्यर्थै:] Sid. Kau.]; @deva[साक्षात्कृत्य-त्वा], @deva[लघणीकृत्य-त्वा], @deva[उरसिकृत्य-त्वा] (having accepted) @deva[मनसिकृत्य कृत्वा]; but @deva[उरसि कृत्वा पाणिं क्षेते]; @deva[मध्येकृत्य-त्वा], @deva[पदेकृत्य], @deva[पदे कृत्वा], &c.

**(f)** These compounds are also formed with nouns derived from roots for their second members; e. g. @deva[अस्तमय:] 'setting of the sun,' @deva[पुरस्कार:] 'reception, showing respect,' @deva[तिरस्कार:], @deva[सत्कार:], @deva[अलंकृति:], &c.
