**(a)** It is not compounded with verbal derivatives[^1] ending in @deva[तृ] and @deva[अक्] and denoting the agent of the action; @deva[कर्तृसखा] and not @deva[कर्तृसखा]; @deva[घटस्य कर्ता], @deva[ओदनस्य पाचकः] &c.; but @deva[हृदयं भक्षयति स्तूकभक्षिका] as the @deva[अक्] here does not denote the agent.

**Exception**[^2]:—But a compound is allowed in the case of the words @deva[याजक] one who employs as sacrificing priests, @deva[पूजक], @deva[परिचारक], @deva[परिवेषक] a waiter at meals, @deva[स्नापक] a servant who bathes his master or brings bathing water for him, @deva[अध्यापक], @deva[उत्साहक] one who destroys, @deva[होतृ], @deva[भर्तृ], when it does not mean 'a holder,' and others not very important; @deva[ब्राह्मणयाजकः], @deva[देवपूजकः], @deva[राजपरिचारकः]: &c.; @deva[अधिहोता], @deva[क्षुमर्तृ], &c. but @deva[वज्रस्य भर्तृ] 'the thunderbolt-bearer'.

**(b)**[^3] A specific Genitive (i.e. from which a singling out is made) is not compounded with any other words; as @deva[द्विजानो द्विजः श्रेष्ठः]

**(c)**[^4] A noun in the Genitive is not compounded with ordinal numbers, words denoting qualities, words implying satisfaction present participles, past participles meaning 'honoured or approved of' or showing the place of the act, verbal indeclinables and verbal nouns ending in @deva[तव्य]; e.g. @deva[द्विजेषु पञ्चमः], @deva[ब्राह्मणस्य शुक्लः] (@deva[हस्तः]); @deva[काकस्य कार्ष्येयः]; @deva[फलानां तृप्तितः] 'satisfied with fruits'. (an Inst. Tat. is, of course, allowed in these cases); @deva[द्विजस्य कुर्वन् कुर्वाणो वा किंचित्], @deva[सतां मतः] esteemed by the good; @deva[राज्ञां पूजितः]; @deva[इदमेषां आसीदिति] (place of sitting) @deva[गतो दृष्टं वा]; @deva[ब्राह्मणस्य छन्दा], @deva[नृस्य कर्तव्यम्] &c.

**N. B.** Compounds like @deva[राजपूजितः], @deva[राजमतः], &c. ought to be considered as Instrumental Tatpurushas.

**Exceptions.**

**(1)** If the word denoting a quality ends in @deva[तर] it does combine with a noun in the Genitive, and the @deva[तर] is dropped; @deva[सर्वेषां श्वेततरः सर्वश्वेतः] whiter than all others; similarly @deva[सर्वेषां महत्तरः सर्वमहान्] &c.

**(2)**[^5]The words @deva[द्वितीय], @deva[तृतीय], @deva[चतुर्थ] and @deva[तुर्य] are compounded with nouns signifying the whole of which they are parts, and are optionally placed first; @deva[द्वितीयं भिक्षायाः द्वितीयभिक्षा], @deva[भिक्षाद्वितीयम्] half of the quantity of alms; but @deva[द्वितीयं भिक्षायाः] ( begging alms a second time ) @deva[भिक्षाकरणम्].

**Note.**—Compounds like @deva[द्वितीयभिक्षा], @deva[पूर्वकायः] ( see further on ) &c. are not strictly Gen. Tat. as the case of the first word determines the name, but mere Tat.; some call these Nom. Tat. ( @deva[प्रथमतत्पुरुष] ).

**(a)**[^6] A noun in the Genitive, used as an object in a sentence, when the agent and object of the action denoted by the nouns formed by kṛit affixes are both used, is not compounded; as @deva[आश्चर्यो गवां दोह्नगोपेन] 'the milking of cows by one, not a cowherd, is a wonder.'
