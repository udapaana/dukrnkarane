The words @deva[वर] and @deva[श्रेयस्] when used in the sense of the superlative govern the Genitive or the Locative; @deva[पुत्रः सर्वस्पर्शानां वरः] 'a son is the best of things possessed of touch;' @deva[चतुष्पदां गौः श्रेयस् हेमकर्णानां वरम्] 'the cow is the best of quadrupeds and gold of metals'; the neu. sing. of @deva[वर] is used (with a word expressive of negation) in the sense of 'better and not, or but not;' @deva[अकृत्वान्यमनुष्ठेयं वरम्] doing any thing slowly is better than not doing it at all; @deva[अजातस्यमृतस्योर्णं वरं मूढो न चारित्रतः] of (the three kinds of) sons—not born, born and dead, and foolish, the first two are better, but not the last. @deva[याच्ञायां सोत्सवं वरं मरणं नायाचे द्विषत्कृत्यम्।] Meg. I. 8. @deva[वरं मृत्युः विदित्याभिः न तु मानभवनिः] better death then disgrace.
