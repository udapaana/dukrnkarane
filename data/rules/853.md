## Genitive with Verbs Meaning 'to Kill, Punish'

The roots @deva[वध्], @deva[हन्] with @deva[नि] or @deva[वि] or with both, @deva[विध्], @deva[विच्छ्] and @deva[विवु] all mean 'to kill'; @deva[विध्] with @deva[वि] or @deva[निच्छ्] with both punish, &c; @deva[चोरस्य दण्डयति राजा] a king punishes a thief; @deva[निजबलैर्जगत्रिपूनां] (Siṣ. I. 27.) to kill the enemies of the world (the demons) by his own power; @deva[अन्तर्मन्युस्य निहन्तुः] kill (drive away) your anger; @deva[राक्षसस्य निहन्ति] or @deva[विहन्ति] or @deva[वधति] or @deva[विहनिवधति रामः] Rāma will kill the Rākṣasas; @deva[वृषलस्य व्यायति व्यायति] does injury to a Vṛṣala; @deva[साहासिकस्य निष्टं गन्धः] &c.; in other senses they govern the Accusative; @deva[धान्यं निघृष्टे] he grinds fried rice.[^1]
