## Genitive with Past Passive Participles in Present Sense

When past passive participles are used in the sense of the present tense the Genitive is used; @deva[राज्ञः मतो भक्तितः पूजितो वा] respected, known or honored by the king; @deva[यद्वाङो वा सतां मतः रामस्य संमतम्] Bhaṭṭi. VIII. 124.[^1]

(a) Past participles showing the place of an action, as well as those used as abstract nouns are used with the Genitive @deva[अरण्यगतिवक्तदनानं यात्नै रामणेः । अङ्कतिभूतिसैर्यगतानाम्यो विहङ्गः ॥ रस्य युद्धि, कोपिकास्य स्पाहो, नट्यस्य युद्धनम्, छात्रस्य हविनं], &c. (Mb.) See. Bhaṭṭi. VIII. 125.[^2]
