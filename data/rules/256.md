At the end of a Bah. compound—

**(a)** @deva[जानु] is changed to @deva[ञु] necessarily when preceded by @deva[प्र] or @deva[सम्] and optionally when preceded by @deva[उच्चै];[^1] @deva[प्रजत्ते जानुनी यस्य स प्रञ्नुः] 'one having the knees wide apart' i. e. 'bandylegged;' @deva[सञ्नुः] 'one having well-turned or beautiful knees,' @deva[उर्चैर्जानुः—ञुः] 'long-shanked.'

**(b)** @deva[जाया] becomes @deva[जानि];[^2] @deva[युवती जाया यस्य स युवजानिः] 'one whose wife is young;' @deva[भूजानिः] lit. 'one whose wife is the earth, i. e. a king,' &c.

**(c)** @deva[गन्ध] becomes @deva[गन्धि] when preceded by @deva[उत्], @deva[प्रति] or @deva[दुः];[^3] @deva[उद्दतः गन्धः यस्य स उद्गन्धिः] 'whose smell is spread above,' @deva[प्रतिगन्धिः] 'having a repulsive smell,' @deva[दुर्गन्धिः]. This change of @deva[गन्ध] is possible only when the smell referred to is inseparably connected with ( looks like a part and parcel of ) the object denoted by the other member; as @deva[दुर्गन्धि पुष्करं सन्निहं च] ( which receives its odour from the lotuses &c. growing in it; ) @deva[दुर्गन्धिवारिहः]; but @deva[शोभना गन्धा अस्य इति गन्ध आपणिकः] 'a perfumer dealing in sweet smells.' @deva[गन्ध] is also changed to @deva[गन्धि][^4] when it means 'a particle of,' 'a little of,' or when the compound involves comparison; @deva[सुरस्य गन्धः परिमन्द तत् सृणगन्धि भोजनम्]; so @deva[सृतगन्धि] a dinner in which there is a scanty supply of ghee, &c.; @deva[पद्मस्य इव गन्धः यस्य तत् पद्मगन्धि] 'that which has the smell of a lotus.'

**(d)** @deva[नासिका] becomes @deva[नस] when it is preceded by a preposition or when the compound is used as a distinguishing name and it is preceded by any word except @deva[स्थूल];[^5] @deva[उन्नता नासिका यस्य स उन्नसः] 'one having a prominent nose,' @deva[प्रणसः] 'one having a good nose,' @deva[द्रुरिव नासिका यस्य द्रुणसः][^6] 'one whose nose is like a tree,' 'large-nosed;' but @deva[स्थूलनासिकः]. @deva[नस] becomes @deva[नष्] optionally when preceded by @deva[खुर] or @deva[खर]; as @deva[खुरणसः] or @deva[खुरणषः] 'having a nose like a horse's hoof i. e. flat;' @deva[खरणसः-णषः] 'sharp-nosed.' @deva[नासिका] when preceded by @deva[वि] becomes @deva[ञ] or @deva[ष्ण]; as @deva[विगता नासिका यस्य स विञः] or @deva[विष्णुः] 'one having a deformed nose.'

[^1]: @deva[प्रसंभ्यां जानुनोर्ञुः] । @deva[उच्चैर्दिभाषा] Pāṇ. V. 4. 129, 130.

[^2]: @deva[जायाया निङ्] Pāṇ. V. 4. 134. @deva[स्त्रियो भोटर्जिति] Pāṇ. VI. 1. 66. @deva[जाया] at the end of a Bah. substitutes @deva[नि] for its final @deva[आ]. The preceding @deva[न्] or @deva[त्] is dropped before a consonant except @deva[य्].

[^3]: @deva[गन्धस्येदुतिदुर्भ्यांश्चः] Pāṇ. V. 4. 135. @deva[गन्धस्येवेति तत्कालस्यग्रहणम्] Vārt. There is a difference of opinion among critics as to the meaning of the Vārt.. @deva[गन्धस्य] &c. Some hold that the smell must be the natural property of a thing to necessitate the final @deva[च]. Cf. Jayamaṅgala on Bhaṭṭi. @deva[आत्मविद्यान्यन्यवद्धः दुर्गन्धः] &c. II. 10, and Mallinatha on Rag. IV. 46. But the view of such eminent grammarians as Kaiyaṭa, Bhaṭṭoji, Nagesha, &c. is as given above.

[^6]: @deva[वृक्षपलाशंज्ञायामन्यः] । @deva[उत्सर्गादिहुलम्] Pāṇ. VIII. 4. 3. 28.
