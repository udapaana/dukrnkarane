When several words of the same form ( or dissimilar in form but of the same import[^1] ) are compounded together, only one of them is retained with the necessary number-

e. g. @deva[रामश्च रामश्च रामौ], @deva[रामश्च रामश्च रामश्च रामाः] This is called @deva[एकशेषद्वन्द्वः]. In the case of the mas. and fem. genders the one of the mas. gender is retained.[^2] as @deva[हरी च हरयश्च हरीः]; similarly @deva[शाखौघी], @deva[खुरौ], @deva[अजौ], &c.

**(a)** This principle is further extended to some words dissimilar in form, as in the following examples:—@deva[भ्राता च स्वसा च भ्रातरौ]; @deva[पुत्रश्च दुहिता च पुत्रौ]; @deva[माता च पिता च पितरौ] (see also @ref[193]), @deva[श्वशुरश्च श्वश्रूश्च श्वशुरौ][^3] (also @deva[श्वश्रूश्वशुरौ]); @deva[सः च सा च तौ], @deva[सः च देवदत्तश्च तौ], @deva[सः च यश्च तौ] or @deva[तौ][^4]. In the case of words of the mas. fem and neu. gender that of the neu. gender is retained; as @deva[तत्र देवदत्तश्च ते]; @deva[तत्र देवदत्तश्च यज्ञदत्ता च तानि]
