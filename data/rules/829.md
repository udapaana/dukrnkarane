## Dative with नमः, स्वाहा, स्वधा, अलं, वषट्

The particles @deva[नमः], @deva[स्वाहा], @deva[स्वधा], and @deva[स्वस्ति] (exclamations used in offering oblations to gods and manes respectively), @deva[अलं] meaning 'equal to, a match for' and @deva[वषट्] (an exclamation accompanying an oblation to a deity) govern the Dative case; @deva[तस्मै नमः संभवे] 'bow to that Śambhu;' @deva[प्रजाभ्यः स्वाहा] 'may it be well with the subjects,' @deva[स्वस्ति] (Rag. V. 17.) 'a farewell to thee;' @deva[अग्नये स्वाहा] 'this offering to Agni,' similarly @deva[पितृभ्यः स्वधा]; @deva[देवेभ्यो हरिं अलं] 'Hari is a match for the demons;' so @deva[अलं मल्लाय मल्लः] (Mah. Bhā.) 'one wrestler is a match for another; (see Bhaṭ. II. 39, Bhaṭṭi. VIII. 98.) @deva[इन्द्राय वषट्] 'this oblation to Indra.'

(a) But when @deva[कृ] is used with @deva[नमः], it becomes the principal verb and therefore governs the Accusative case; @deva[नमस्करोति देवान्] 'bows down to gods' (when the sense of an infinitive is suppressed, of course the Dative will be used; see @ref[828]).

(b) Words having the sense of @deva[अर्थं], such as @deva[समर्थं], @deva[शक्त], &c. and also the verb @deva[अर्ह], are used with the Dative (Sid. Kau.) @deva[देवेभ्यो हरिं समर्थः शक्तो वा]; The Dative is sometimes used instead of the Genitive; @deva[विश्वामित्राय योगम्] (Kum. II. 3.) 'having devotion to the feet of that ascetic.'[^1]

---

[^1]: Pāṇ. II. 3. 16 and related Vārtikas
