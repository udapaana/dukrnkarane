## Comprehensive Catalogue of Samavṛttas

This section presents a comprehensive catalogue of Samavṛtta metres organized by the number of syllables per pāda (quarter). Each metre is given with its definition, gaṇa scheme, and illustrative examples from Sanskrit literature.

---

## Metres with 8 Syllables per Pāda

### (1) @deva[अनुष्टुभ्] or @deva[श्लोक]

This is the commonest of all Sanskrit metres; it forms the chief metre of the great epics and many of the Purāṇas.[^1]

There are many varieties of this metre; but that in which there are eight syllables in a Pāda the fifth being short and no deviation from these rules occur in the Mahābhārata and Rāmāyaṇa.

### (2) @deva[गजगति] (4+4)

**Definition:** @deva[नभलगा गजगति]

**Gaṇa Scheme:** न, भ, ल, ग = ∪∪∪ | —∪∪ | ∪

### (3) @deva[प्रमाणिका] (4+4)

**Definition:** @deva[प्रमाणिका जरो लगो]

**Gaṇa Scheme:** ज, र, ल, ग = ∪—∪ | —∪— | ∪

### (4) @deva[माणवक] (4+4)

**Definition:** @deva[भातलगा माणवकम्]

**Gaṇa Scheme:** भ, त, ल, ग = —∪∪ | ——∪ | ∪

### (5) @deva[विद्युन्माला] (4+4)

**Definition:** @deva[मो मो गो गो विद्युन्माला]

**Gaṇa Scheme:** म, म, ग, ग = ——— | ——— | —

### (6) @deva[समानिका] (4+4)

**Definition:** @deva[रलो रजो समानिका हु]

### (3) @deva[उपजाति]

**Definition:** A mixture of Indravajrā and Upendravajrā gives a metre called Upajāti. It is said to have fourteen varieties.

**Gaṇa Scheme:** Mixed pattern of इन्द्रवज्रा and उपेन्द्रवज्रा

When other two metres are mixed in one stanza, they are still called Upajāti.

**Gaṇa Scheme:** म, र, भ, न, म, न, ग

## @deva[दण्डक] (Daṇḍaka)

Metres with 27 syllables or more in each pāda are denoted by the general name Daṇḍaka. Many varieties of this are mentioned (as the number of syllables in each pāda may sometimes reach 999), such as @deva[चण्डवृत्तप्रयात], @deva[प्रचितक], @deva[मत्तकोकिल], @deva[कर], @deva[सिंहविक्रान्त], @deva[कहुसमस्तनक], @deva[अनभङ्गोद्वर], @deva[सङ्ग्राम] &c.

Mālavikāgnimitra I.36 is an instance of the species last named, having 54 syllables in each pāda.

## SECTION III

## @deva[विषमवृत्तानि] (Viṣamavṛttāni)

Unequal Metres

The most common metre of this class is called @deva[उद्गता].

### @deva[उद्गता]

**Definition:** @deva[प्रथमे सजौ यदि सल्युै च तसजयुर्कान्युनन्तरम् । यदधं भनजल्युगा स्युखौ सजसा जगौ च भवतीयमुद्गुता]

**Gaṇa Scheme:**
- First quarter: स, ज, स, ल
- Second quarter: न, स, ज, ग
- Third quarter: भ, न, ज, ल, ग
- Fourth quarter: स, ज, स, ज, ग

Another variety of Udgītā is mentioned where the first quarter has भ, न, भ, ग instead of भ, न, ज, ल, ग.

Other kinds of metres in which every quarter differs in the number of syllables, are included under the same name '@deva[गाथा]' (Gāthā). The same name is applicable to a strophe consisting of any number of quarters other than four.
